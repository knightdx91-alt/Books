## The Long Goodnight

The glow of the solitary streetlight was the only illumination of the otherwise dark street. Walking slowly, the cool October air forcing him to pull his jacket around him, Troy looks around. The normally busy road was deathly quiet at one thirty in the morning. With a sigh Troy keeps walking North, hoping that a car will come along so he can hitch a ride. After drifting between towns these last few years, you'd think he would be used to the loneliness of the night. yet, on nights like this, when it seems the world is standing still, thoughts of the life he left behind intrude on his mind. With a loving wife he adored and a job he actually enjoyed, he thought his life was perfect. until the night that changed everything.

"You know I hate going to these things." Troy grumbled, flipping on his blinker and turning on to ocean drive. "I've got a ton of paperwork to do plus I told dad I would give him a call." Selena reached across the center console of their '03 Honda Civic and patted him on the arm with a smile. "I know dear, but I also know they come to see both of us, not just me. Besides, we don't have these meetings often and you didn't come to the last one." Troy shrugged but had to admit she had a point. Doesn't mean he had to like it though. "Have they asked any of the new entrepreneurs in the area if they want to join yet?" Troy asks, interested despite himself. The group was really to showcase new business owners in the area and to help them network, but sometimes they got him or Selena to talk about their experiences operating a business, and things to look out for. Such as, common mistakes new business owners might make.

"They've added a few" selena says, laying her head back and closing her eyes, "I'm not sure who they are though. Oh, Grace called me yesterday. Apparently the city council is wanting to vote on a city tax increase." "Bloody vultures" Troy mumbles. Selena giggled, "I agree but I'm not sure we've got enough support if we try to block it." Troy nods his head and stops at a red light. Turning to Selena, he takes her hand. "Look, I know I complain about these meetings and about a million other things. I just want you to know that I love you and I appreciate everything you do." pulling her hand to his lips, he gently kisses her knuckles. Smiling, she gently cups his face with her other hand, "I love you too." she says softly, "still not going to get you out of talking to the group though." she teases. "Drat" he replies with a smirk as he pulls out into the intersection once the light turned green. Suddenly high beams from their right light up the interior of the car then the world went black.

"Mr. Steadman are you with us?" The young police officer, Mark he had said his name was, asks gently. Troy turns from looking out the window and stares at mark. Mark starts fidgeting under Troy's gaze, but continues. "Sir, we just wanted to come by and tell you that if you need anything, anything at all, to please give us a call. We know that you and Mrs. Steadman have done alot for the community and we won't forget it. It's a terrible tragedy what happened, and you have our sincere condolences. Just know that we are here to help in any way that we can." Troy nods his head, "Thank you, but I'd like to rest now please," he says quietly. The officers hurry to the bed and shake his hand before retreating to the hallway. The realization that he will never

see her face or be able to touch her again, breaks him. Tears that he had been holding in start streaming down his face. Curling up on the bed, he holds her face in his mind and drifts off into a troubled sleep.

Headlights cresting the hill behind him pull Troy from his memories. Sticking out his thumb, Troy steps off the road and waits to see if the car will slow down. Seeing the car slow down as it gets closer, his heart starts to beat faster as he begins to hope that he won't have to walk all the way to Jamestown. Readjusting his duffel bag, he leans down and looks into the open car window. A young looking brunette smiles at him, the dash lights reflecting off her clear blue eyes. "Need a ride?" she asks. Troy nods his head, "Do you mind if I put this in the back?" "Sure" she replies, hitting the button to unlock all the doors. After setting his bag in the back, Troy settles himself in the front seat. "Thanks for picking me up." Troy says politely after a few minutes, "It would have been a long walk to Jamestown. My names Troy by the way." "Mine's Anna," she says, "how long have you been walking?" she asks as she glances at him out of the corner of her eye. Troy shrugs, "A few days. I've got a job lined up in Jamestown, so I'm just trying to make it for that. I actually don't have to be there until monday morning, so I'm making good time." "Where's the job at?" she asks as she changes lanes. Troy starts rubbing his hands together in front of the vent, trying to warm up his hands. Seeing this, Anna reaches over and turns up the heat slightly. "George Holmes runs a general store up there." he began. When Anna heard the name she smiled. "Do you know him?" Troy asked, curious. "In a way." she says mysteriously. Seeing that she

wasn't going to elaborate he sighs. "Well," Troy goes on, "I saw his ad in a newspaper I got from a trucker saying he needed help, so I gave him a call and he told me the job was mine if I wanted it." Anna nods, "That makes sense. He's a good man, always willing to help people in need. It's not always worked out for him, but he never stops." Seeing a diner coming up, she gets in the right lane. "Are you hungry?" she asks. At the mention of food, Troy's stomach makes itself known, rumbling in answer. Anna laughs, "well there's my answer." "I don't have any money on me" Troy says quickly, "I'll be fine really." Anna pulls into the parking lot, then turns to him with a raised eyebrow. "You're hungry and so am I, so let's eat. You can pay me back later if it's such a big deal." With that she turns the car off and gets out. Confused, Troy gets out and follows her. "Pay you back? How am I supposed to do that?" he asks while holding the diner door open for her. With a smile she just walks past him and settles into a booth. Shaking his head, he follows her.

The diner was old but in good repair with white and red booths, and stained glass lights overhead.
