## The Long Goodnight

The glow of the solitary streetlight was the only illumination of the otherwise dark street. Walking slowly, the cool October air forcing him to pull his jacket around him, Troy looks around. The normally busy road was deathly quiet at one thirty in the morning. With a sigh Troy keeps walking North, hoping that a car will come along so he can hitch a ride. After drifting between towns these last few years, you'd think he would be used to the loneliness of the night. yet, on nights like this, when it seems the world is standing still, thoughts of the life he left behind intrude on his mind. With a loving wife he adored and a job he actually enjoyed, he thought his life was perfect. until the night that changed everything.

"You know I hate going to these things." Troy grumbled, flipping on his blinker and turning on to ocean drive. "I've got a ton of paperwork to do plus I told dad I would give him a call." Selena reached across the center console of their '03 Honda Civic and patted him on the arm with a smile. "I know dear, but I also know they come to see both of us, not just me. Besides, we don't have these meetings often and you didn't come to the last one." Troy shrugged but had to admit she had a point. Doesn't mean he had to like it though. "Have they asked any of the new entrepreneurs in the area if they want to join yet?" Troy asks, interested despite himself. The group was really to showcase new business owners in the area and to help them network, but sometimes they got him or Selena to talk about their experiences operating a business, and things to look out for. Such as, common mistakes new business owners might make.

Apparently the city council is wanting to vote on a city tax increase." "Bloody vultures" Troy mumbles. Selena giggled, "I agree but I'm not sure we've got enough support if we try to block it." Troy nods his head and stops at a red light. Turning to Selena, he takes her hand. "Look, I know I complain about these meetings and about a million other things. I just want you to know that I love you and I appreciate everything you do." pulling her hand to his lips, he gently kisses her knuckles. Smiling, she gently cups his face with her other hand, "I love you too." she says softly, "still not going to get you out of talking to the group though." she teases. "Drat" he replies with a smirk as he pulls out into the intersection once the light turned green. Suddenly high beams from their right light up the interior of the car then the world went black.

"Mr. Steadman are you with us?" The young police officer, Mark he had said his name was, asks gently. Troy turns from looking out the window and stares at mark. Just know that we are here to help in any way that we can." Troy nods his head, "Thank you, but I'd like to rest now please," he says quietly.
