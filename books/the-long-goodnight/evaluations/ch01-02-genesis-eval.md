**The Hostile:** Two challenges. (1) *Credibility:* a woman alone picks up a male stranger on an empty road — the text pre-empts this well ("she'd already thought it and picked him up anyway"), so it holds. (2) *Coincidence-engineering:* Anna picking up exactly the drifter and steering him to her dad's store "on a hunch" is a big lever — the text leans on charm to sell it. It works for now but the whole plot rests on this hunch; if later chapters don't deepen *why* she does this, a hostile reader files it as contrivance.

**The Casual Reader:** Would buy after 3 pages. Likes Xavier without being able to say why; likes Anna immediately. The reveal in Ch2 is exactly the "oh that's clever" beat that makes someone text a friend. Verdict: **8.5** — stays.

**The Devoted Reader (active — literary/suspense):** Would clock the "in a way" as a plant on first read and grin on reread. Primed to hunt the fair-play architecture the outline promises. Would adopt if the necklace clue lands as cleanly as this reveal did.

## PATH TO 8.5 (Floor is 8.0 on both — mandatory)

**Chapter 1 — Pacing 8.0 → 8.5 requires:** the final page must load a *sharper* question than it resolves. Right now it closes clean on "he knocked." Restore ~600–900 budgeted words of decelerated diner/road texture so the middle doesn't feel skated, AND end the chapter on a beat that makes the knock feel *chosen against his own instinct to keep drifting* — a half-second where he almost doesn't knock. The most achievable surgery: extend the diner scene (more of Anna reading the town, one more Marcus beat) and add two sentences of hesitation at the screen door before he knocks. That converts a soft landing into a pulled trigger.

**Chapter 2 — Pacing 8.0 → 8.5 (bottleneck shared by Originality/Theme):** the chapter peaks at the reveal (mid-chapter) then decompresses through the hire. Move more weight *after* the reveal — the back third should tighten, not soften. Surgery at the closing beat (Anna's car pulling up as he takes the apron): make her arrival land a beat of dread/anticipation in Xavier (he now knows who she is; she doesn't know he knows) instead of a warm button. That single change turns the ending hook from "warm" to "charged" and lifts propulsion. Restore budgeted length in the morning sensory open (the store-as-future-fire-site can be quietly seeded here without spending Ch3's material).

**Both — Prose 8.5 → 9.0** is deferred by design: the register can't hit its ceiling until Voice-Under-Pressure is tested (the counting tell, the present-tense intrusions, the fire). Do not force it early. These openings are correctly holding those instruments in reserve.
