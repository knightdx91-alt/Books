# The Long Goodnight — Outline / Roadmap

> Working roadmap. Planning doc, not prose. Update as decisions change.

## Logline
Three years after a drunk driver killed his wife, a grief-numbed drifter takes a
job at a small-town general store and lets himself love the owner's daughter —
just as someone starts stalking her, and the whole town, including her, begins
to wonder if the trouble arrived with the stranger.

## Length
Target **85k+ words** — full-length novel. (~30–34 chapters at ~2,600–2,800
words, or fewer/longer chapters; settled at beat-sheet stage.)

## Genre & reader promise
**Small-town romantic suspense** with a **whodunit spine.** Slow-burn grief
romance braided with an escalating stalker mystery. Comp: Nora Roberts,
*Northern Lights* (outsider lands in a small community; romance and threat
braided; town-wide suspect pool). Tag/blurb it as romantic suspense so mystery
readers find it and romance readers aren't ambushed by the fire.

## POV — LOCKED (amended: concealed-antagonist interludes permitted)
Primary POV is strict **single third-limited, Xavier only.** No head-hopping
between named characters. The main narrative line stays entirely in Xavier — the
romance, the town, the store — and anything that happens to Anna within that line
reaches us only *through* him (she tells him; he finds the aftermath).

**The one sanctioned exception — the WATCHER interludes.** A small number (3–4
total across the book) of short, set-off interludes are dramatized directly from
the stalker's POV to build dread (the book's tertiary engagement type) the way
the Nora Roberts comps do. These are the ONLY non-Xavier POV in the book. They
are governed by hard **concealment / fair-play rules** — breaking any of these
breaks the whodunit:

1. **Never named, never self-described.** The watcher is never named, never
   assigned an identifying physical detail (job, uniform, age, gait, vehicle),
   and never names Anna's other men. He refers to himself as "he," to Anna as
   "she/her." All three suspects (Derek, Caleb, Xavier) are male, so "he" leaks
   nothing.
2. **Withhold, never lie.** An interlude may conceal identity but must never state
   a falsehood the reader would rely on. Every sentence must read as literally
   true in hindsight after the Ch27 reveal. Fair-play is preserved by *omission,
   not deception.*
3. **No knowledge only one suspect could have — except reread-plants.** The
   watcher's interior may reference facts true of the culprit **only** where those
   facts have already been fairly planted in Xavier's line (e.g. "the thing she
   threw back at me" — legible as breakup color, decodable to *the necklace →
   Derek* only once that history is planted, Ch7/Ch12). Never a fact that fingers
   a single suspect before its fair-play window.
4. **A third voice.** The watcher's register is a cold, flat, possessive interior
   deliberately UNLIKE Xavier's numb-grief voice AND unlike Derek's charming
   public voice and Caleb's inarticulate one — so the reader cannot voice-match it
   to any suspect. (See voice-dna.md — Watcher concealment card.)
5. **Placed only after the suspect pool exists** (first interlude no earlier than
   ~Ch9), so the reader's mind races *across* Derek/Caleb, never at an empty set.
   Typographically set off (centered ornament / italic block), unlabeled.

**Cost accepted (author decision):** because the interludes show the watcher as a
consciousness separate from Xavier (they occur when Xavier is elsewhere/asleep),
the *reader* stops seriously suspecting Xavier. Xavier-as-suspect therefore
survives only as **in-town social pressure** (the town side-eyes the outsider),
which still isolates the couple and raises romance stakes — it is no longer a
suspect the *reader* entertains. The whodunit the reader plays is now
**Derek vs. Caleb**, powered by the "Derek left town" pull (Ch20–23).

## Principals
- **Xavier Steadman** — protagonist. Widower, early 30s. Wife **Selena** died in a
  car crash three years ago (drunk driver ran the light). Since then he has drifted
  between towns doing odd jobs, shunning company. Numb, decent, out of practice
  with people. (Renamed from "Troy" — that name is used in another book.)
- **Anna Holmes** — love interest. Mid-20s. George's daughter; lives ~a mile
  outside Jamestown. Sharp, warm, reads people, low tolerance for men who see only
  a pretty face (the double-cheeseburger beat). Picks Xavier up hitchhiking on a
  hunch and drives him to her father's store — **without revealing she's George's
  daughter.**
- **George Holmes** — Anna's father; owns **Holmes General Store** in Jamestown;
  hires Xavier. Mentor/father-warmth. **Dies of a heart attack mid-book**; the
  store passes to Anna, who **keeps Xavier on** to help run it.
- **Derek Vance** — Anna's ex-boyfriend; the stalker. Opening menace line:
  *"we'll see when she breaks up with him."* (See mystery design below.)
- **Caleb Ruiz** — a local quietly sweet on Anna; resents the newcomer. Red-herring
  suspect.

## The Anna reveal — LOCKED (Act 1)
Anna drops Xavier at Holmes General Store with no reveal. **The next day**, over
lunch, George asks how Xavier got to Jamestown so fast; Xavier says a woman named
Anna gave him a ride; George laughs; Xavier asks what's funny; George:
*"well, looks like you've already met my daughter."* Xavier blindsided. Plays as a
surprise the reader shares (strict Xavier POV), and retro-loads Anna's earlier
coyness ("in a way," the mysterious smile) as fair-play foreshadowing.

## Theme & climax — the spine (protect this)
Selena's **life-insurance money** is Xavier's frozen grief. He won't touch it —
it feels like "blood money," and spending it means letting her go. The climax is
one action that resolves external plot and internal arc together: after the store
burns, Xavier finally **uses the money to rebuild it for Anna.** That gesture is
him choosing the living and saying the long goodnight to Selena. The title lands
here: the years of grief were one long goodnight; the ending finally says it.

## The mystery — design
The reader must **figure out who** the stalker is. Rules that follow:
- **Misdirection — plant then pull the rug.** Keep the ex's menace line, but then
  the ex **appears to leave town / move on / clean up.** So the obvious suspect
  gets *ruled out* in the reader's mind ("can't be him, he's gone") while he's
  been circling all along.
- **Suspect pool (need real alternatives):**
  1. **Derek Vance (the ex)** — real culprit, hidden by being dismissed ("he left
     town"). The reader's whodunit is primarily **Derek vs. Caleb.**
  2. **Caleb Ruiz** — local quietly sweet on Anna; resents the newcomer. Carries
     the reader-facing red-herring load now that Xavier is off the reader's list.
  3. **Xavier himself** — with the Watcher interludes in play, Xavier is **no
     longer a suspect the reader entertains** (the interludes show a separate
     consciousness). He remains a suspect *in the town's eyes only* — outsider
     distrust, a flicker of doubt even from Anna at the low point — which isolates
     the couple and raises romance stakes without competing with the interludes.
- **The Watcher interludes carry the dread the confined POV can't.** They let us
  dramatize the stalking directly (watching the lit store, leaving the gift,
  the fixation curdling from "win her back" to "remove the rival") while the
  identity stays sealed. See the POV lock's concealment rules and the interlude
  slots in `chapter-outline.md`.
- **The catch (earned, not "somehow"):** Derek is careful all book, but the arson
  is *rage, not planning* — so this is where he slips and leaves the physical
  evidence that gets him **arrested by the law** (not killed by Xavier). The care
  breaking down under fury is *why* he's finally caught.
- **Fair-play clue — LOCKED:** the "gift" left for Anna is **the necklace Derek
  bought her, which she threw back at him when they broke up.** Because she
  returned it *to Derek*, only Derek could possess it and re-gift it — the object
  is physically singular, which makes the culprit provable in hindsight, not just
  guessable. Xavier registers the necklace as an odd, too-personal present but
  can't place it (he wasn't there for the breakup); the attentive reader connects
  it to Derek.
  - **Fair-play requirement:** the necklace's history must be **planted early** —
    the reader has to learn, before Ch 12, that Derek gave Anna a necklace and she
    threw it back at him. Seed it in Anna's breakup backstory (around Ch 7). Then
    the necklace's reappearance can only mean Derek, and "he's gone" becomes a lie
    the reader can catch.

## Escalation ladder = clue ladder
1. **Ambiguous, hides in the genre** — Anna feels watched; small gifts at the
   store; hang-up calls. Could be a secret admirer.
2. **Not sweet** — things moved inside the store; a note; a property/pet scare.
   Clue: whoever it is *knows her routine* → someone close, not random.
3. **Point of no return** — a real physical threat to Anna. Now we know he's
   dangerous; suspicion swings across the pool; Xavier gets side-eyed.
4. **Pivot to Xavier** — the fixation turns from winning Anna back to removing the
   rival.
5. **The fire** — climax. Xavier asleep upstairs (the store is also his home).
   He escapes; the arsonist slips and is caught; then the money → rebuild.

## Act structure (private scaffolding — never shown, freely broken)
- **Act 1** — The night ride (Anna picks up Xavier, diner, no reveal) → drop-off
  at Holmes General Store → **the lunch reveal** (George: "you've already met my
  daughter"); Xavier hired; settles into Jamestown; slow warm with Anna; George as
  father-warmth; Derek introduced with the menace line.
- **Act 2** — Romance deepens *against* Xavier's guilt over Selena; the stalking
  begins and escalates (ladder rungs 1–3); **George's heart attack** → store
  becomes Anna's, she keeps Xavier on, shared grief bonds them, his guilt-wall
  peaks (a false ending where he nearly drifts away). The ex "leaves town."
- **Act 3** — Fixation pivots to Xavier (rung 4); **the fire** (rung 5); escape;
  arrest via the evidence; then Xavier spends the money to rebuild Anna's store —
  the long goodnight.

## Tone guard
Act 3 stacks catastrophe (dead father, burned store, near-death). Keep Act 2's
middle genuinely warm — falling in love, cozy small-town texture, the store as a
home — so the darkness lands instead of whiplashing.

## Cross-book differentiation (so the four books don't rhyme)
- **Shape:** this one is a slow-burn romance with a late thriller spike + whodunit
  spine — deliberately different from the other three's shapes.
- **Midpoint engine:** a natural-causes death (heart attack) that transfers the
  store — not the same turn used elsewhere.
- **Voice:** Xavier's close, numb, past-tense grief-register must not sound like
  the other three protagonists (lock this in the voice-DNA cards).
- **Name:** Xavier, not Troy (reused elsewhere).
