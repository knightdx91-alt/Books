# Character Bible — The Long Goodnight

> **Standing rule:** every time a NAMED character is created — however minor —
> add them here with a **sound signature** (how they talk / how their presence
> reads) and a **distinct-from** note. No two characters may sound the same.
> Cross-check new entries against the "cover-the-name test": if you could swap a
> line between two characters and not notice, one of them isn't finished.
>
> Full voice cards for the principals live in `voice-dna.md`; this Bible is the
> master roster + the fast at-a-glance sound check as the cast grows.

Legend: **PRINCIPAL** (full card in voice-dna.md) · **SUPPORTING** · **WALK-ON**
(one scene; still gets a signature so it never drifts into a principal's voice).

---

## Xavier Steadman — PRINCIPAL (protagonist)
- **Sound:** Short declaratives. Blue-collar-precise nouns (tools, weather,
  hinges). Answers with fewer words than asked; trails off near grief; repeats
  your last word back as a stall. Never names his feelings or Selena.
- **Tell:** counts things (steps, seconds, dollars) when barely holding on.
- **Distinct from:** Caleb — both inarticulate, but Xavier *withholds*, Caleb
  *overspills*.

## Anna Holmes — PRINCIPAL (love interest)
- **Sound:** Quick complete sentences with a turn at the end — bland setup,
  deadpan payload (the double-cheeseburger method). Questions like scalpels.
- **Tell:** "In a way" (coy deflection; hides being George's daughter in Act 1).
  Goes quiet when a joke won't hold — her silence is the alarm.
- **Distinct from:** George — both bury meaning in surface talk, but Anna's turn
  *cuts*, George's *hugs*.

## George Holmes — PRINCIPAL (Anna's father, store owner)
- **Sound:** Old-store folksy; long circling stories that bury the kind thing at
  the end and underplay it. Opens with "Well—". Calls Xavier "son" once.
- **Tell:** never says the warm thing directly — smuggles it. Dies mid-ordinary-
  sentence (heart attack); no dramatic last words.
- **Distinct from:** Anna — same fold-it-in-the-surface habit, opposite payload
  (warmth vs blade).

## Derek Vance — PRINCIPAL (antagonist / the stalker)
- **Sound:** Charming-flat in public — smooth, reasonable, likeable. Reasonable
  sentences with an ownership word hidden inside ("my Anna," "she'll come
  around"). Calm statements that are actually threats; never raises his voice
  until the end.
- **Tell:** possessive about Anna; the menace line said like a joke; re-gifts **the
  necklace Anna threw back at him** (the fair-play clue, Ch 12) as if it's
  tenderness — only he could have it. Control shatters at the arson — that's why he
  slips.
- **Distinct from:** Caleb — Derek's resentment is *concealed and smooth*;
  Caleb's is *open and clumsy*. This contrast IS the misdirection; keep it audible.

## Caleb Ruiz — SUPPORTING (local sweet on Anna; red-herring suspect)
- **Sound:** Earnest, stiff, means well and says it badly. Starts sentences he
  doesn't finish around Anna; blunt/grudging toward Xavier. Over-explains when
  nervous. A bad liar — transparency is what clears him.
- **Tell:** "I'm just saying." Mutters; won't meet Xavier's eye.
- **Distinct from:** Derek — transparency vs concealment (see above).

## Selena Steadman — SUPPORTING (Xavier's late wife; appears in memory only)
- **Sound:** Warm, teasing, grounded — the marriage's easy back-and-forth ("still
  not going to get you out of talking to the group"). Affection with a light
  ribbing edge. Rendered only in flashback/memory; her *absence* is her main
  presence in the present-day narration.
- **Distinct from:** Anna — Selena is settled/married-warm; Anna is guarded/
  sharp-warm. The new love must not read as a Selena replacement.

## Marcus — WALK-ON (young diner server, Ch 1; Rosalie out sick)
- **Sound:** Eager-to-please, smitten-hopeful; can't finish the word after Anna's
  name; takes her deadpan order like scripture and deflates under her flat wit.
  Turns toward Anna "like a plant to a window." Speaks in fragments when she's near.
- **Distinct from:** Caleb — both smitten and inarticulate around Anna, but Marcus
  is *hopeful/harmless* (a boy at a window) while Caleb is *resentful/grudging*.
  Distinct from Xavier: Marcus overspills eagerness where Xavier withholds.

## Officer Mark — WALK-ON (delivers condolences at the hospital, Act 1 flashback)
- **Sound:** Young, nervous, over-formal official kindness; fidgets under
  silence; recites duty-phrases ("our sincere condolences," "here to help").
- **Distinct from:** George — both kind, but Mark is stiff-official, George is
  unhurried-folksy.

## Grace — WALK-ON (Selena's friend; phone call, mentioned)
- **Sound:** (Minimal so far — town-gossip conduit; brings news like the council
  tax vote.) Lock a signature the first time she appears on-page: brisk, plugged-
  in, community-minded. Flag if she risks blurring with Anna.

## Rosalie — WALK-ON (diner cook, called in sick; mentioned only)
- **Sound:** Unseen so far. If she ever appears on-page, give her a signature then
  and add it here — do not let her default to generic-diner-worker.
