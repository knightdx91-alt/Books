## Chapter 1 (draft)

*Begin initialization...*

Josh pushed the button mentally as he sat on his narrow bed in his prison cell. Joshua
Matthews was part of a pilot program that the federal prisons were conducting with the neural
link, a brain to computer interface. The difference between the ones currently on the market
and the one Josh had in his head were the beta features he was currently testing. Apparently
some really smart folks had figured out how to do a mind VR that resembled the old sword art
online games, which meant he could play games in his head. Josh and four others were chosen to
test this short term immersion because they all had extensive computer skills, so they could
give helpful feedback. The immersion had a limit of eight hours, as the scientists did not know
how long term immersion would affect the body. The neural link was not just about playing games
though. It was honestly like being inside of a real computer. The only thing he could not do
was surf the net without the prison unlocking the gateway. Until Josh could find out a way past
that anyway. He only had three years so he had to make every day count.

*Please create your avatar...*

Josh browsed through the options before deciding to just go with his normal look. He was 6'1"
around 210 pounds with short hair and grey eyes. Thankfully the clothes were normal, so he chose
brown chinos and a grey t-shirt and converse sneakers.

*Analyzing sound preferences...*

"Hello Josh and welcome to the Rehabilitation Module." Scarlett Johansson's voice says, "I will
explain some of the functions you will have access to, then let you explore on your own. If at
any time you need me just call out Reiki. Now, part of your contract status you must participate
in some kind of rehabilitation program before your term ends. Besides that you are free to
utilize any of our other exciting features."

Josh, who had been watching the development news for the neural link closely before he was
locked up, already knew what he wanted to try. "I'd like to try your ai game module." Josh says.
Josh hears a sweet chuckle in his mind, "Of course you would know about that. Would you like an
explanation of the game before you start?" Josh thought for a minute before telling her No. Josh
had always loved video games. What had piqued Josh's interest was neural link's boast that their
ai could create you a custom game based on your video game likes. Their ai analyzes all of your
games you've played, how you liked them and things you didn't like. It was supposed to be able to
create your perfect game. Josh had told Reiki no so that he could go into the game blind with no
information. Something he did with every new game he bought.

## What the opening establishes (for the architect)

- **Josh / Joshua Matthews** — POV. Incarcerated (~3 years left), extensive computer/hacking
  skills, lifelong gamer who likes going into games blind. Motivated to find a way past the
  prison's locked network "gateway."
- **The tech** — an experimental **neural link** (brain-computer interface) with beta features;
  full "mind VR" (SAO-style). Feels "like being inside of a real computer," not just a game.
  8-hour immersion limit (safety unknown). 5 inmate testers with computer skills.
- **Reiki** — the module's AI assistant (Scarlett Johansson-esque voice); runs the
  "Rehabilitation Module"; can build a **custom AI game tailored to the player's tastes**
  (Josh declined the tailoring, wants it blind).
- **Hook** — Josh enters an AI-generated LitRPG world; the neural link doubling as "being inside
  a real computer" plus the locked gateway signals a hacking/escape thread beyond the game.
