# Book Three — Hook & Pull Audit (openings + endings)
Date: 2026-07-11 · REPORT ONLY (no prose modified; author sign-off required)
Scope: chapter-1 … chapter-20, first + last lines rated for page-turn force.

Register note: this is quiet literary fantasy (comps N.K. Jemisin / Samantha
Shannon). Threshold applied is the literary bar (hook ≥6, pull ≥6), NOT the
thriller bar. A quiet voice hook that earns its lean-in counts as strong. The
book's problem is NOT weak pulls — the closes are consistently strong. The two
real weaknesses are (a) a handful of low-energy / recap OPENINGS and (b) a
variety problem: three chapters open on the same "There is a thing…" essay
template, and the early-mid CLOSES rhyme (the door-refrain already flagged in
STATE.yaml).

Legend: S = strong · A = adequate · W = weak

---

## Per-chapter

### Ch.1 — What the World Became
- OPEN (S): "The almond tree at the bottom of the garden had flowered twice
  since they came to the valley, and both times Viridia had felt it before she
  saw it." Sensory + gap (felt before saw). Excellent series-resume hook.
- CLOSE (S): "…something that was not attacking and did not need to be leaned in
  toward the warmth she had become." Ominous promise. Lands the book's engine.

### Ch.2 — The First Door That Should Not Be
- OPEN (S): "She did not sleep that night, and in the morning she told them she
  was going north." Decision + momentum from Ch.1. Clean.
- CLOSE (S): "There would be more doors. And she would be the one who had to
  close every one." Ominous promise. Strong — but see door-refrain flag below.

### Ch.3 — A City With No Head
- OPEN (A): "The broadsheets came up the slow line a week stale, and they came
  in two kinds now, and Viridia had learned to tell them apart before she had
  read a word." Voice hook; the "before she had read a word" gives a small gap,
  but it opens on newspapers and settles into essay. Functional, not gripping.
- CLOSE (S): "She felt how easy it would be." Temptation/decision. Superb — the
  short line after the long paragraph is the book's best pull mechanism.

### Ch.4 — The Easy Mark
- OPEN (S): "The second one came while they were still a half-day short of home,
  and it announced itself the way the first had not, which was loudly…" In
  medias res. Strong contrast-of-registers hook.
- CLOSE (S): "Something she did not have yet, and did not want, and could already
  feel waiting for her at the far end of the arithmetic…" Ominous promise. Strong
  — but rhymes with Ch.2's close (see flag).

### Ch.5 — What She Will Not Do
- OPEN (W): "Vale and his colleagues went down the slow line in the grey of the
  second morning, on foot from the mill to the dead track, and Viridia walked
  them as far as the first bend because it was a thing you did…" Low-energy
  travelogue; no gap, no tension, a goodbye-scene we already know the shape of.
  Weakest opening tied with Ch.6.
  - Sharper (from existing material, pull the interior forward): open on the
    line already IN the chapter — "He is right that I could. That is the part
    he does not know he won that he won." A voice/temptation hook that starts on
    the stakes instead of the walk to the bend.
- CLOSE (S): "It was going to bed. It had a long day ahead." Clever ominous
  personification of the void. Excellent.

### Ch.6 — The Cost of Half-Sight
- OPEN (W): "She had said the trail ran west, and she had said the pull ran to
  the source, and she had said the source was her, all of it at the long table…"
  This is a RECAP of Ch.5's close — the banned "after the events of…" pattern in
  voice-drag form. The reader just read this. Weakest opening in the book.
  - Sharper: cut straight to the chapter's own present-tense threat. The line
    "It opened at the mill." (currently buried after the section break) is a
    superb in-medias / sensory hook — promote it, or open on the ordinary-Tuesday
    beat that immediately precedes the death so the loss lands cold.
- CLOSE (S): grief pull — "…one more name than she had woken with." Emotional
  gut-punch, earned by Sela's death. Strong.

### Ch.7 — The Rim, Read Whole
- OPEN (A): "There is a thing she had been doing for a year without naming it,
  which was keeping the count small enough to carry." Voice/essay hook with a
  real gap. Works — BUT this "There is a thing…" template also opens Ch.13 and
  Ch.15 (variety flag).
- CLOSE (S): "…into the second act of a thing she had set running with the one
  full unguarded hand…" Ominous promise. Strong, though overlong — the pull is
  diluted across a very long final paragraph.

### Ch.8 — The Man Who Picked Up the Chalk
- OPEN (S): "The grey west was grey the way a thing is grey when no one has
  decided what to do with it." Voice hook; sets the let-go country instantly.
- CLOSE (S): "He's not the cause. He's just the first one who'll work out what I
  am." Revelation + ominous. Strong Act-Two-opening button.

### Ch.9 — Drake's Daughter, Again
- OPEN (A): "She went up the way she had gone up the first time, into the wind
  and the thin biting cold of a place the air had given up most of what made it
  kind…" Sensory; competent but a familiar climb-to-the-mountain beat.
- CLOSE (S): Drake's "…there is no closing it. There is only learning to hold
  it, and you had better start learning… you are the only wall there is."
  Revelation from the one being old enough to know. Strong.

### Ch.10 — Absence Drawn to Presence (MIDPOINT)
- OPEN (S): "She had thought, climbing down off Drake's mountain in the dark with
  the truth said out loud at last, that the saying of it was the worst of it, and
  she had been wrong…" Question hook (worse than what?). Strong — earns the gut-
  punch chapter.
- CLOSE (S): "…carrying the only thing she had ever truly been alone with… the
  knowing of exactly what her mercy cost." Emotional. Strong midpoint residue.

### Ch.11 — The Ledger Reopens
- OPEN (A/S): "She came back from the source-cavern and drank three cups of the
  bad coffee and did not sleep." Voice/sensory, concrete. Good aftermath hook.
- CLOSE (S): "She was still deciding what it meant that she would go anyway."
  Decision/question plant. Strong.

### Ch.12 — The Thing She Fought
- OPEN (S): "The town of Lest had a wall around it, and the wall was the wrong
  shape, and Viridia saw the wrongness from the train before she saw anything
  else." Question hook (why wrong?). Strong.
- CLOSE (S): "…how do you fix a pressure without controlling a single soul… I
  don't think anybody has." Question plant — the book's thematic spine, well
  placed. Strong.

### Ch.13 — Balance, Not Victory
- OPEN (A): "There is a difference between fixing a thing and balancing it…"
  Voice/essay; genuinely good on its own — BUT it is the SECOND "There is a
  thing/difference…" opening (after Ch.7) and Ch.15 makes it three. The template
  is now a tic at the chapter-OPENING level. Recommend varying at least one.
- CLOSE (S): "It had no bottom, and neither… did the cost." Ominous/emotional.
  Strong.

### Ch.14 — The Brink
- OPEN (S): "The bells started at Coldharbour before the sun was up, and by the
  time Viridia reached the high street the town was already running the wrong
  way." In medias res. Strong action opening.
- CLOSE (S): "She started walking before the others, because it was hers to
  start." Decision. Clean, strong, active-protagonist button.

### Ch.15 — What It Will Take
- OPEN (A): "There is a thing that happens to a company on the morning it sets
  out to do the last large thing… nobody packs as though it matters." THIRD
  "There is a thing…" opening. Individually strong; collectively a variety
  problem. This is the one to change (Ch.7 and Ch.13 do more distinctive work).
- CLOSE (S): "She stepped through." Clean cliffhanger button, Act-Two close.
  Strong.

### Ch.16 — The Approach
- OPEN (S): "The seam did not open onto the cavern… the country above it was the
  worst place she had ever stood, and she had stood in a burning school." Voice +
  callback threat. Strong Act-Three opener.
- CLOSE (S): "She set her hand to the source." Cliffhanger into the climax.
  Strong.

### Ch.17 — The Weight, Taken Up (CLIMAX)
- OPEN (S): "She set her hand to the source, and the source, which had no front
  and no manners and no notion of being ready, took it." Picks up mid-breath from
  Ch.16; voice inside action. Strong.
- CLOSE (S): "There would be time. That was the worst of it, and the truest.
  There would be all the time there was." Ominous/emotional. Strong.

### Ch.18 — The Cost
- OPEN (A): "It came to her the way weather comes to a country, not as a thing
  she chose to look at but as a thing that was simply happening now across the
  whole reach of her…" Weather-SIMILE opening — narrowly avoids the banned weather
  hook because the tenor is the cost, not literal weather, but the vehicle is soft
  and the sentence is long. Acceptable; could be tightened.
- CLOSE (A/S): "The cold was going back to its edge. She held the door at the
  width that let it." Quiet turn-of-the-tide button. Appropriately quiet for the
  register; slightly low-charge but earned.

### Ch.19 — What the World Keeps
- OPEN (S): "She lifted her hand out of the pool." Then "Nothing tore." Micro-
  cliffhanger resolution; clean, tense, spare — a welcome short opening after
  many long ones. Strong.
- CLOSE (A): "…the one weight that did not lift, taken up in her own two hands,
  and going on." Emotional; earned but it TRAILS — the final paragraph
  decompresses across many clauses rather than compressing. The residue is right;
  the delivery is diffuse. Consider ending the paragraph a beat earlier on a
  harder image (e.g. the born child felt across the sill) rather than the summary
  cadence.

### Ch.20 — A Place Where a Core Should Be (FINALE)
- OPEN (A): "The first weeks of a world with no one at the top of it went, in
  Wend, like this." Voice/frame hook; slightly essayistic but suits the mosaic
  denouement.
- CLOSE (S): "…there was a place where a core should be and was not, and that the
  world was not, after all, finished with her yet." The unexplained-void button,
  the series-forward hook. Strong final residue; correctly does NOT explain.

## Chapters with NO page-turn reason (flag)
None fully dead. The two openings that fail to earn the turn on their own:
- **Ch.5** — travelogue goodbye; the tension is real but buried past the walk.
- **Ch.6** — recap of Ch.5's close before the chapter starts its own clock.

## Weakest HOOKS (ranked)
1. **Ch.6** — recap opening (banned pattern). Promote "It opened at the mill."
2. **Ch.5** — low-energy travelogue; lead on the "I could" temptation interior.
3. **Ch.15 / Ch.13 / Ch.7** — the "There is a thing/difference…" template used
   three times to OPEN chapters; vary at least Ch.15 (and ideally Ch.13) so the
   device reads as deliberate essayistic voice, not a structural tic.
4. **Ch.18** — soft weather-simile opening; tighten the vehicle.

## Weakest PULLS (ranked)
The closes are the book's strength; nothing is weak. Minor notes only:
1. **Ch.19** — right residue, diffuse delivery; the final paragraph trails
   rather than compresses. Trim to a harder last image.
2. **Ch.7** — strong pull diluted across an over-long final paragraph.

## Cross-chapter VARIETY flags (the real structural note)
- **Opening template repetition:** "There is a thing…" opens Ch.7, Ch.13, Ch.15.
  Three times crosses from motif into tic AT THE HOOK POSITION. Change one or two.
- **Door-refrain closes (already in STATE.yaml, confirmed here):** Ch.2 ("there
  would be more doors"), Ch.4 ("faster than her hand could draw"), and the
  Ch.1/Ch.5/Ch.7 "…toward the warmth she had become" ominous-rim button all
  rhyme. The EVENTS escalate; the CLOSING CADENCE reads episodic. Vary the
  closing image (not the meaning) on at least one of Ch.2/4 so the early-mid
  sequence reads as a rising series rather than the same button struck.
- **Positive:** hook TYPES are otherwise well distributed — in medias res
  (Ch.4/14), question (Ch.10/12), sensory/voice (Ch.1/8/9/11), decision (Ch.2),
  clean-spare (Ch.19). Pull types span ominous, emotional, decision, question
  plant, cliffhanger, revelation — good coverage across the six.

## Bottom line
Book Three's ENDINGS are publish-ready; the closes pull hard and vary in type.
The actionable work is at the OPENINGS: fix the two that don't earn the turn
(Ch.6 recap, Ch.5 travelogue) and de-duplicate the "There is a thing…" opening
template (Ch.7/13/15 → keep one or two, vary the rest). All are line-level,
literary-preserving changes — no plot, no canon. Pair with the already-logged
door-refrain differentiation for the early-mid stretch.
