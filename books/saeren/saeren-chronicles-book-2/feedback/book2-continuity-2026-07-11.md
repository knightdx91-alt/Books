## WARNING ISSUES

### [WARN-001] Hair color drift — "silver-green" vs canonical "silver-blonde"
- **Audit:** 1b (physical description consistency); canon guardrail.
- **Chapters:** 2 (twice), reinforced 3.
- **Description:** The canon guardrail and ENTITY_STATE fix Viridia's hair as
  **silver-blonde** (Book One Ch.1 states it twice: "silver-blonde hair"; Book Two
  Ch.6, 10, 16, 19, 20 all say silver-blonde). But Chapter 2 twice describes her
  hair as **silver-green**, and Chapter 3 reinforces it via the dragon-scale motif.
- **Evidence:**
  - Ch.2, para 19 (dragon's scales): "settling toward **the silver-green of her own
    hair**".
  - Ch.2, para 45 (Ferro's POV): "a girl with **silver-green hair** standing in the
    open".
  - Ch.3, para 27: "the scales went **silver-green** under her palm the way they did
    now" (Raizen matching her hair color).
  - Contrast — Ch.6, para 59: "a slight **silver-blonde** girl"; Ch.10, para 21:
    "the **silver-blonde** hair worn loose"; Book One Ch.1: "silver-blonde hair"
    (x2).
- **Why WARNING not CRITICAL:** hair-color contradiction is normally CRITICAL, but
  here it reads as a deliberate poetic choice tied to the prismatic-scale
  mirror-motif (his scales "settle toward" her hair; her whole core is the dragon's
  mirror), so it may be an intended tint rather than an error. It still contradicts
  an explicit standing guardrail and Book One prose.
- **Suggested fix (author's call):** Either (a) change the two Ch.2 instances +
  Ch.3 to "silver-blonde" to match canon, or (b) if the green tint is intentional
  (the well changed her), make it consistent across the book and update the
  guardrail/ENTITY_STATE. Recommend (a) — it's the smaller change and Book One is
  upstream canon.
- **Cascade risk:** If (b) is chosen, Book One would then disagree with Book Two on
  a fixed physical trait — do not open that seam. (a) is the safe path.

### [WARN-002] Age number on the page — "fourteen years" (Ch.18)
- **Audit:** 2d (age tracking); canon guardrail ("NO age number on the page").
- **Chapter:** 18, para 67.
- **Description:** The no-age-number guardrail is otherwise held flawlessly (Ch.4
  "half-grown girl"; Ch.7 "half-grown human thing"; Ch.12 turn-to-15 placed with no
  number — "a year older on it than yesterday, and no one in the column knew"). But
  Ch.18 puts a number on the page that encodes her age, and it is also arguably
  off-by-one now that she has turned fifteen.
- **Evidence:**
  - Ch.18, para 67: "the deep-water stillness she had spent **fourteen years**
    living beside and a year and a month now grieving".
  - The stillness with her mother's name is a thing she has lived beside her whole
    life, so "fourteen years living beside" = her age = fourteen. Per the Ch.12
    turn-to-15 beat she is now **fifteen**, so the number is both (a) a banned
    on-page age number and (b) internally one year short.
- **Suggested fix:** Replace the count with an unnumbered phrase, e.g. "the
  deep-water stillness she had lived beside all her life and a year and a month now
  grieving" or "…she had lived beside as long as she'd been alive…". Removes the
  guardrail breach and the off-by-one at once.
- **Cascade risk:** None. Local rephrase; no downstream reference depends on the
  number.
