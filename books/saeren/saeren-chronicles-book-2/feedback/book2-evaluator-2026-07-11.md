## Anti-AI Scan — Literary thresholds (0-3 clean / 4-6 watch / 7+ fingerprint)
**Result: ~5 → WATCH band.** Same fingerprint as Books One and Three — the signature is consistent across the trilogy, which is both its voice and its tell.

- **#11 Explanatory Extension — STRONG (primary flag).** The self-completing "…the way you [do X]" simile recurs at high density: "the way a sleeping child's hand falls toward the side of the bed" (Ch.9); "the way you find a door in a house you have lived in your whole life with your eyes shut" (Ch.18); "the way a held breath holds a half-second longer when it hears a sound" (Ch.18). Deliberate, consistent-across-three-books voice; read as instrument, not accident, but it is predictable and it is the pipeline's known tic. ~0.6–0.8/1K in the dense chapters, over the literary 0.5/1K line.
- **#18 Thematic Echo Chamber — MODERATE.** Closed vocabulary does much of the connective work: "the cold working part," "the careful face," "banked to a flicker," "filed it under the heading," "the one tell she allowed herself" (breath), "the arithmetic," "the whole of her," "small true things," "held things end all at once." Individually earned; in aggregate, saturating — heaviest in Ch.11/16/18.
- **#8 Parallelism/anaphora — MINOR, allowlisted/deliberate** (the "she let them see… she let it stay" cadences in Ch.16). Load-bearing at the emotional peaks.
- **#16 Philosophical Asides — MINOR, mostly earned** ("hope is not a thing you decide about, it is a thing that happens to you while you are deciding about other things," Ch.11; "a sentence isn't made right by which direction you point it," Ch.9). Coffee-mug-extractable but dramatized.
- **#3 rule-of-three:** occasional. **#14 emotional-control:** present but IS the character (intentional, and *ruptured* in Ch.16 — the rupture is the point).
- Clear: #1, #2, #4 (em-dash gated ≤4), #5, #6, #7, #9, #12, #13, #15, #17 (dialogue is interrupted/naturalistic — Amber talks over herself, Jazen enters mid-scene), #19, #20.

**⚠ ONE HARD FINDING (not an AI pattern — a fourth-wall meta-slip):** Ch.19 contains **"two books' worth of every dark she had crossed"** and **"the weight that had been there through two books' worth of every dark"** (also Ch.16/Ch.9 use "two books'"-adjacent framings). "Books" as a unit of the character's lived time breaks the fiction — the CLAUDE.md pipeline note explicitly scans for "Book One/Two/Three" meta-slips and this is that class of error. **Fix:** replace with an in-world measure ("a year's worth," "every dark she had crossed since Hazel," "the whole of two hard years"). Typo-class, not craft, but it is a real breach and should be corrected before any further build.

## Per-Chapter Weak Spots (all still ≥ 8.5; thinning/fix targets)
1. **Ch.19 length (6,816 w) — the one structural note.** Four full emotional set-pieces stacked (transformation → Drake/Varissa → Lor-ar → Alice → "where do we go"). Each is excellent; the *accumulation* risks blunting the individual peaks and fatiguing the Devourer. **Direction:** consider relocating the Alice fireside beat to the head of Ch.20 (which opens reflective anyway), letting Ch.19 close on Lor-ar's release — the stronger curtain. Not required for the gate; a polish lever.
2. **Meta-slip "two books' worth" (Ch.19, and audit Ch.9/16).** Fix as above — highest priority because it is a correctness breach, not a taste call.
3. **Motif over-saturation in Ch.11/16/18.** "the cold working part," "filed it under the heading," "the one tell she allowed herself/her breath," "held things end all at once" cluster densely. **Direction:** thin to one load-bearing instance per chapter where events already carry the weight.
4. **Ch.9 argument edges toward stated thesis.** The consent problem is spoken very explicitly ("The not-asking is the whole crime"). It is rescued by Viridia's collapse and Jazen's counter-ditch, but a reader allergic to on-the-nose morality will feel the seam. Trust the dramatization slightly more; one or two of the restatements could go.

## Cross-Book Pattern Detection (vs Book One and Book Three)
The three books share **one consistent authorial fingerprint** — deliberate, and the source of both their distinction and their WATCH-band scan:
1. **Same simile architecture (#11):** the self-extending "…the way you [do X]" construction is the load-bearing figurative move in all three books. Densest in the interior chapters of each.
2. **Same closed motif vocabulary (#18):** "the cold working part," "the careful face," "filed it under the heading," "small true things," "the one tell (her breath)," "held things end all at once" recur across all three. Book Two adds "the arithmetic" as a governing metaphor.
3. **Same emotional-rendering spine:** grief-held-inward, ruptured exactly once per book at a sanctioned breakpoint (Book One banked → Book Two Ch.16 spends it → Book Three Ch.10 the private break to Raizen). This is *designed* and tracked in canon, not accidental — credit as architecture, not tic.
4. **Same "reflective essayistic → forward" finale structure** (Book Two Ch.20, Book Three Ch.20 both count the cost then face east). Bookend consistency, but predictable across volumes.
5. **Divergence — Book Two's advantage:** it has a *war-story motor* the other two lack, giving it the strongest external propulsion and the single most powerful set-piece (Ch.16) in the trilogy. Its Emotion (9.0) is the highest dimension score across all three evaluations.

**Systemic note:** the fingerprint is the pipeline's, not this book's alone. It is the trilogy's voice; it is also why every book lands in the anti-AI WATCH band rather than clean. No book should try to erase it, but each would gain from *dosing it down* in the 2–3 densest chapters.
