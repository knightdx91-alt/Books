# Hook-Craft Diagnostic — Book Two: The Resistance

Date: 2026-07-11. REPORT ONLY — no prose modified. Author sign-off required before any change.
Scope: chapter openings (first lines) and closings (last lines), all 20 chapters.
Calibration: literary/upper-YA. Hook target >=6, Pull target >=7 (the book's positioning is the
quiet literary voice; pulls carry more weight than loud hooks). "Strong / adequate / weak" below
is relative to that bar.

Book-wide finding: the PULLS are the book's great strength — nearly every chapter ends on a
compressed, forward-charged line (a decision, a cut, a named dread). No chapter leaves the reader
without a reason to turn the page. The softer edge is the OPENINGS: several chapters begin with a
slow essayistic/travelogue wind-up or a waking/room-establishing beat that the voice rescues but
does not sharpen. The three worth the author's eye are Ch.8, Ch.10, Ch.13.

Legend for types — Hooks: In Medias Res / Question / Sensory / Voice / Contrast / Ticking Clock.
Pulls: Cliffhanger / Revelation / Question Plant / Emotional Gut Punch / Ominous Promise / Decision.

## Chapter 1 — The Box of Invisible Air
- OPENING (adequate, leaning strong). Voice + sensory.
  > "For three mornings she had woken already braced — for smoke, for the bell, for the particular weight of a building deciding to come down on the people inside it."
  Technically a waking beat, but the bracing-that-does-not-believe-the-quiet earns it and ties Book One's fire directly into the first breath. The one risk: the load-bearing first sentence is long and back-loads its image. Fine as is.
- CLOSING (strong). Question Plant / Ominous.
  > "She only knew that the line was coming in."
  Clean, taut, sets the whole book's engine (the name coming up the road). Keep.

## Chapter 2 — The Thread Pulled Taut
- OPENING (strong). Question / Voice.
  > "The thread went slack a little after dawn, and Viridia knew before she was properly awake that he was close."
  Information gap + emotional pull in one line. Keep.
- CLOSING (strong). Ominous Promise / Emotional.
  > "...a dragon felt her decide, and rose, and came toward the hall to be the thing that was there."
  Keep.

## Chapter 3 — Alive Is Not the Same as Safe
- OPENING (strong). Voice.
  > "The three days did not pass. They sat."
  Best-in-book short hook. Keep.
- CLOSING (strong). Ominous Promise.
  > "And that the dark had wanted her to say yes."
  Keep.

## Chapter 4 — His Mother's Papers
- OPENING (strong). Revelation / Question.
  > "The box came up out of the ground in front of her before she knew what it was, and that was the first time she understood that Lady Lightwell had thought about her after she was dead."
  Keep.
- CLOSING (adequate). Question Plant / Decision.
  > "But soon. And maybe by us after all."
  Emotionally right, but the italic coda lands soft after the stronger "chosen. First. On purpose." beat three sentences up. Consider (author's call) ending on the harder image rather than the wistful tag.
  Alt to consider: end on "...by a woman in the ground who would never know she'd come through." — a colder, more forward line. Minor; the current version is not broken.

## Chapter 5 — The Furnace and the Match
- OPENING (strong). Voice.
  > "The lie people tell about hard things is that they are hard."
  Keep.
- CLOSING (strong). Cliffhanger / Revelation.
  > "It was three words long. // Who else knows."
  Excellent. Keep.

## Chapter 6 — The Window Narrows
- OPENING (adequate). Voice (deliberate echo of Ch.5's last line).
  > "Who else knows."
  The repeat is intentional (chain continuity) and works, but two chapters running now hinge on the same three words; by design this is fine once.
- CLOSING (adequate — weakest pull in the book). Ominous / Question.
  > "...and the window narrowed by exactly the width of the silence before Jazen answered."
  This cuts away BEFORE Jazen answers — a held-breath ending. It reads as slightly manufactured because the withheld answer is a small beat (yes/no to a plan), not a life-or-death turn, and the next chapter does not pay it off as a cliffhanger. It also decompresses across a very long final sentence rather than compressing.
  Alt to consider: end on Jazen's actual answer, or on a single hard line of Viridia's resolve, so the chapter turns on a decision rather than a withheld one. Flag for author.

## Chapter 7 — What She Tries First
- OPENING (adequate/strong). Question / Voice.
  > "She sent herself out into the world twice that day, and by two roads that should not have cost the same, and did."
  Keep.
- CLOSING (adequate). Ominous Promise.
  > "...deciding how you teach the unmaking to a child who is right not to want it."
  Good thematic dread but soft-focus (a distant father musing). Slightly lower charge than neighbors. Acceptable; not a fix priority.

## Chapter 8 — Dust to Dust
- OPENING (WEAK — flag). Sleep/waking beat.
  > "She slept the way Drake had told her to, which was to say badly and on purpose, lying still in the dark of the children's hall..."
  This is a waking/sleep opening — the one opening in the book that opens on the character in bed. The voice ("badly and on purpose") half-rescues it, but it is a slow, low-tension entry to a chapter that ends on a hard confrontation. Reader momentum from Ch.7's dread dissipates here.
  Alt to consider (author's call): open on the going-UP instead of the lying-down — e.g. lead with the threshold/ascent beat that already exists a few lines down ("This morning she went up."), and fold the "slept badly on purpose" into the second beat. Start with motion, not stillness.
- CLOSING (strong). Decision / Cliffhanger (dialogue).
  > "'I know,' Viridia said. 'That's why we have to sit down.'"
  Keep.

## Chapter 9 — The Right
- OPENING (adequate). Sensory.
  > "The hall smelled of the wounded man and of the lamp that had burned all night..."
  Sensory immersion with the dying-ward-mage as the off-note. Solid, quiet. Keep.
- CLOSING (strong). Decision / Emotional.
  > "She picked up the bread. And she ate it. And she did not let her hands shake..."
  Keep.

## Chapter 10 — The Almost
- OPENING (WEAK — flag). Establishing shot / Sensory.
  > "The room they had got for the last door was a back room in a railway hotel two valleys over, the kind of place where men who sold grain to the army stayed one night and left no name in the book..."
  A single ~70-word sentence that pans the room before it puts anyone in motion — the classic establishing-shot opening. The detail is fine; the placement is camera-first. Momentum is low for a chapter that becomes a chase.
  Alt to consider: open on the councilor already crossing the yard wrong ("She marked the wrongness of it" — the coat too good for the cloth), i.e. lead with the watched figure and the tell, then let the room settle in behind. Person-doing-something first, room second.
- CLOSING (strong). Ticking Clock.
  > "...Viridia pumped the trolley faster into the closing afternoon and kept her hands from shaking on the bar, because the day was not done with her..."
  Keep.

## Chapter 11 — The Cost of Hiding
- OPENING (strong). Voice / Ticking Clock.
  > "They beat the glass to the camp by the length of a held breath, which is to say they did not beat it at all."
  Keep.
- CLOSING (strong). Decision.
  > "'We march now,' she said... 'From the ash. We march now.'"
  Keep.

## Chapter 12 — Goodbyes
- OPENING (adequate). Voice.
  > "The road took them all morning to become an army."
  Fine aphoristic entry; lower charge but tonally correct for a goodbyes chapter. Keep.
- CLOSING (strong). Ominous Promise / Decision.
  > "They took the long road east to the capital. There was no other road left to take."
  Keep.

## Chapter 13 — The Road and the Walls
- OPENING (WEAK — flag). Travelogue / Voice.
  > "The plain went on for two days, and the two days taught Viridia a thing about armies that the camp had never had room to teach her, which was that an army on a road is mostly not a fighting thing at all but an eating, sleeping, blistered, quarrelling, grieving thing..."
  A long march-summary essay opening — the lowest-tension entry in the back half. The chapter ends on a full-stop cliffhanger, so the contrast is stark: soft in, hard out.
  Alt to consider: open on a single concrete beat from the march (the stuck wheel she puts her shoulder to; the infant carried a half-mile) and let the "army is a carried thing" thesis follow the image. Ground the reader in one body doing one thing before the two-day generalization.
- CLOSING (strong). Cliffhanger.
  > "He opened his mouth to speak."
  Keep. (Pairs with Ch.14's opening — good chain.)

- OPENING (strong). Question.

## Chapter 20 — None of Them Know Why (FINALE)
- OPENING (strong). Voice.
  > "She had counted her dead all her life, and days later, on a road going east in the early light, she counted them again."
  Keep.
- CLOSING (strong — series residue + hook). Ominous Promise.
  > "...into a whole world that did not know it was whole, and none of them, not one single soul on any road in any direction, knew why."
  Deposits the intended residue (unfixable cost carried inward) and the Marick/void-rim seed sits just above it. Correct final-chapter pull: emotional residue, not forward suspense. Keep.

## Summary of weakest points (author decision required — nothing changed)

WEAKEST HOOKS (openings), in priority order:
1. Ch.8 — opens on the character sleeping/lying still; the one waking-beat opening in the book, low tension before a hard confrontation. Suggest opening on "she went up" (motion first).
2. Ch.13 — two-day march-summary essay; the lowest-tension entry in the back half against a hard cliffhanger close. Suggest opening on one concrete body/action, then the thesis.
3. Ch.10 — establishing-shot room pan (one ~70-word camera sentence) before anyone moves; a chase chapter deserves a person-first entry. Suggest leading with the councilor crossing the yard wrong.
(Ch.6 and Ch.12 are aphoristic/quiet but tonally correct — not fixes, just the softer end of the range.)

WEAKEST PULLS (endings):
1. Ch.6 — the only ending that reads slightly manufactured: it cuts away on the SILENCE before Jazen answers a plan question, and the next chapter doesn't pay it off as a real cliffhanger; it also decompresses over a long final sentence. Suggest turning it on an actual answer/decision.
2. Ch.4 — soft italic coda ("maybe by us after all") lands after a stronger line; consider ending on the colder "chosen. First. On purpose." image.
3. Ch.7 — distant, musing dread; lower charge than neighbors but tonally fine.

CHAPTERS WITH "NO REASON TO TURN THE PAGE": NONE. Every chapter ends with forward charge. The
weak items above are relative softness within a strong field, not broken hooks. The pull system is
the book's engine and it holds all the way to the finale.

CHAIN / VARIETY: transitions are strong — Ch.5->6 (three-words echo), Ch.13->14 ("opened his
mouth to speak" -> "began to speak"), Ch.14->15 ("one breath"), Ch.17->18 ("reached for the well")
form deliberate momentum bridges. Hook and pull types are well varied; no monotony flag. The climax
run (Ch.14-18) is correctly relentless per the structural targets.
