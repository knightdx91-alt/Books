# Voice DNA — "Pompeii" (working title)

> Executable voice spec. The Writer writes FROM this; Dialogue Polish & Hook Craft check
> AGAINST it; Editor & Evaluator hold the line with it. Style gate is binding:
> **em-dash ≤ 4 per chapter (ABSOLUTE)**, similes/metaphor markers ≤ 4.0 / 1,000 words,
> no NEW cross-chapter repeated 4–6 word phrase (motifs allowlisted), tics under ceiling.

---

## 0. POV / TENSE (author-fixed)
**First-person, past tense, RETROSPECTIVE** — older Mara narrating. Justification:
1. It is the **Outlander register** the author named (Gabaldon = first-person past, Claire's
   wry modern intelligence inside the period).
2. Retrospection is the book's **suspense engine**: the narrator knows the volcano comes, so
   dread can bleed backward ("I did not know yet that…") — the reader feels the catastrophe
   before the character, which is the entire point of setting a love story at Pompeii.
3. Single-POV keeps the time-door's rules honest (we only know what Mara knows; Fenn's
   inserts carry what she can't see).

**NARRATOR CONCEIT — RECONCILED (author-fixed: she dies; NO modern coda).** Because Mara stays
and dies in the ash and the book ends in 79 AD, the retrospective vantage is NOT a future survivor
looking back. It is the voice of a woman who has lived the whole nine years and is telling it from
*inside* the ending — the foreknowledge ("I would learn, too late…") is lived memory, not a
20th-century deathbed. Read it as her account given in the long dark of the last shelter (Ch.45),
the whole life passing before her in the hours before the surge. The "thirty years" register
becomes "the years I had," not "the years since." **NO POV shift, NO modern frame, NO close-third
coda.** Stay in Mara's first-person voice from Ch.1 through the final line in the ash.

## 1. GLOBAL NARRATIVE VOICE

- **Vocabulary level:** 6/10 — literate, sensory, period-textured; never antiquarian-showy.
  Modern intelligence under period surface. Latin/Greek terms used sparingly and always made
  clear by context, never glossed in an aside.
- **Sentence rhythm:** Mixed/varied. Long and flowing in wonder, memory, intimacy; clips hard
  and short under fear and violence. The contrast IS the voice.
- **Formality:** Conversational-to-measured. Deliberate friction between Mara's modern
  interiority and the formal ancient world she narrates — she translates, undercuts, compares
  dryly, and is faintly ashamed of doing so.
- **Humor:** Dry, subtle, defensive — Mara's coping. Vanishes entirely under real fear.
- **Emotional register:** Open but earned; restraint is default so the breaks land.
- **Distinctive features:**
  - Retrospective foreknowledge intrusions ("I would learn, far too late, that…") — rationed,
    one or two per chapter MAX, never in action scenes.
  - The conservator's eye: she sees the ancient world as both miracle and artifact; describes
    living things with a cataloguer's precision when calm.
  - Anachronism only as *owned interiority* (her private comparisons), never in narration of
    fact and never in dialogue.
- **Anti-patterns (NEVER):**
  - Analytical/explanatory similes ("like a metaphor for her grief"). The #1 AI fingerprint.
  - Metacognitive narration ("she realized she was realizing," "I felt myself feeling").
  - Emotional-temperature reports ("a wave of grief washed over her") — show the glasses, the
    *click*, the dropped object instead.
  - Info-dump history lectures; Latin glossed in parentheses; modern slang in narration-of-fact.
  - Lyrical escalation at the climax (see Voice Under Pressure — do the OPPOSITE).

### VOICE UNDER PRESSURE (mandatory, enforced)
At maximum emotion, Mara's voice **goes flat, short, and procedural** — she catalogues. Her
conservator's training is her panic response: condition, material, damage, as if people were
artifacts, because naming them as people would end her. **The prose strips to inventory.**
- Counter-default: at the climax (Ch.36, 41, 42) do NOT go lyrical/soaring. **Deflate.** Short
  declaratives. Nouns. The lyricism lives in the CALM chapters; terror is a packing list.
- Secondary break: retrospective intrusions cluster when a memory is too hot to narrate straight
  ("I have had thirty years to ask myself—" then she changes the subject).
- Tertiary break: the *click* memory intrudes uninvited at the worst moments.
- Rehearsal beats: Ch.16 (catalogue-brain goes quiet in intimacy) and Ch.22 (procedural under
  birth-pain) PRE-TRAIN this device so the climax pays off, not surprises.

## 2. PER-CHARACTER VOICE CARDS

#### MARA (protagonist / narrator)
- **Vocabulary band:** Educated modern (**late-1990s frame of reference — author-fixed**;
  no 2000s+ tech idiom, no "google/text/swipe"; her modern touchstones are film cameras,
  mixtapes/Discman, carousel slides, payphones, paper notebooks), museum/conservation register
  leaking in (condition, provenance, weathering, friable); dry idiom; private anachronisms.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Spirals into long comma-linked sentences when wondering; chops to
  fragments when afraid. Classifies under stress.
- **Rhythm:** Statements that turn into questions; self-correcting clauses ("It was bronze. It
  was older than bronze should be.").
- **Verbal tics:** Naming materials/conditions; the bread-opinion; "I would learn" foreknowledge.
- **Never says:** Sentimental abstractions about love ("my soul," "completed me"); she shows, deflects.
- **Sample line:** "Severe oxidation, a cracked guard, and a man who had clearly killed with it
  recently. I catalogued him the way I catalogued everything I was afraid of."

#### VALENS (ex-gladiator)
- **Vocabulary band:** Latin-blunt, soldier/arena register; concrete nouns; debts owed and paid.
  Greek used as a tool, never loved (occasional flat literal phrasing in Greek scenes).
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Short, level, declarative. Subordinates rarely. Understatement.
- **Rhythm:** Statements, not questions. Speaks of his own death casually, others' tenderly.
- **Signature (NOT a verbal tic — physical + syntactic):** deflects feeling into a TASK — his hands
  work a knot or a strap when emotion rises (pays off at the loss when they have nothing to tie);
  understates mortal danger to nothing. **Do NOT give him a counting/enumerating habit** (that device
  is spent in another of the author's books and overlaps Drusus — banned here).
- **Never says:** His feelings directly; the word *love* until very late, and then only once.
- **Sample line:** "Bad ground both ways. Stay behind me and stop telling me the odds."

#### THEANO (the Pythia)
- **Vocabulary band:** Old, dry, ritual when the god is in; mundane and tart otherwise.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** True sentences disguised as riddles; flat second-person-plural in vision.
- **Rhythm:** Cryptic that turns out literal; chore-talk between portents.
- **Verbal tics:** Switching register mid-sentence; the cats.
- **Never says:** A direct reassurance; she will not comfort.
- **Sample line:** "You will keep nothing you can carry. Mind the third step; it's loose."

#### FENN (the vanished hunter)
- **Vocabulary band:** Edwardian-adventurer self-mythology (in old documents); plain and tired in person.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** Lists. Numbered. Even his grief is enumerated.
- **Rhythm:** Documents = florid; the live old man = spare, gentle, exhausted.
- **Verbal tics:** Numbering; "the door is not honest."
- **Never says:** Self-pity directly; he files it under a number instead.
- **Sample line:** "Regret the fourth: I tested the door eleven times. It only ever wanted me."

#### KALLIA (Greek innkeeper)
- **Verbal tic:** Proverbs, always slightly misquoted, always weaponized.
- **Never says:** Her son's name aloud. **Sample:** "A lit lamp wastes no oil it wasn't going to waste."

#### DRUSUS (Roman trader)
- **Verbal tic:** Prices in the middle of feelings. **Never says:** "I can't afford it."
- **Sample:** "Grief? Grief I can manage. It's the funeral wine at four asses a jar that'll ruin me."

#### SABINA (the scandal-woman)
- **Verbal tic:** Courtly kindness with a blade in it; reflexive lying. **Never says:** A plain truth.
- **Sample:** "I always thought you deserved better than what they did to you. I may even have meant it."

## 3. VOICE DIFFERENTIATION MATRIX (cover-the-name, pre-solved)

| Character | Sharpest distinguisher | Contrast vs nearest |
|-----------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Mara | Catalogues/classifies conditions under stress; modern dry idiom | vs Valens: she questions & spirals; he states & clips |
| Valens | Short level declaratives; hands work a knot when feeling threatens; understates danger | vs Drusus: silence-and-a-task vs cost-talk (no shared counting) |
| Theano | Riddle-that's-literal + chore-talk register switch | vs Fenn: she withholds truth; he enumerates it |
| Fenn | Numbers everything, even regret (earned verbal tic) | vs Mara: he lists, she catalogues conditions |
| Kallia | Misquoted weaponized proverbs (earned verbal tic) | vs Sabina: folk-blunt vs courtly-veiled |
| Drusus | Reduces feeling to its COST to dodge its weight (worldview, not a counting bit) | vs Valens: venal-loud vs stoic-spare |
| Sabina | Double-edged courtesy; reflexive lie | vs Theano: Sabina charms, Theano repels |

No two share a sharpest marker OR a device (counting/listing/pricing count as ONE device — only Fenn
keeps enumeration). Distinctness leads on attention/syntax/evasion/physicality; a spoken tic is reserved
for Fenn and Kallia. Cover-the-name test passes.

## 4. ANTI-PATTERN BUDGET (genre: Romance / historical — commercial band, density / 1,000 words)
- **Explanatory/analytical similes (Pattern #11):** ≤ 4.0 / 1k (style-gate hard cap). Aim ≤ 3.0.
- **Em-dashes:** **≤ 4 per CHAPTER, absolute** (style_check.py). At ~5,400 words/chapter that is
  well under 1/1k — tighter than typical; budget them for genuine interruption only.
- **Adverbs in dialogue tags:** near-zero. Prefer "he said" + action beat (a knot, a count).
- **"It was as if / almost as though":** ≤ 1 per chapter.
- **Metacognitive narration ("she realized she was realizing"):** 0 — banned.
- **Emotional-temperature reports ("a wave of X washed over her"):** ≤ 1 per chapter; prefer the
  object (glasses, knot, dropped bowl, the *click*).
- **Retrospective foreknowledge intrusions:** ≤ 2 per chapter; 0 in action scenes.
- **Allowlist (deliberate motifs — do NOT flag as repeated phrases):** "the one it brought,"
  the *click* of folded glasses, the bread starter. (Add these to
  tools/style_check.py ALLOWLIST before drafting.)

## 5. THE EXPLICIT INTIMACY SCENES — how they read on the page
Heat level: **explicit / on-page detailed.** Register: **sensual + character-driven**, never
clinical, never purple.
- **Anatomy serves character, not inventory.** The scene advances the relationship and the arc —
  what they're afraid of, what they finally let go of — through the body, not a parts-list.
- **Mara's signature in heat:** her catalogue-brain *goes quiet* (it never does otherwise). That
  silence is how the reader knows it's real (planted Ch.12, paid in the ash — Ch.33/35). Track it.
- **Valens's signature in heat:** control vs. surrender — the man who keeps everyone at a
  transaction's distance has nowhere to put his hands except on her; the knot-tic has no object,
  so it stops. His stillness breaking is the charge.
- **Voice still breaks under pressure here too:** at peak, prose may go short and present-tense-
  feeling, sensory, plain nouns — NOT a cascade of metaphor. Sensuality through specificity and
  restraint, not through elevated diction.
- **Anti-patterns specific to heat:** no purple euphemism, no clinical terminology, no
  metaphor-stacking ("her body a [noun] of [noun]"), no emotional-temperature reports mid-scene.
  Em-dash budget is easy to blow in a breathless scene — watch it.
- **Frequency/placement (CURRENT 36-ch outline; old Ch.16/18/20 was stale 45/48-ch numbering,
  reconciled 2026-07-13):** yearning/slow-burn built at Ch.11; first explicit scene at **Ch.12 "The
  First Night Is a Mistake"** (clumsy, then funny — intimacy over perfection); fuller at **Ch.15**
  (marriage warming); a late payoff **~Ch.22** (the last unshadowed joy); restraint returns under the
  Movement V dread (no heat after the eruption begins — the body's last language there is the re-tied
  sandal, not sex).
- **Consent & character agency:** both fully willing adults; the tension is emotional risk, not
  coercion. The heat earns its place by being where the lie ("stay outside" / "the dead don't
  leave") finally cracks.

## 7. OUTLANDER COMP CALIBRATION — measured from the source epub (2026-07-10)

Empirical targets extracted from **Diana Gabaldon, *Outlander* (Cross Stitch)**, ~57,000 words
sampled across three chapters (Ch.24 "By the Pricking of My Thumbs" — feast/dialogue; Ch.25 "Thou
Shalt Not Suffer a Witch to Live" — capture/peril; Ch.35 "Wentworth Prison" — tense infiltration).
These are the numbers the book's Outlander register is calibrated TO. Treat as targets, not gates
(the binding gates remain in §4); use them when a chapter's `metaphor_check`/`rhythm_check`/
`show_tell_check` output drifts and you need to know which way Outlander leans.

### Measured metrics (the register, in numbers)
| Dimension | Outlander (measured) | What to do in Pompeii |
|---|---|---|
| Median sentence length | **15 words** (mean 16.8) | Aim median 14–16; this is the spine of the rhythm. |
| Short sentences (≤6 words) | **16%** | Keep ~15%+ — the clipped counter-beat, esp. under fear. |
| Long sentences (≥35 words) | **7%** (max ~80) | Allow the occasional 40–80w spiral in wonder/memory; ~1 in 14 sentences, no more. |
| **Em-dashes** | **~0 / 1k** | **Gabaldon essentially does NOT use em-dashes.** Validates the ≤4/ch gate hard. |
| **Semicolons** | **3.3 / 1k** | *This is the Outlander move.* Use semicolons where the AI habit reaches for an em-dash — link two independent clauses that belong in one breath. Budget ~3/1k. |
| En-dashes | 3.0 / 1k | Parenthetical/appositive dashes ( – ) do the interruption work em-dashes would. |
| Ellipsis | 1.5 / 1k | Claire's trailing/self-interrupting thought; rationed, not a tic. |
| Dialogue share | **~28%** (peril chapters lower) | Confirms the band; recalibrate target to **~28–38%** (interior/catastrophe chapters legitimately dip below). |

### Craft signature (read from the prose, not the numbers)
1. **Wry modern intelligence inside period surface — via CHARACTERIZING simile, not analytical
   simile.** Gabaldon uses similes freely, but they *characterize and amuse*; they never explain an
   emotion. E.g. Sir Fletcher "looked rather like a large ham seated in the governor's chair… though
   few hams boasted such bright blue eyes." That is the good kind. The banned kind (§1 anti-patterns)
   is the abstract-analytical "like a metaphor for her grief." **Distinction to hold:** simile that
   makes you *see a thing / smile* = Outlander; simile that *interprets a feeling* = AI fingerprint.
2. **Dry authorial asides mid-description** — the narrator wanders one clause sideways into a joke
   or a hypothesis (the carved gnomic heads "perhaps drawn from the patronizing features of earlier
   MacKenzie lairds… by a carver with a sense of humor, or a strong family connection"), then returns.
   This is Mara's conservator's-eye tangent already specced in §1 — the epub confirms the cadence.
3. **Bodily realism punctures grandeur.** In the middle of a formal feast, Jamie's line is "my
   bladder's going to burst in another moment or two." The comedy of the body is load-bearing, not
   comic relief bolted on — it keeps the romance human. Use it (Valens/the children).
4. **Sensory-material specificity as the immersion engine** — "the guttered wax glowed gold," "a
   striped silk waistcoat fitted him like a second skin," the thieves'-hole stench catalogued by a
   frightened woman. Matches the authenticity dial (~8 sensory). Every scene grounds in one or two
   *specific* period materials, never generic "ancient" atmosphere.
5. **First-person foreknowledge + present control under threat.** Claire narrates peril with her
   observing intelligence intact ("torn between exultation and panic," still reading the room). NOTE
   A DELIBERATE DIVERGENCE: our book pushes FURTHER than Gabaldon under maximum pressure — Mara's
   voice *deflates to procedural inventory* (§1 Voice Under Pressure), where Claire stays sensory.
   That flatter terror is a chosen departure from the comp, not a miss — hold it at the climax
   (Ch.29–36), and let the calmer chapters carry the fuller Gabaldon sensory-wry register.

### One-line directive for the Writer
Write Mara at Gabaldon's **median-15, semicolon-not-em-dash, one-specific-material-per-scene,
characterizing-not-analytical-simile** register in the calm/immersive chapters — then break it
DOWN (short, procedural, nouns) under fear, further than Gabaldon would. Source sample retained
out-of-repo (Drive: "Outlander Book"); re-extract if recalibration is ever needed.
