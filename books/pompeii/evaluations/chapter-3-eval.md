## Cross-Chapter Pattern Detection — ONE GATE TRIP + TWO WATCH-ITEMS
1. **[REQUIRED FIX — repeated-phrase gate] A whole Ch.2 sentence-cadence has been cloned into Ch.3.** The donor is Ch.2 L110; two of its signatures reappear in Ch.3:
   - "…no longer yours to be casual with" — Ch.2 L110 verbatim / Ch.3 L61 verbatim ("the way you handle a find you have just understood is real and therefore no longer yours to be casual with").
   - "a floor going soft" — Ch.2 L110 / Ch.3 L73 ("feel the floor of the known world go soft under his hands").
   This trips the "no NEW cross-chapter repeated phrase" guardrail (same violation class as the "have never been able to" trip caught in Ch.2). The gates note for Ch.3 says clean; it is not, on this axis.
2. **[WATCH] "tell myself anything" echo:** Ch.2 L170 "I have always been able to tell myself anything." / Ch.3 L91 "I am good at telling myself things." Varied enough to pass, but it is the third chapter leaning on this exact self-deception beat. Rotate the phrasing.
3. **[WATCH — motif saturation] Bread and the *click* now appear in all three chapters.** Both are ALLOWLISTED deliberate motifs and both are used freshly here (good taverna bread vs the earlier stale loaves; the *click* deployed as the specced tertiary voice-break). Not a defect in Ch.3. But the *click* especially must now be RATIONED — it is the climax's payoff instrument (Ch.36); using it every chapter spends its charge early. Recommend: no *click* in Ch.4–5 unless load-bearing.
