# Character Bible — "The Hour of Ash" (working title "Pompeii")

> **Purpose.** One entry per NAMED character. Each entry ends with a **DISTINCTNESS GUARANTEE** —
> the single sharpest marker that makes the character un-blur-able, plus the explicit contrast
> against their nearest neighbour in the cast. If you can delete the name tag from a line and still
> know who is speaking/acting, the guarantee is holding. This doc is the companion to
> `voice-dna.md` (§2 voice cards, §3 differentiation matrix) and the forthcoming
> `ENTITY_STATE.yaml` (structured facts/timeline). **When you introduce or evolve a named
> character, update this file in the same commit.** Canon here defers to STATE.yaml / foundation.md
> on any conflict.

**Legend:** ★ = fully drafted on the page · ◐ = blueprinted, not yet written · ⚠ = open continuity
item to resolve before the character recurs.

### How distinctness works here (read before adding characters)
Distinctness is **NOT** "give everyone a verbal tic." A cast where every character has a catchphrase
reads as machine-dealt quirks — the AI fingerprint we fight. Separate characters on the **deeper axes
first**, in this order:
1. **Attention / worldview** — what they notice and reduce the world to (Mara: material condition;
   Drusus: cost; Theano: omen vs chore).
2. **Syntax / register** — sentence shape and formality (Valens: short level declaratives; Sabina:
   ornate courtly subordination; Verrall: flat plain North).
3. **Evasion** — what they will NOT say (Valens: feeling; Kallia: her son's name; Sabina: any plain truth).
4. **Physicality / silence** — the body and the pause (Valens's hands on a knot; Verrall squaring a cloth).

A **verbal tic is optional seasoning**, earned by only a few: **Fenn** (numbering — he is literally a
bookkeeper of his regret) and **Kallia** (misquoted proverbs — cultural armour). Everyone else is
distinguished WITHOUT a catchphrase. **No two characters may share a *device* (counting, listing,
pricing are the same device in different coats), and no signature may repeat across the author's other
books** (cross-book pattern rule — e.g. "counting things" is spent elsewhere and is banned here).

## PRINCIPALS

### MARA REYES ★ — protagonist / first-person retrospective narrator
- **Age / era:** 34, late-1990s modern frame (1998). Crosses to ~70 AD; the story spans ~9 years to 79 AD.
- **Occupation:** Museum conservator / freelance antiquities cataloguer. "I sit in a basement four
  days a week with a scalpel and a tray of solvents." Handles the dead beautifully; lives among them.
- **Physical:** Mid-thirties; favours her father "round the eyes" (Verrall clocks it, Ch.2). Steady
  hands over a corroded hinge; those same hands fail her at the drawer and at the shop counter.
- **Wound:** Her father died with a dig unfinished and a 25-year-unkept promise ("next season I'll
  take you with me"). She has spent her adult life finishing other people's excavations, none of her own.
- **Lie:** *"The past is safer than the present. The dead don't leave."*
- **Want:** Finish what her father couldn't; follow the map; go back (literally and emotionally).
- **Need:** Stop curating grief and live *forward* — let the living love her, knowing the living can be lost.
- **Arc:** Keeper-of-the-dead → maker-of-the-living → at the door, chooses a *future* over a *return*
  (she STAYS and dies in the ash as an ACTIVE choice).
- **Chaos (4/4):** bread-opinions (uninvited, never justified); sunk-cost completionism (finishes what
  she shouldn't — the survey, the marriage-to-a-place, the staying-too-long); the *click* of folded
  glasses intruding at the worst moments; failed emotional management (laughs at a foreign funeral;
  snaps over a broken bowl that was never about the bowl).
- **Contradiction:** Meticulous cataloguer who is catastrophically impulsive about the one thing that matters.
- **Voice-under-pressure:** deflates to flat procedural *inventory* under fear/violence/birth — the
  opposite of lyrical (the chosen divergence from Gabaldon). Catalogue-brain *goes quiet* in intimacy.
- **DISTINCTNESS GUARANTEE:** *Catalogues/classifies under stress in modern dry idiom; spirals into
  long comma-linked sentences when wondering, chops to fragments when afraid.* **vs Valens:** she
  questions and spirals; he states and clips. **vs Fenn:** she catalogues *conditions* (a
  conservator); he *numbers* (a bookkeeper).

### VALENS ◐ — the ex-gladiator (former arena name "Lupus")
- **Status:** Freed (papers he was promised and cheated of); runs a horse-and-mule trade outside Delphi.
- **Physical:** Big, scarred, watchful, courteous; "too well-spoken for a freedman, too scarred to be
  safe to ask why." Killer's stillness; gentle with animals.
- **Scandal (author-fixed):** Accused over a **provincial legate's wife** — he was her sworn
  **bodyguard**, framed politically. Never her lover. He won too well; a powerful man needed him gone;
  her name was the cleanest blade.
- **Wound:** Being the best made him useful to destroy. The arena he survived; the *lie* he could not fight.
- **Lie:** *"If I am needed but never loved, I can't be betrayed again. Be useful. Stay outside."*
- **Want:** Anonymity, debts paid, the freedom-papers he was cheated of.
- **Need:** To be *chosen* by someone who knows exactly what he is and stays.
- **Arc:** Man who keeps everyone at a transaction's distance → husband/father who lets himself need →
  at the climax, the one who *understands* the door and tells her the truth he could have hidden.
- **Chaos (4/4):** re-ties knots/harness-leather in tense scenes (his hands need a task to survive
  feeling); hyper-vigilant threat mind-reading (wrong in mundane ways); the smell of arena sand
  intrudes in good moments; weeps once — at his baby son mispronouncing his old arena name — and leaves the room.
- **First live scene:** Ch.7 "The Roman With the Mules" (the mule-yard two-hander). Confirmed to this
  card: short level declaratives; hands work a knot/strap when feeling threatens (paid at the scene's
  peak — "nothing left to work... open and still"); understates mortal danger ("a liability with legs");
  kindness turned sideways ("I'm sorry, which is more than I said to the last three"); withholds any
  warmth behind the closing lie "It's nothing to me either way," told to a knot instead of to her face.
  His refusal is protective, not cold-hearted (the Krisa widow / the poppies). Runs the yard with a boy
  (unnamed, ~12) and a string of mules/horses outside the sanctuary road.
- **Marriage:** marries Mara (Ch.13); children **Lucius** then **Aelia**.
- **DISTINCTNESS GUARANTEE (no verbal tic — control & distance):** *Syntax of a man who trusts nothing
  he hasn't secured himself — short, level, declarative; subordinates rarely; understates mortal danger
  to nothing ("Two ways out, both bad"); speaks of his own death casually and others' tenderly; withholds
  the word "love" until once, very late.* His only recurring TELL is **physical, not spoken** — his hands
  work a knot or a strap whenever feeling threatens, and the wound-payoff at the loss is his hands with
  **nothing left to tie** (re-read seed #3; also the heat-scene device — the knot-tic has no object, so
  it stops). **vs Mara:** he states and clips; she questions and spirals. **vs Drusus:** Valens holds
  money and feeling both at arm's length in silence; Drusus can't stop naming the price of everything.

### THEANO ◐ — the Pythia (Oracle of Delphi) [personal name a proposal; confirm before Ch.10]
- **Surface:** Not the white-robed cliché — 70 AD Delphi is in genteel decline. Shrewd, tired; knows
  the oracle is half theatre and half something she can't explain, and can no longer tell which half spoke.
- **Wound:** She has given true prophecies that destroyed the people who heard them; she fears her own accuracy.
- **Lie:** *"Truth told plainly is a weapon; mercy is to be vague."*
- **Want / Need:** To be left alone / to risk one clear true thing before she dies.
- **Arc:** Gives Mara the **WHERE** (Pompeii) but withholds the **WHEN** — not theatrics; she has SEEN
  the when and it is a death sentence. Her first prophecy already encodes the ending (re-read seed,
  pays Ch.35 as mercy not cruelty).
- **Chaos (3/4):** the temple **cats** (knows all their politics, none of Rome's); catastrophizes the
  trivial, serene about the apocalyptic; goes cold/procedural at the wrong human moment.
- **Contradiction:** Mouthpiece of a god she privately doesn't believe in — until she does.
- **DISTINCTNESS GUARANTEE:** *Speaks true sentences disguised as riddles, then switches mid-breath to
  flat chore-talk ("You will keep nothing you can carry. Mind the third step; it's loose"); flat
  second-person-plural when the god is in; will not comfort, ever.* **vs Fenn:** she *withholds* the
  truth; he *enumerates* it. **vs Sabina:** Theano repels; Sabina charms.

### AUGUST FENN ★(documents) / ◐(live) — the vanished treasure-hunter
- **Two registers:** In documents (Ch.1 note, Ch.2 ledger, later journal inserts) = florid
  Edwardian-adventurer self-mythology that *comes down* off its own confidence as it goes. In person
  (live, Ch.20) = spare, gentle, exhausted, old.
- **Surface / the world's verdict (Verrall, Ch.2):** "a fraud… the ordinary way," a self-mythologising
  adventurer who spent 30 years "close" to a wreck, then a hoard, then a *door*. Vanished autumn 1968,
  age **34**, without his coat, owing for a barometer.
- **The truth (author-fixed):** He did not vanish; he *arrived*. Went through young, could **not
  return**, lives marooned and old in the past under a Roman name. He has tracked the doors' rhythm and
  is the one who tells Mara the **WHEN** — the Pompeii door opens **late summer 79 AD**.
- **Function:** The cautionary mirror — the man who kept returning and never *stayed*, and so belongs
  nowhere. Living proof of Mara's lie. Who she becomes if she never chooses.
- **Canon marginalia (the door-law, assembled by the reader ahead of Mara):** #1 (Ch.2 ledger flyleaf)
  *"The map is honest… it is the door that lies… the map tells you where, and something else entirely
  decides when, and whether, and for whom."* → #2 (Ch.5) *"I, too, woke in the wrong year."* → #3
  (Ch.10) doors "only open for the one they brought." → long letter (Ch.18) → note (Ch.24) → final note (Ch.29).
- **Chaos:** lists/numbers everything, even regret ("Regret the fourth…"); a pier in a city that no
  longer exists, a coat left on it (unprompted memory in the notes).
- **DISTINCTNESS GUARANTEE:** *Numbers everything — attempts, costs, regrets — in a bookkeeper's
  double columns; "the door is not honest" is his refrain; files self-pity under a number instead of
  saying it.* **vs Mara:** he *lists*; she *catalogues conditions*. **vs Verrall (his mirror-in-this-
  century):** Fenn *believed* and priced his way through; Verrall *disbelieves* but "minds whether it's there."

## SECONDARY & SUPPORTING

### VERRALL ★ — antique dealer, "Verrall & Son" (Ch.2); Fenn's estate came to him
- **Age / place:** 81, a coastal town three hours up the coast from Mara's father's house, late-1990s.
- **Physical:** "A face like a walnut and pale intelligent eyes," pared-down old strength; buttons his
  cardigan wrong on the cold step. Squares a cloth on the counter (can't leave a thing crooked — Mara
  recognises her own compulsion).
- **Wound:** No son, though the 1959 sign says "& Son" — he painted a future into the name to get
  credit and "the future didn't arrive, we weren't given one, my wife and I," and never repainted it.
- **Role/function:** Delivers the first LIVE mystery thread (Fenn's logged **ledger**); the pragmatist
  who valued the map only because it was "too accurate to be fake"; gave it to Mara's father last spring
  (three visits) and has "been sorry since."
- **Want (his single ask):** *"To be told, at the end, whether there was anything in it."* Mara promises
  to come back and never does — a debt she carries into the ash. This refrain is the chapter's spine
  and the thematic echo of the book's close.
- **DISTINCTNESS GUARANTEE:** *Flat, unsentimental North-of-England pragmatism that means things
  precisely because it won't decorate them; the auctioneer's raised finger, weighing a thing before he
  names its price; the refrain "whether there was anything in it."* **vs Fenn:** disbeliever-who-keeps-the-ledger vs
  believer-who-priced-the-door. **vs Drusus:** both are trade, but Verrall values a thing for its
  *truth*; Drusus values it for its *price*.

### KALLIA ◐ — Greek widow / innkeeper who shelters Mara (Movement II)
- **Role:** Mara's first foothold in antiquity; teaches her to *pass* as a woman who exists.
- **Own problem:** A son in the legions, unheard-from two years; she leaves a lamp lit for him nightly
  and pretends she doesn't. Refuses to sell the room he slept in though she needs the coin.
- **DISTINCTNESS GUARANTEE:** *Proverbs, always slightly MISQUOTED, always weaponised ("A lit lamp
  wastes no oil it wasn't going to waste"); never says her son's name aloud.* **vs Sabina:**
  folk-blunt vs courtly-veiled. **vs Verrall:** both grieve an absent "son," but Kallia leaves a lamp
  lit and lies about it; Verrall keeps a ledger and admits it.

### MELITA ○ — Delphi valley woman, the olive-harvest neighbour (Ch.6; may recur as village texture)
- **Role:** The valley's welcome, in one broad woman — draws Mara out at the communal olive harvest and,
  by being satisfied with a half-answer about a mother, folds her into the village. Mara's first
  acceptance by anyone who is not Kallia.
- **Own problem:** Her mother's "ideas" and the marriage they made — the ordinary grievance-fondness a
  mother earns; she narrates herself readily, which is what makes her safe to stand near.
- **DISTINCTNESS GUARANTEE:** *Talks ABOUT herself in a warm scornful flood (mothers, marriages, "ideas")
  so that she never has to ask a hard question back — the opposite of Kallia's terse withholding; NO
  verbal tic (per TIC BUDGET — she is distinguished by garrulous self-narration, not a catchphrase).*
  **vs Kallia:** floods vs clips; tells everything vs guards one shut room.

### DRUSUS ◐ — Roman trader; Valens's reluctant partner & friend (recurs)
- **Role:** The bridge into Roman society; gets the family to Campania; comic relief that curdles.
- **Own problem:** Drowning in debt to the wrong people; bored inside his marriage. Buys luxury he
  can't afford the day after each near-ruin.
- **DISTINCTNESS GUARANTEE (worldview, not a counting bit):** *He can only understand the world through
  COST — he reaches for the price of a thing to avoid its weight, converting every emotional stake into
  what it will run him ("Grief? Grief I can manage. It's the funeral wine at four asses a jar that'll
  ruin me"); never admits "I can't afford it."* This is an evasion-through-money worldview, NOT a
  quantifying tic. **vs Valens:** venal-loud, all surface, vs stoic-spare, all withheld — Drusus fills
  silence with cost, Valens fills it with a knot.

### SABINA ◐ — the woman from Valens's scandal; the late legate's widow (Movements III–IV)
- **Role:** The past Valens fled walks into the present he built (arrives ruined and frightened, ~Ch.18,
  midpoint), bringing the legate's faction to Campania.
- **Own problem:** Her own ruin since; she now needs Valens as much as she once doomed him. Lies even
  when the truth would serve her better — reflexively.
- **DISTINCTNESS GUARANTEE:** *Courtly, exquisite courtesy with a blade inside every kindness; the
  reflexive lie ("I always thought you deserved better than what they did to you. I may even have meant
  it"); never says a plain truth.* **vs Theano:** Sabina charms, Theano repels. **vs Kallia:**
  courtly-veiled vs folk-blunt.

### LUCIUS ◐ — Mara & Valens's son (b. ~year 3 of the past-span; Ch.17)
- **Distinctness (TEMPERAMENT, not an inherited tic):** the **grave, literal, useful** child — watches
  before he acts, wants to be trusted with real tasks, takes the world at its word and is wounded when
  it isn't. His father's steadiness without the fear yet. (A light echo — his hands are good with a knot
  — is characterisation, not a tag; use sparingly.) Mispronouncing Valens's old arena name as a baby
  word is what breaks Valens (Ch.17-area).

### AELIA ◐ — Mara & Valens's daughter (b. ~year 6; Ch.22)
- **Distinctness (TEMPERAMENT, not an inherited tic):** the **sensory, greedy-for-the-world, delighted**
  child — tastes, touches, names everything, finds wonder where there's danger. The "It's snowing!"
  delight at the ash-fall (Ch.31) is her essence and the book's cruellest sweetness. (A light echo — she
  frowns at bad bread like her mother — is characterisation, not a catchphrase.) Distinct from Lucius by
  ENERGY: he is still and literal, she is quick and sensory.

### EMILIO REYES ★(as absence) — Mara's deceased father, the archaeologist [name author-fixed 2026-07-11]
- **Given name EMILIO** (surname Reyes; named on the page in Ch.1: "His name was Emilio Reyes").

- **Collision RESOLVED:** was originally "Reyes," which clashed with protagonist Mara **Reyes**; renamed

## ANTAGONIST FORCES (no single villain — Outlander-style pressure systems)
1. **The door itself** — indifferent, rule-bound; "it only takes one." The true antagonist.
2. **History / Vesuvius, 79 AD** — the immovable deadline she cannot negotiate.
3. **Status & gender in antiquity** — a foreign woman with no man, no name, no papers (the grinding
   antagonist of Movements II–III).
4. **The legate's faction** — the human threat that follows them to Campania (delivered via Sabina).

## CAST-WIDE DISTINCTNESS MATRIX (cover-the-name, pre-solved)

Columns are the **primary axis** each character is separated on (see the four axes above) — deliberately
NOT all "verbal tic." Only Fenn and Kallia lead with a spoken signature; the rest are worldview, syntax,
evasion, or physicality.

| Character | Primary axis | Sharpest distinguisher (delete-the-name test) | Never says |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mara | Attention | Catalogues material *conditions* under stress; spiral-then-clip syntax | Sentimental abstractions about love |
| Valens | Syntax + physicality | Short level declaratives; understates mortal danger; hands work a knot when feeling threatens | His feelings; "love" (until once, late) |
| Theano | Register-switch | True sentence disguised as riddle, then flat chore-talk mid-breath | A direct reassurance |
| Fenn | Verbal tic (earned) | Numbers everything, even regret; "the door is not honest" | Self-pity (files it under a number) |
| Verrall | Syntax + physicality | Flat plain North that means it by refusing to decorate; squares the cloth | A decorated condolence |
| Kallia | Verbal tic (earned) | Misquoted, weaponised proverbs | Her son's name aloud |
| Drusus | Worldview | Reduces every feeling to its cost, to dodge its weight | "I can't afford it" |
| Sabina | Evasion | Ornate courtly courtesy with a blade in it; reflexive lie | A plain truth |
| Lucius | Temperament | Grave, literal, useful; still before he acts | — |

**Nearest-neighbour tie-breaks resolved (by DIFFERENT devices, not variants of one):**

Kallia/Verrall — lamp-lit-and-denied vs ledger-kept-and-owned. Lucius/Aelia — still/literal vs quick/sensory.
