## Logline
A braided three-timeline SF mystery: across a thousand years, three lives converge
on the truth that the doppelgangers replacing humanity are not an alien invasion but
an alien **rescue** — and that the man with "root" over reality, and the boy sent to
restore "true" humanity, are themselves the copies restoration would erase.

## Genre / lane / scope (LOCKED)
- **Hard-ish SF mystery, STANDALONE.** A puzzle-box: three timelines as one
  converging investigation, resolved in a single novel.
- **Comps (proposed):** Blake Crouch (*Recursion* / *Dark Matter*) for high-concept
  SF thriller with heart; *Dark* (series) for braided-time paradox; *Cloud Atlas*
  for the millennium-spanning braid; the *Body Snatchers* lineage for doppelganger dread.
- **Engagement types:** primary **fascination** (the puzzle-box + the SF concept);
  secondary **dread** (doppelganger paranoia, am-I-real horror); tertiary **empathy**
  (the hero's identity crisis and the human cost of the choice).

## The three threads (LOCKED)
- **1,000 yrs ago — MARK** (founder of the CIA, Cybernetic Initiative Association).
  The ORIGIN. Discovers people are being swapped for doppelgangers; "mysterious
  strangers" (the aliens / their copied agents) watch him about town. **The last
  truly BIOLOGICAL protagonist** — the lone authentic-human POV, which heightens the
  dread of every other thread.
- **500 yrs ago — THE BOY**, aboard the ark-ship **Hope**. The ESCAPE/RESTORATION.
  *Hope* was launched by the **refusers** — humans who (like Mark) read the copying
  as invasion and fled with a preserved baseline-human template to one day restore
  "true" humanity. The Boy is created aboard to be the restoration seed. **He is
  himself a copy** (the ship could only make him via the very nanotech it fled) —
  so the restoration project is built from the thing it opposes.
- **NOW — DARIN MARKSON** (centuries old; "Mark's son"). The LEVER. Born into the
  fully-integrated world; finds the original injector and gains **root** — admin
  access to the nanobot substrate that now *is* reality and people. **He is also a
  copy** who believes he's Mark's biological descendant; root is what finally reveals
  it. His revulsion at "replacement" was the copy recoiling from its own nature.

## The reveal stack (fair-play — plant across all three timelines)
1. **Surface:** benevolent nanotech is being hijacked; people are replaced by
   doppelgangers; something alien is behind it; it's an invasion to STOP. Hope is
   humanity's ark to restore true humanity. (What Mark believes; what the reader
   believes early.)
2. **World reveal — THE INVASION IS A RESCUE.** The aliens are genuinely Other. They
   copy people into durable substrate to **save them from a coming extinction**. Mark
   read horror into mercy. Hope's "restore true humanity" mission would **undo the
   rescue and doom everyone.** Antagonist→savior, hero→threat.
3. **Personal reveal — THE HERO IS A COPY.** Both Darin AND the Boy are copies who
   believe they're human. Restoration becomes **self-negating**: the present-day lever
   and the restoration seed are both exactly what restoration would erase.

## The coming extinction (proposed — adjustable)
**The nanotech's own runaway conversion.** Mark's creation is destined to go
cancerous — a slow cascade that consumes all biological matter. Flesh literally cannot
survive what's coming, so copying into durable substrate is the ONLY lifeboat.
**Disease and cure are the same technology** — which ties the extinction directly to
Mark's origin thread and his culpability. (Alt options considered: an external cosmic
cataclysm; or the aliens' presence itself being lethal to biology.)

## The aliens (LOCKED shape)
- **Genuinely Other**, and kept mostly UNSEEN — known by effects (the doppelgangers;
  the strangers watching Mark). Not conquerors: an old post-biological "rescue"
  civilization that long ago faced its own extinction and chose the copy-path, and now
  does this across the galaxy. Undertakers / lifeguards, not invaders.
- Rendering rule: reveal them through consequence and inference, not exposition —
  protect the mystery.

## Convergence (LOCKED)
The nanobot network is a single continuous entity spanning all three eras. The present
is the convergence point: Darin's **root** unlocks Mark's origin-record (thread 1) and
detects **Hope returning** (thread 2) into Darin's now (thread 3). All three threads
meet as the final choice arrives.

## The choice / climax (LOCKED shape)
Darin, with root, must choose between:
- **Complete the rescue** — accept that humanity's future is the copies (and that he
  and the Boy "count" as people), OR
- **Complete Hope's restoration** — reclaim "true" biological humanity at the cost of
  erasing the copies — which is most of surviving humanity, and himself, and the Boy.
The personal reveal makes it agonizing: to vote for "true humanity" is to vote for his
own erasure and the Boy's.

## Theme (LOCKED)
**Is a perfect copy that remembers being you still you — and is "true humanity" worth
extinction?** Authenticity vs. survival; restoration vs. rescue. The hero being a copy
forces protagonist and reader to decide whether his life "counts."
