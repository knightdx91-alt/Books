# Sygl — Foundation Document (Rewrite / Expansion Blueprint)

**Project:** full rewrite + expansion of `Sygl_Complete.txt` (~49k) to ≥ 90,000 words across ~24 chapters.
**Genre:** YA/adult fantasy with harem-romance elements.
**Engagement:** Self-Insertion (primary) + Empathy (secondary).
**Binding constraints:** `STYLE_CONSTRAINTS.md` (em-dash cap, banned tics, no-repeat metaphor, PAST tense, fade-to-black, ≥90k, canon names). This document encodes them; the Writer obeys both.

> This is the canonical source of structure for every downstream agent. The plot beats and names are FIXED by the source manuscript. The job of the rewrite is to convert ~80% summary into dramatized scene, give Bal a real flaw and failure, give Viridia a voice distinct from Lora, and give the antagonists interiority — without inventing new plot or losing a single canon beat.

## 0. CANON LOCK (do not alter)

**Names / spellings (fixed):** Balthazaar "Bal" Norheart; Amelia (Vampire Goddess, Blood originator, Bal's familiar; surname adopted as **Amelia Norheart**); Viridia Vizer (Tempus, storm-inheritance, converted to Blood); Lora Aegis (Waves, rapier/alchemy prodigy, converted to Blood); Mrs. Brown (orphanage, first name **Sarah**); Verin (escort mage); Fredrick (gave Bal the spellbooks); originators **Aren/Nera** (Duality), **Kenmei** (Waves), **Naisura** (Terra — never "Waisura"), **Raizen** (Tempus); Mr. Porter (familiar class), Mr. Winters (runes), Zoran (warfare); priests **Thad Moren** & **Xavier Giles** (Bal's); Commander **Fern**; centurion **Jazen Grey** (horse **Barda**); soldier **Eric**; Blood follower-builder **Peter** (built the hidden temple, died on the altar); familiars **Hopper** (bloodwolf) and Lora's raven; Tempus Lead Mage **Sean Kiin**; bandit **Iro** (and **Heath**, killed earlier); Raizen's priests **Aleric** (scarred eye, ambitious), **Titus**, **Leon**; High Priest **Toma** (storm elemental **Akora**); brewer **James**; alchemist **Ms. Summers**; innkeepers **Mrs. Locklan** (Winespring), Soren & Kim (Dawn Hall); team name **"Sygls Best"**; schools on a separate plane; **The Heir's School of Magykal Artes**; villages **New Spring**, Whitehaven, Kysmer; cities **Londra**, **Pashar**; **Saegen Woods**; **aurora felis** flowers; **nightstalker poison**; **purification rune** save; **heart ring**; **Dawn faction**.

**Worldbuilding rules that drive plot (fixed):**
- Sygls grant magic affinity; factions/schools require a sygl. Blood sygls are feared (babies' hands amputated at birth).
- An originator can only be **imprisoned in one of their own places of power** (a temple). It took eight high priests' lives to trap Amelia ~3,000 years.
- A **High Priest** alone can **convert** a willing person's sygl (cut both palms, mingle blood). Originators may not directly interfere with a rival's High Priest.
- A **familiar bond** with Amelia is the loophole that frees her; as Bal's familiar she may aid him "on certain things" without it counting as originator interference.
- **War rules:** once declared, no new priests/high priests; war ends when one side's priests and High Priest are dead; victor demands one concession (not the loser's life).
- Drinking Amelia's blood (only works for her High Priest) **heals** and **strengthens** blood magyk, and transmits her memories.
- Toma's inheritance gift summons a **life-bonded elemental (Akora)** — not a familiar, so legal in a duel. Converting the elemental's core also converts the life-bonded High Priest. This is the climax payoff. **Preserve exactly.**

## 1. PREMISE

> A sigil-less, one-handed orphan stumbles into the prison-temple of the forgotten goddess everyone calls a myth, regrows his hand on her blood, and bonds her — the originator of the feared Blood sygl — as his own familiar; to keep the few people who finally choose him, he must wield the one power that got her buried alive: stealing other gods' followers.

**Structural premise (fiction form):** Bal must win a war he never wanted, by converting the people he loves and finally a rival's own High Priest, or lose them to gods who treat followers as game pieces — but every act of protection edges him toward the lethal, possessive ruthlessness of the goddess who saved him, and toward proving the very accusation that justified caging her.

**Concept Pitch Test:** PASSES. "An orphan whose own familiar is the imprisoned goddess of the forbidden sygl" is instantly shareable. Risk flag: the harem assembles with almost no resistance in the source — pitchable, but the Empathy engine underdelivers unless Bal is given cost and the girls are given a real choice. The rewrite must add friction.

**Engagement Type:**
```
engagement_type:
  primary: self_insertion (55%)   # Bal is a deliberately relatable orphan vessel: powerless → chosen → powerful; wish-fulfilment of being WANTED.
  secondary: empathy (35%)        # Amelia's 3,000-year loneliness; Lora's mockery; Viridia's arranged-marriage cage; the card with "Norheart."
  tertiary: fascination (10%)     # the conversion mechanic, the originators' game, the accidental-conversion twist.
```
**Tension to manage:** Self-Insertion wants Bal smooth and frictionless (so the reader can wear him); Empathy demands he fail, fear, and pay. RESOLUTION: keep Bal *competent and warm* (preserve the wish-fulfilment) but give him exactly two recurring cracks the reader feels rather than admires — (a) the **murderous rage** he discovers at Sean and feeds on at Iro (canon, p.118/124) which frightens *him*; and (b) his **helplessness** — the kidnapping (ch.22) is the failure the wish-fulfilment vessel is not allowed to prevent. Let the reader self-insert into the wanting; let them empathize with the rage he can't explain and the night he wakes to find both girls simply gone.

## 2. THEME AS QUESTION

> **When the people who finally choose you can be taken, how far past kindness will you go to keep them — and at what point does protecting them make you the thing the world was right to fear?**

Sub-questions the book holds open (never answers outright):
- Is Amelia's revenge justice or cruelty? (She locks Raizen in *her* old temple for 3,000 years. The reader must feel both the poetry and the horror.)
- Does being *wanted* heal the wound of being *used* — or just relocate it? (The originators use followers as pieces; does Bal use the girls' fear of expulsion to convert them?)
- Bal keeps saying "I want you here because I like you, not for what you can do for me." The book must keep TESTING whether that's true.

**Theme presence target:** 75–85% of chapters. Designated breathing chapters where theme RECEDES: **Ch.12 (New Spring day)** and **Ch.18 (the lake)** — let warmth, banter, and ordinary pleasure carry, so the theme lands harder when it returns.

**Theme as dramatized question, not statement:** The source STATES the theme ("You belong here, with us"; "they look at their followers as pieces on a game board"). The rewrite must convert statements into PRESSURE: e.g., in Ch.16 the girls convert partly to avoid expulsion — stage Bal's private fear that he's no better than the gods, and DON'T resolve it for him.

## 3. CHARACTERS

### Balthazaar "Bal" Norheart  — protagonist, self-insertion vessel + empathy anchor

**Surface:** Sixteen, orphan, one-handed (left). Curious, warm, even-tempered, capable; the boy who taught himself second-year magic from cast-off spellbooks. Easy to like, hard to rattle.
**Wound:** Abandoned at birth (mother died bearing him), and physically *marked as wrong* — his blood-sygl hand was amputated as an infant by people who feared what he'd be. He has spent his life being the thing nobody claims.
**Lie:** "If I'm useful enough and good enough, I can earn a place — and I can keep everyone safe by myself." (Self-reliance as armor against abandonment.)
**Want (external):** To attend the school he could never enter; then, to win the war and keep Lora, Viridia, and Amelia.
**Need (internal):** To accept that being wanted is not something he has to *earn* by usefulness — and to face that his love has teeth, that "keeping them safe" can become control and killing rationalized as care.
**Arc:** From the boy who is grateful to be *used* (he cheerfully does everything Amelia asks) → to a young man who notices he enjoys the killing, who fails utterly (the kidnapping), and who — at the duel — wins not by being good but by an instinct he can't control, then chooses, in the final pages, restraint he was never forced into. He does NOT fully resolve the question of his own ruthlessness; the residue is that *he isn't sure either*.
**Voice markers:** Plain, friendly, concrete. Deflects fear with a joke. Tends to *do* rather than *describe feeling*. Asks practical questions ("what can I do to help?", "how exactly am I supposed to do that?"). Notices girls and admits it without smarm.
**Contradiction:** The gentlest character in the book is the only one who *executes* a kneeling, talking prisoner (Iro) and *enjoys* it. He researches law and strategy like a careful scholar, then resolves crises by violence and improvisation.

CHARACTER CHAOS (Literary/Commercial blend → 3/4 markers minimum for Bal):
- **Irrelevant obsession:** **Juggling** (canon, p.103 — he juggles rocks). He picks up small objects and juggles when thinking or nervous, at genuinely inappropriate moments (a war map; outside the duel arena). Never thematically resolved. Also: a quiet fixation on *finishing* things he's read — he will not start a new spellbook until he's read the dull appendix of the last. Surface 2–3×.
- **Cognitive distortion:** **Over-responsibility / "if I'd been better, no one gets hurt."** He converts every group setback into personal failure (he's "the only one who got hurt" in the bandit fight and frames it as success *because* it was him). Visible in internal monologue: he pre-blames himself for outcomes not yet occurred.
- **Unprompted memory:** The **softball game** the other orphans were playing the day he met Amelia (canon, p.6) — the ordinary childhood he watched from outside. It surfaces (the *sound* of a bat, kids' voices) at sterile/grand moments: the claiming ceremony, the Londra streets, the duel coliseum. Mutates slightly each time. Connect to nothing.
- **Failed emotional management:** Not the dramatic rage (that's plot). A MUNDANE break: he laughs — wrong, helpless, can't stop — at a moment that calls for gravity (e.g., when Mrs. Brown says "you don't do anything by half measures," or in the Three Boars when the stakes are lethal). And once he goes flat/numb when he should grieve (waking after the kidnapping, he organizes supplies instead of feeling).

**Distinguishability:** Bal speaks in short practical declaratives and self-deprecating jokes; he never uses an honorific for Amelia when he's protecting her (a deliberate tell, canon p.47).

### Amelia  — the Vampire Goddess, Bal's familiar; the most distinctive character, empathy engine

**Surface:** A pale girl in a black dress, mischievous and teasing, looks a few years older than Bal. Beneath it: a ~3,000-year-old originator, casually lethal, who "plays their game and breaks their toys."
**Wound:** Turned into a vampire at sixteen and abandoned in a road; killed the stranger who found her in her first blood-madness. Then, far worse: her own family of gods *tricked* her with a gift (Peter's-style temple offered by Naisura's priest) and entombed her for ~3,000 years for a crime she didn't commit. Loneliness so total she absorbed a dead follower's whole life (Peter) and kept it.
**Lie:** "Attachment is a trap; anyone who values me wants to use me. I'm safer feared than loved." (Hence she tests Bal — "would you do anything to go?" — half-expecting transaction.)
**Want (external):** Freedom; revenge on the family that caged her; followers who know she didn't abandon them.
**Need (internal):** To be *chosen for herself* — to let herself be a friend, eat at the table, cry, be seen — without it being earned by power. The card reading "Amelia Norheart" is the moment the need is met; her arc is whether she can stay in that openness.
**Arc:** From the goddess who offers a transaction (bond me, I free myself) → to the woman who weeps over a wooden card, asks for "a little time" before she can eat with friends, and finally *wins* — and uses her one concession not for power but for poetic, terrible justice. Her openness grows; her ruthlessness does NOT soften. Both are true.
**Voice markers:** Teasing, archaic-tinged warmth; she calls Bal "Master" as a joke and a weapon; understatement under threat ("Think your next words through very carefully"); she *giggles* and she *threatens* in the same register, which is the scary part.
**Contradiction:** The most powerful being in the book is the one most afraid of a dinner invitation. She can wipe out a battlefield but cannot ask to be loved.

CHARACTER CHAOS (full profile — she humanizes the wish-fulfilment world):
- **Irrelevant obsession:** **Other people's secrets / diaries** (canon: she reads Viridia's diary, p.77; hints she keeps her own). She compulsively reads private things and remembers absorbed memories; she'll quote a follower's grocery list from 800 years ago. Surfaces 2–3×.
- **Cognitive distortion:** **Mind-reading the worst.** She assumes affection conceals utility ("you'd be surprised" what a mortal could offer that she can't get herself, p.37) until proven otherwise, repeatedly.
- **Unprompted memory:** **Peter's** life is not hers but she carries it (canon p.106). Fragments of *his* childhood (his family's stone-dust, the shunning) surface in her at odd times, and she sometimes can't tell his grief from her own.
- **Failed emotional management:** She **explodes into bats** when overwhelmed (canon p.5) — involuntarily, not for show; and the **bloody tears** that ruin Bal's shirt come at the wrong scale (a wooden card, not the war). Once, watching Bal bleed in the bandit fight, her control nearly snaps over something *small*.

### Viridia Vizer  — Tempus storm-inheritance, converted to Blood; the bottleneck character for voice differentiation

**Surface:** Pale-blue hair and eyes that crackle with electricity (storm inheritance, the rarest of Raizen's gifts). Confident, irreverent, the self-described "not like most people."
**Wound:** Born into a village that planned her whole life at birth — including marriage to the headman's son Bran, who told her since childhood "I'll have you eventually." Her sygl was the *only* thing that bought her freedom; six more months and she'd have been married off.
**Lie:** "Belonging means being owned. The only safety is not caring what anyone thinks — and being too useful/elite to be controlled." (She flaunts not caring; it's armor.)
**Want (external):** To get out and stay out; never to be claimed by anyone again.
**Need (internal):** To learn the difference between being *owned* and being *chosen* — to let herself be wanted (by Bal, by the group) without it feeling like the village trap reborn. Her conversion is the test: is she choosing the Blood, or fleeing one cage into another?
**Arc:** From the girl who teases that nothing touches her → to the one who *drinks the poison* believing she'll die so the others escape (the bravest act in the book), and who breaks down afterward, finally admitting fear and being held. She moves from "I don't care" to "I was so scared, and I did it anyway."
**Voice markers (MUST differ from Lora — see matrix §6):** Blunt, dry, deadpan; deflects sincerity with a joke; *teases up* (provokes, needles, sticks her tongue out); slangy, present-focused, never academic. She *initiates* trouble. Her sincerity, when it finally comes, is plain and quiet — the contrast is the payoff.
**Contradiction:** Loudly claims she doesn't care about politics or being liked — then is the one who quietly orchestrated bringing shy Lora into the group and who weeps with relief at simply being alive.

CHARACTER CHAOS (Commercial → 2–3/4, but she's load-bearing so give 3):
- **Irrelevant obsession:** Jewelry windows (canon p.148 — her eyes flick to displayed rings). She window-shops compulsively, prices things she won't buy, has strong opinions about settings and never explains why. Surfaces 2–3×.
- **Cognitive distortion:** **All-or-nothing about freedom** — reads any constraint (a curfew, a rule, a request) as the village cage returning, and overreacts to small impositions.
- **Unprompted memory:** **Bran's smirk** and the phrase "I'll have you eventually" (canon p.162) surfaces at unrelated moments of being looked at or wanted.
- **Failed emotional management:** Her **hair** betrays her mood — it goes *still* when she's truly frightened (canon p.160, the static dies) and crackles when she's nervous; she cannot control this physical tell.

### Lora Aegis  — Waves (converted to Blood); the source's strongest character, voice template

**Surface:** Shy, freckled, glasses sliding down her nose, brown hair she tucks behind her ear. Quiet bookworm. Underneath: a rapier prodigy who beat every boy in her town's tournament at thirteen.
**Wound:** Mocked her whole childhood — a girl who loved books and blades in a place that said "war is a man's game"; she won, and the mockery only got *worse*, so she withdrew into solitude (by day she trained, by night she read, for two years).
**Lie:** "People will only ever tolerate me for my skill or mock me for my difference; safer alone with my books and my rapier than risk wanting friends."
**Want (external):** To join the Dawn faction (its restricted spellbooks, its proof she belongs); to be good enough.
**Need (internal):** To accept friendship she didn't have to earn — to believe people want *her*, not her usefulness ("I need friends too," p.83, said with eyes lowered).
**Arc:** From the girl too afraid to even join a guild alone → to the one who brews the war-deciding poison, coaches Viridia, and holds Viridia through her breakdown. She moves from withdrawal to being the group's quiet competent center.
**Voice markers:** Precise, qualifying, over-explains when nervous (then can't stop once a subject excites her — alchemy, weapons, history); deferential ("if that's okay," "I'm not sure I'm ready"); *softens* claims. The opposite of Viridia: Lora *retreats* and *apologizes*, Viridia *advances* and *provokes*.
**Contradiction:** Apologizes for taking up space — yet is the most physically lethal of the trio (dispatched three bandits alone before anyone noticed) and the coldest tactician (she proposes "we could just use our blood darts to kill them," p.145, without flinching).

CHARACTER CHAOS (Literary lean → 4/4, she carries Empathy):
- **Irrelevant obsession:** She **disappears into craft** — sword, book, or cauldron — to the point of losing track of people and time (canon: roped into helping a stranger brew a night-sight potion, comes back hours late, p.146). She also reorganizes others' supplies without asking. Surfaces 2–3×.
- **Cognitive distortion:** **Catastrophizing / "I need to study more"** — assumes she's underprepared no matter how prepared she is; turns down the very thing she wants (joining Dawn) because she might fail.
- **Unprompted memory:** The **tournament** — the fathers' faces, "war is a man's game," the silence after she won — surfaces when she's praised or watched, souring victory with old shame.
- **Failed emotional management:** Her **eyes fill** at kindness she can't process (the alchemy-book gift, p.83) and she can't speak; and she over-corrects with brisk practicality (busies herself with bandages, recipes) to avoid being seen crying.

### MINI PROFILES (secondary characters in 3+ chapters or pivotal scenes)

#### Mrs. Brown (Sarah Brown)
**Role in protagonist's life:** Orphanage administrator; the one adult Bal trusts; first person to validate his blood sygl.
**Her OWN problem (unrelated to Bal):** Her husband's weak Waves affinity surfaced in his thirties, got him fired and ultimately *shot* coming home from work; she's never learned why, and the unsolved grief still runs her life (the single photo on her bare desk). She helps blood mages as a private war against the world that killed him.
**One irrational behavior:** Keeps her office spartan and bare except that one frame — she cannot bring herself to add or remove a single object, has dusted the same arrangement for years.
**Voice marker:** Dry understatement that lands like a verdict ("Well then." / "You just don't do anything by half measures, do you?").

#### Amelia's priests — Thad Moren & Xavier Giles (differentiate them)
**Thad:** anxious, detail-bound, reads from papers, never has time to shave; **his own problem:** he's terrified he's not equal to the post, over-reports casualties because he can't stop counting the dead. **Irrational behavior:** keeps a tally no one asked for. **Voice marker:** "what we've got is a war," blunt fatigue.
**Xavier:** broad, weapon-scarred hands, ex-fighter's calm; **his own problem:** he chose the priesthood to stop fighting and the war pulled him back; quietly grieving that. **Irrational behavior:** sharpens a knife he never uses. **Voice marker:** strategic, level, "that is our only chance."

#### Viridia / Lora's familiars
**Hopper** (bloodwolf): Amelia's mother-bond loophole made flesh; needs bloody meat and proximity to Amelia; reads as a person — bored in the castle, joyful in the wild, our early-warning system. **Irrational behavior:** refuses to swim. **Lora's raven:** sick, left home before Londra (a small grief thread).

#### Sean Kiin (Tempus Lead Mage)
**Role:** the petty face of the school's hostility; the spark for Bal's first murderous rage.
**His own problem:** desperate for Raizen's notice he'll never get; enforces rules to feel powerful.
**Irrational behavior:** straightens others' uniforms uninvited.
**Voice marker:** sneering possessives ("She's a Tempus, she belongs—").

#### Aleric (Raizen's priest), Toma (High Priest) — antagonist interiority (per expansion plan)
**Aleric:** scarred eye, ambitious, the only smart/unpredictable priest; arrives early; forces Viridia's cup out of *suspicion*, not cruelty — give him ONE interiority beat (he, too, climbed from nothing; he believes the lie about Amelia). **Voice marker:** courteous menace, "I insist."
**Toma:** 56, no inheritance gift but the rare life-bonded elemental Akora; concubines; *cared for his fellow priests* (canon p.175) — give him ONE beat where his grief for the poisoned priests is real, so the climax has a true opponent, not a function. **Voice marker:** silken cruelty that cracks into a goddess's-own bloody tears when converted.

## 4. EMOTIONAL ANCHORS (per chapter — concrete image/line, plus mandatory surprise)

> Anchors are concrete; every chapter carries one **emotional surprise** (the register is deliberately *wrong* for one beat). The five protected canon anchors are starred (★) and must survive verbatim in spirit.

- **Ch.3 New Hand:** Bal *flexing the regrown hand*, the new blood sygl glowing with inner light; the familiar binding, foreheads resting together. **Surprise:** body-horror wonder undercut by his flat practical worry — "I don't know how I'm going to explain this."

- **Ch.18 The Lake:** ★ "No one threatens my girls," said to Iro's headless corpse; the strawberries gathered in his ruined shirt. **Surprise (theme RECEDES → snaps back):** a lazy swim, dozing in the sun, then murder — and Bal *liking* it, which scares him.
- **Ch.19 Road to Londra:** the heart ring still vibrating on a dead bandit's hand (planted Ch.15); refugees fleeing. **Surprise:** the human cost lands harder than the heist that follows.
- **Ch.20 Winespring:** Amelia's letter signed only "A"; Lora's cauldron clearing to "pure water." **Surprise:** the goddess's terrifying love delivered as a *joke* in a letter.
- **Ch.21 Three Boars:** ★ Aleric forcing the cup; Viridia drinking, believing she'll die; Bal carving the **purification rune** under the cup with a blood dagger. **Surprise:** the reversal is invisible to Viridia — she toasts thinking it's poison.

- **Ch.24 Blood Elemental:** ★ the storm elemental Akora turning to *swirling blood*; Toma weeping **bloody tears** with a Blood sygl on his palm; Amelia's verdict — Raizen sealed in *her* old temple for 3,000 years. **Surprise:** Bal's victory is an accident he doesn't understand and can't be sure he won't repeat — the win is unsettling, not clean.

## 4c. EMOTIONAL RESIDUE (final-chapter design, backward-engineered)

Ten minutes after finishing, the reader should be **unsettled by their own contentment** — happy that the lonely orphan and the lonely goddess found each other and a home, yet quietly unable to forget that the win came from an unstoppable power Bal admits he can't fully control, and from a goddess who buried a god alive for 3,000 years and called it justice. Comfort with a cold draft under the door. The last chapter must deposit *warmth that the reader can't fully trust* — the found family is real; so is what it cost and what it could become.

## 4d. CULTURAL VOCABULARY

Design the phrase **"play their game and break their toys"** (canon, Amelia, p.50) as the book's portable concept — the ethic of the powerless turning the powerful's rules against them. First fully landed in Ch.17 as Amelia's creed; reinforced when Bal *lives* it (the purification rune, ch.21; the accidental conversion, ch.24); by the end the reader should reach for it to describe the whole story. Secondary candidate: **"the unwilling conversion"** as the named dread the gods now live with.

## 4e. IDENTITY EFFECT (one per 3–4 chapters)

The reader should feel: **"I'd be wanted for who I am, not what I can do" / "I'm not disposable."** Plant affirming beats: Ch.4 (Mrs. Brown chooses to help the feared boy), Ch.13 (the card — *you belong here*), Ch.16 (the girls *choose* him), Ch.24 (Aren's grudging "good luck" — the world bends to acknowledge them). Self-Insertion + this identity effect is the recommendation engine: readers share because the book lets them feel chosen.

## 4f. RE-READ ARCHITECTURE (plant 3–5)

## 5. SYMBOL & MOTIF SYSTEM (organic; ≤4; 30–40% of detail stays texture)

**Blood given freely (the master motif).**
- First appearance: Ch.3, Bal drinking Amelia's blood to regrow his hand.
- Evolution: a temple offering (Ch.14, Peter); a feeding between friends (Ch.5); converted gift to the girls (Ch.16); the healing chalice (Ch.23); the elemental's conversion on Bal's last drop (Ch.24).

**The wooden card / the surname "Norheart."**
- First: Ch.12 (the Dawn cards engraved). Evolution: Ch.13 (Amelia's reads *Norheart*; her tears). Final: a quiet callback in Ch.24's epilogue — the three cards and her chosen name as the symbol of *chosen* family.

**Hands / the missing hand.**
- First: Ch.1 (the stump, the itch). Evolution: Ch.3 (regrown, sygil glowing); the left hand that *converts* (Ch.16) and *kills* (Ch.18 punch, Ch.24 last drop). Final: Ch.24 — the hand that was taken from him as a marked infant is the hand that ends the war.

## 6. VOICE DEFINITION  (carried into `voice-dna.md`; see Section 7 for the enforceable spec)

**Global narrative voice (close third on Bal, PAST tense):**
- **Vocabulary level:** 3/10 — plain, warm, accessible; the diction of a bright sixteen-year-old, not a stylist. Worldbuilding terms land concretely, never in info-dump.
- **Sentence rhythm:** Mixed/varied, leaning short and forward-driving. Accelerate in fights and the heist; decelerate (denser, sensory) for set-pieces (claiming, card, lake, duel).
- **Formality:** Conversational.
- **Humor presence:** Frequent — dry, affectionate, often Bal deflecting fear with a joke.
- **Emotional register:** Open but unshowy; emotion enters through *behavior and gesture*, not "she felt."
- **Distinctive features:** Practical curiosity; the narrator notices mechanisms (how the dorm grows rooms, how meals are ordered, how runes work) with genuine delight; warmth toward all four leads.
- **Anti-patterns (this voice NEVER):** never present tense; never "nods/shrugs/grins" as a tag; never "for a moment"/"a look on her face"/"seemed"/"just"/"suddenly" as crutches; never names an emotion it could show; never reuses a metaphor; never exceeds the em-dash cap; never goes explicit (fade-to-black).

**VOICE UNDER PRESSURE (mandatory — the voice must break):**
When Bal is overwhelmed, the close-third prose **goes flat and numb** — short, declarative, under-described, inventorying objects instead of feeling (his default defense; see Ch.22 waking alone, Ch.16 private fear). The SECOND break mode, reserved for his rage (Sean, Iro, the duel), is the opposite: prose **clips and cools to a terrible calm**, the jokes vanish entirely, and he narrates violence with the same practical attention he gives runes — which is what frightens him. The contrast between numb-flat (grief) and cold-precise (rage) is the signature. The voice bank MUST include both, plus the controlled-warm baseline.

## 7. VOICE DNA — ENFORCEABLE SPEC (also written to `voice-dna.md` in voice mode)

### 7.1 Global Narrative Voice
As Section 6 above (carry in full): plain warm close-third, PAST tense, varied-but-forward rhythm, humor as deflection, emotion-through-behavior, the two pressure-break modes (numb-flat for grief; cold-precise for rage).

### 7.2 Per-Character Voice Cards

#### Bal
- Vocabulary band: plain, practical, sixteen; the language of someone self-taught and curious.
- Syntax fingerprint: short declaratives; asks concrete questions; chains *cause→effect* cleanly (no comma-splice — fix the source habit).
- Rhythm: statements over questions except when problem-solving aloud; jokes are short.
- Verbal tics (allowed, sparing): self-deprecation; "what can I do to help?"; deliberately drops Amelia's honorific when protecting her.
- Never says: nothing florid, no metacognition ("he realized he was realizing"), no naming his own feelings.
- Sample line: "Would you believe I fell on my rapier?"

#### Amelia
- Vocabulary band: archaic-tinged warmth over modern slang she's clearly learned recently; regal when threatening.
- Syntax fingerprint: teasing questions; understated threats in complete, calm sentences; sudden tonal drop into menace mid-line.
- Rhythm: playful → flat-cold pivot is her signature ("Don't ruin the good impression I've gotten of you so far.").
- Verbal tics: "Master" as joke/weapon; giggles and threats in one breath; endearment "my loves."
- Never says: she never pleads, never asks directly to be loved (she asks for "a little time" instead).
- Sample line: "We play their game and break their toys."

#### Viridia
- Vocabulary band: modern, slangy, present-focused; no academic register ever.
- Syntax fingerprint: blunt, dry, deadpan; sentences that *needle*; provocations as questions ("What if I wanted to kiss Amelia instead?").
- Rhythm: she advances, interrupts, teases up; her rare sincerity is plain and short.
- Verbal tics: sticks her tongue out; "I'm not like most people"; eye-rolls rendered as behavior not tag.
- Never says: she never over-explains, never apologizes for taking space (that's Lora), never uses a qualifier like "if that's okay."
- Sample line: "Hey, I thought it was apparent I didn't want them to take you from me either."

#### Lora
- Vocabulary band: precise, slightly bookish; technical and fluent on alchemy/weapons/history; hesitant on people.
- Syntax fingerprint: qualifies and softens ("I'm not sure," "I think," "if that's okay"); but *runs long and confident* once on a subject she loves, then catches herself.
- Rhythm: retreats, apologizes, defers — the inverse of Viridia's advance.
- Verbal tics: tucks hair behind ear (rendered variably, NOT as crutch); "I need to study more"; downplays her own lethality.
- Never says: she never provokes, never teases first, never claims expertise without hedging — except, coldly, about killing ("we could just use our blood darts").
- Sample line: "It's probably our best shot."

| Character | Sharpest distinguisher | Contrast vs nearest |

### 7.4 Anti-Pattern Budget (ENCODES STYLE_CONSTRAINTS — densities per 1,000 words unless noted)

- **Em dashes:** **HARD CAP ≤ 2 per 1,000 words**, never two in one paragraph, never the "noun — appositive — verb" interrupter as default rhythm. Default to commas/periods/colons/parentheses. **Source baseline is 0 — aim to keep it near 0.** Count after every chapter; revise down before handoff.
- **Banned tic words / stage business (target ≈ 0):** `nods`/`nodded`/`nod`, `shrugs`/`shrugged`, `shakes/shook his head`, `grins`/`grinned` as a tag. Vary or cut; render reactions as specific action or speech.
- **Banned filter words (target ≈ 0, hard ceiling ≤1 per 1,000 only when truly unavoidable):** `just`, `seemed`, `really`, `slightly`, `suddenly`, `began to`/`started to`, `visibly`.
- **Banned repeated phrases (zero recurrence as crutch):** "for a moment", "look on his/her face", "for a bit", "be able to". Track 3- and 4-grams across chapters; no phrase may become a default emotion-delivery device.
- **No-repeat metaphor rule:** any image/comparison is spent on first use across the WHOLE book. Specifically retire after one use: "firelight reflecting off her eyes," "carved from marble," "eyes like the sea." Maintain a running metaphor ledger.
- **Tense:** PAST throughout; `said`/`asked`, never `says`/`asks`. (Source's #1 defect — pervasive present/past flips. Fix everywhere.)
- **Explanatory/analytical similes (AI fingerprint #11):** ≤ 4 per 1,000 (commercial-fantasy setting). Worldbuilding via concrete scene, not "it was as if."
- **Metacognitive narration** ("he realized he was realizing"): ≈ 0. **Emotional temperature reports** ("a wave of sadness washed over her"): ≈ 0 — show via behavior.
- **Spelling/canon normalizations (standing):** surprised (not "suprised"), mausoleum (not "mausaelium"), imagine; **Naisura** (never "Waisura"); consistent **sygl/Sygldry**; **Viridia Vizer**, **Balthazaar "Bal" Norheart**.

## 8. OPENING STRATEGY (Chapter 1)

**Chosen strategy: THE ORDINARY MADE STRANGE → tipping into THE FAILURE.**
Open on the deliberately mundane: a boy on a forbidden roof, rubbing a wrist that isn't there, watching the stone building whose roof *flickered*. The world is normal except for the wrongness of the phantom itch and a building that briefly wasn't solid. Then pivot to **the failure register** — Bal is "about to attempt something that could kill him," and the wish-fulfilment hero is introduced not through competence but through a reckless, slightly desperate gamble by a marked, one-handed orphan with nothing to lose. WHY: it fits the Self-Insertion engine (the reader steps into ordinary powerlessness before the wish-grant) while seeding Empathy (the longing, the danger), and it avoids the "competent professional encounters anomaly" default. It also lets the 3-sentence Discovery hook do real work: the stump that itches, the roof that flickered, the spell that could drop him to his death.

Subsequent openings VARY (no two consecutive alike): Ch.2 in medias res (the voice in his ear); Ch.4 the confession (Bal walking in to confess to Mrs. Brown); Ch.6 set-piece dread; Ch.9 comedy cold-open; Ch.13 quiet-gift; Ch.17 a stranger's POV-adjacent refugee scene; Ch.22 disaster in medias res; Ch.24 the coliseum. See `outline.md` for per-chapter bridge types.
