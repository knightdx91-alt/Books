# Sygl — Hard Style Constraints (read before writing or editing ANY chapter)

These are non-negotiable. Every writer/editor/disruptor pass must comply, and the
evaluator must flag violations.

## 1. EM DASHES — watch constantly
- Treat em dashes (—) as a scarce resource. **Hard cap: at most 2 em dashes per 1,000 words**, and ideally fewer.
- Default to commas, periods, colons, or parentheses instead. Recast the sentence rather than reaching for a dash.
- Never use an em dash twice in the same paragraph. Never use the "noun — appositive — verb" interrupter pattern as a default rhythm.
- Run a count after every chapter. If over cap, revise down before handoff.

## 2. VERBAL TICS — zero tolerance
Banned/strictly-rationed stage business and filler:
- `nods` / `nodded`, `shrugs` / `shrugged`, `shakes his head`, `grins` as a tag — vary or cut.
- Filter words: `just`, `seemed`, `really`, `slightly`, `suddenly`, `began to` / `started to`, `visibly`.
- Dialogue tags: prefer `said`/`asked`; do NOT default to present-tense `says`/`asks`. PAST TENSE throughout.

## 3. NO REPEATED PHRASES OR METAPHORS
- No phrase ("for a moment", "look on his/her face", "be able to") should recur as a crutch. Track repeats across chapters.
- A metaphor or image used once is spent. Do not reuse the same comparison (e.g. a character "carved from marble", eyes "like the sea") in more than one place across the whole book.
- After each chapter, scan your own 3- and 4-grams for unintended repetition and revise.

## 3b. "the way [x]" SIMILE CAP (pipeline fingerprint)
- The explanatory construction "...the way [someone does/feels x]" is a recurring machine
  tic across chapters. **HARD CAP: at most 2 per chapter — and STAGGERED, not uniform.**
- Do NOT hit 2 every chapter (that recreates the fingerprint). Alternate: aim for **0–1 in
  every other chapter**, so the running average is "~2 every other chapter," never a
  mechanical constant. A chapter with zero is good.
- Keep only the most concrete instances; recast the rest with plain syntax or a "like"
  simile (respect the simile cap ≤4/1k). Vary sentence shape instead of reaching for it.
- Count after every chapter; if over 2, revise down before handoff.

## 4. TENSE & POV
- PAST tense, consistent. Close third on Balthazaar unless a scene explicitly shifts.

## 5. CONTENT
- Romance/intimacy is **fade-to-black** (matches source tone). Build tension; cut before explicit acts.

## 6. LENGTH
- Whole novel **≥ 90,000 words**, expanding the source (~49k). Deepen scenes, dramatize summarized "telling", bridge timeline jumps. Expansion must add substance, not padding.
