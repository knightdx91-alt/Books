# Sygl Manga — Chapter 1 Adaptation Workup
## "The Roof and the Flicker"

Source: `../manuscript/chapter-01.md` (finalized prose). This is the panel-by-panel
plan for turning Ch.1 into manga. Prose is ~3,900 words; **target length: 22 pages**
(author decision — see AUTHOR DECISIONS below). Caption voice: **dry/wry, matching the
prose.**

---

## ⚠️ CANON — CHARACTER DESIGN (must be enforced every panel)

**Bal in Chapter 1 = "PRE-REGROWTH BAL". Two hard rules:**
1. **NO LEFT HAND.** His left arm ends at the wrist in a *soft white knot* (a neat,
   old, healed stump — not a fresh wound, not bandaged). He does everything one-handed
   with his RIGHT. The left stump is shown constantly (juggling, casting, the itch).
2. **NO SYGL / NO MARK ANYWHERE.** He is completely unmarked. Do NOT draw any glowing
   palm sigil, Blood mark, or magic symbol on him. He is the *sigil-less* boy — that
   absence is the whole point of the chapter.

**When magic happens it comes from the STUMP**, not a hand and not a mark: he pushes
the levitation spell *through the blockage in his left wrist*. Visualize as a faint
strain/glow at the stump, not a drawn sigil.

**3. HE IS THE *ONLY* UNMARKED ONE.** Not one of a few — the *single* exception in an
orphanage of ~40 children. Everyone else wakes with a sygl; he never did. This absolute
singularity is the chapter's emotional engine and must READ VISUALLY: whenever we show
other kids, imply/telegraph that they all bear marks and he alone does not. Stage him as
the lone figure — apart in frame, outside the group, the one hand with nothing on it.
Canon line to honor: *"From the roof he could not be the only boy in the building without
a sygl. From the roof he was the only boy at all."*

**Design carries forward:** "Regrown-hand Bal" (Ch.3+) is a SEPARATE design with a
left hand AND the Blood sigil on the left palm. Keep the two reference sheets separate
so panels never mix them up. (The existing `panel-bal-flux.png` and `bal-manga.png` are
BOTH regrown-Bal — wrong for Ch.1; keep as future reference only.)

**Other Ch.1 design notes:**
- Age 16, orphan. Messy dark hair, kind/even-tempered face, easy to like.
- Worn hand-me-down clothes. Carries a small backpack (compass, pocketknife, rune kit,
  a battered book `Sygldry Year 2`).
- The carpet: small wash-basin rug, dull gold thread, sky-blue border darned in the
  wrong colour (a concrete, drawable detail — keep it consistent).

---

## ADAPTATION CHALLENGE (read before scripting)

Ch.1 is a **quiet interior chapter**: one boy alone on a roof, heavy internal monologue,
then a burst of action (the flight). Manga can't narrate thought for pages. Strategy:
- **Externalize interiority** into (a) sparse caption boxes (Bal's 1st-person voice, kept
  short), (b) a few visual metaphors, and (c) body language.
- **Compress** the backstory (sygls, Posy, Fredrick) into 1–2 pages of visual flashback
  fragments, not talking-head exposition.
- **Spend the page budget on the flight and the temple** — that's the cinematic payload
  and the hook.
- Dialogue is almost nonexistent until the last line, so this chapter leans on
  **caption boxes + strong staging**. That's fine and normal for a manga opening.

## PAGE-BY-PAGE PANEL SCRIPT

Format: **P# / panel# — [shot] description. // CAPTION or DIALOGUE / SFX.**
Captions = Bal's interior voice (box). Keep them terse.

### PAGE 1 — Cold open on the three facts (the Discovery hook)
- **1.1** — [Extreme close-up] The left-wrist STUMP, thumb of the right hand rubbing it.
  Soft white knot, no hand. // CAP: *The stump itches. It only ever does that when I
  look at the mausoleum.*
- **1.2** — [POV up] The great grey mausoleum across the yard; one edge of its roof
  drawn faintly *wrong*, shimmering. // CAP: *The roof flickered three days ago.*
- **1.3** — [Wide] Bal small on the orphanage's slate roof, cross-legged, a rug across
  his knees, forty-foot drop below. // CAP: *And this spell could drop me forty feet if
  I get one syllable wrong.*
- **1.4** — [Close, ¾ face] Bal's calm, almost amused expression. // CAP: *None of that
  felt like a good enough reason to stop.*  // TITLE DROP: **SYGL — Ch.1: The Roof and the Flicker**

### PAGE 2 — Establish the one-handed boy
- **2.1** — [Action] Three gutter-stones in the air; right hand throwing/catching.
- **2.2** — [Detail] The left stump lifting out of habit to "catch" and missing, by the
  width of a hand that isn't there. // CAP: *Habit. The hand's been gone longer than I
  can remember.*
- **2.3** — [Wide] The roof, the closed grey mausoleum behind, lane running to the trees.
  // CAP: *Up here I'm not the only boy without a sygl. Up here I'm the only boy at all.*
- **2.4** — [Down-angle] Below, other boys playing stick-and-rag-ball; **stage it so a
  marked hand is visible on one of them** (a mark catching light as he swings), the group
  together — Bal alone above, apart, not looking. // SFX: *crack* (the stick). // CAP:
  *Watching the game only teaches you what outside feels like.*

### PAGE 3 — The sygl world (compressed exposition, visual)
- **3.1** — [Insert/flashback tint] A younger child's hand, a mark burning into the skin
  — green coil (Terra). // CAP: *Everyone else wakes one day with a sygl. A mark that
  tells the world what they're for.*
- **3.2** — [Flashback] A supper table; a girl (Posy) drops her spoon, holds her glowing
  hand up; the whole table leans in. // SFX: *clatter*. // CAP: *Posy got hers at supper.*
- **3.3** — [Flashback] A cart on the lane, a stranger, Posy leaving. // CAP: *By spring
  a steward came. The mark tells the world what you're good for, and the world comes and
  collects.*
- **3.4** — [Present, close on stump] Bal's stump, no mark. // CAP: *Every child in this
  house woke with a mark. Every one. I got a soft white knot. The world read it and moved
  on to a child it could use.*

### PAGE 4 — The flicker (the inciting mystery)
- **4.1** — [Flashback, days ago] Bal on the roof tracing carved figures on the
  mausoleum's top edge.
- **4.2** — [Big panel] The stone roof goes *thin as breath on glass* — through it: pillars,
  an open floor, a tall pale STATUE facing away. // CAP: *Then, three days ago, I looked
  straight through it.*
- **4.3** — [Close] The stone closes over like water; Bal frozen, stump burning. // CAP:
  *A room that has no business existing inside a sealed tomb.*
- **4.4** — [Present] Bal on the roof now, staring at the mausoleum. // CAP: *I told
  myself I made it up. I didn't.*

### PAGE 5 — The plan (why magic, not a ladder)
- **5.1** — [Insert] A ladder against the wall, far too short, ending at dead stone. //
  CAP: *No ladder here reaches it. Even the long one just stands you at a wall.*
- **5.2** — [Detail] The book `Sygldry Year 2`, open to the appendix; a finger tracing a
  line. // CAP: *A levitation holds until the caster's attention breaks.*
- **5.3** — [Flashback] Fredrick at the door shoving three spellbooks into Bal's chest.
  // FREDRICK: "You'll get more out of these than I will."
- **5.4** — [Close] Bal reading by candle in the dorm at night, other boys asleep. //
  CAP: *The one road across is the one that can kill me. So I read all forty dull pages
  first. I don't start things I haven't finished.*

### PAGE 6 — The decision (theme beat — keep it dramatized, not preached)
- **6.1** — [Wide] Bal alone, the four-o'clock bell ringing inside; nobody coming for him.
  // SFX: *bell*. // CAP: *In an hour someone notices I'm gone from the edge of things.
  Nobody hurries.*
- **6.2** — [Close] His face, something quiet hardening. // CAP: *I could sit here and
  agree with the verdict until they send me out to be useful somewhere.*
- **6.3** — [Close] Hand smoothing the rug flat. // CAP: *Or I go find out whether the
  verdict missed something.*
- **6.4** — [Beat panel, small] // CAP: *I'm tired of agreeing with them. It's the
  closest thing to a reason I've got. And it's mine.*

### PAGE 7 — The cast (the hopeful, silly gesture)
- **7.1** — [Close] Bal kneels, lowers the **left wrist stump** — not his hand — onto the
  carpet. // CAP: *I could use my good hand. I never do.*
- **7.2** — [Tight] The stump against the gold threads. // CAP: *Some stupid, hopeful
  part of me still thinks the push will catch on something. That a mark will finally
  surface there.*
- **7.3** — [Close on mouth] Bal speaks the word. // BAL: "Levimotorcorpus."
- **7.4** — [FX] Strain/heat at the stump as magic squeezes past the old blockage into
  the threads. // SFX: *hmmmm* (low hum).

### PAGE 8 — It works (the beat-subversion: delight, not destiny)
- **8.1** — [Side] The carpet lifts a few inches — and *stops dead*, hovering. // SFX: *fwm.*
- **8.2** — [Close] Bal looks at the gap of air and barks a laugh, surprised. // BAL:
  "...ha." // CAP: *It decided a few inches was plenty. It's waiting to be told otherwise.*
- **8.3** — [Comic beat] Bal, deadpan, to a rug. // BAL: "Higher."
- **8.4** — [Action] He shrugs the backpack on, folds his legs, settles. // CAP: *Then I
  asked it to take me across.*

### PAGE 9 — THE FLIGHT (splash-heavy — the payload)
- **9.1** — [Small strip, top] The roof's edge slides out from under him.
- **9.2** — [BIG SPLASH] Bal on the tiny rug over the forty-foot drop, yard and boys gone
  small below, hair pushed back, stomach-lifting height. // SFX: *WHOOSH.* // CAP: *I
  should've been terrified. I waited politely to be terrified.*
- **9.3** — [Inset, his face] Wide, aching grin — a child on a swing shoved too high. //
  BAL: (whoop) // CAP: *What came instead was delight. If I die doing this — at least it
  was THIS.*

### PAGE 10 — Near-loss (competence undercut, real peril)
- **10.1** — [Close] His mind drifts to the thought — and the rug answers, threads going
  loose. // SFX: *stutter…*
- **10.2** — [Drop] The carpet falls a foot out of the sky; his stomach lurches. // SFX:
  *DIP.*
- **10.3** — [Fierce close] He hauls his whole mind back. // CAP: *No. The carpet. Only
  the carpet.* // He shrinks to the single fact of the rug holding him.
- **10.4** — [Steady] Threads pull taut, the hum steadies. // CAP: *I can be terrified
  later. Right now I'm just a boy who believes in a carpet.*

### PAGE 11 — The pull, and through the roof
- **11.1** — [Close on stump] The stump *lights up* — not the itch, a hard hot pull with
  weight, dragging toward the tomb. // CAP: *A fishhook set in a part of me that has no
  nerves left to feel it.*
- **11.2** — [Approach] The mausoleum rushes close; Bal aims for the thin place in the roof.
- **11.3** — [Impact-that-isn't] He hits where stone should be and passes *through* — no
  jolt, light changing warm. // SFX: *(silence) — sshh.*
- **11.4** — [Interior reveal, downward] A floor swings up out of the gloom between two
  huge pillars. // BAL (thought): *Down — gently — down.*

### PAGE 12 — Inside the temple (wonder splash)
- **12.1** — [BIG] The rug sets him on swept concrete; behind, it slumps dead into a heap
  of dull gold. Two braziers burn; columns climb into shadow; a square of pale sky above
  that cannot be there; the pale STATUE at the far end, face turned away. // SFX: *fmp*
  (rug drops).
- **12.2** — [Close] Bal breathing, very still. // CAP: *I'm inside.*

### PAGE 13 — The details that shouldn't be (dread creeping in)
- **13.1** — [Detail] The floor — swept clean, no dust, no leaves, in a tomb sealed 200
  years. // CAP: *Two hundred years sealed. There should be dust thick enough to write
  in. There's none.*
- **13.2** — [Detail] The two braziers, steadily burning. // CAP: *Someone minds this
  room. Someone keeps two fires lit — in a place the living can't reach.*
- **13.3** — [Close on stump] The itch is simply GONE. // CAP: *My stump stopped itching.
  The absence feels stranger than the itch ever did.*
- **13.4** — [Wide] Bal turning a slow circle, taking it in; the low worn altar near the
  statue; he stays at the room's center. // CAP: *How does the sun even get in here?*

### PAGE 14 — THE HOOK (the whole reason to turn the page)
- **14.1** — [Tight on Bal's ear/cheek, hair stirring] A whisper, warm and amused, a
  hand's breadth from his skin. // WHISPER (small, unowned bubble): "Because it's magic."
- **14.2** — [Whip-pan] Bal whirls — the room stretches empty: braziers steady, statue
  facing away, crumpled rug. // SFX: *whip.*
- **14.3** — [Final panel, wide/ominous] Empty pillars climbing into dark that holds
  nothing he can see — but the hair by his ear is still moving. // CAP: *The temple that
  should've been empty for two hundred years... very clearly wasn't.*
- **END CH.1 →** (leads directly into Ch.2 "The Queen in the Temple": Amelia.)

## GENERATION NOTES (for RunPod / FLUX)

- **Consistency FIRST.** Before generating pages, lock **Pre-Regrowth Bal** (stump, no
  sigil) as a reference sheet: face, hair, clothes, and the stump. Fix a seed + a prompt
  block we reuse. Add to EVERY prompt: `no left hand, left arm ends at wrist, stump` and
  to the NEGATIVE: `left hand, five fingers on left arm, glowing sigil, palm mark,
  signature, border, watermark, text`.
- **Style block (reuse):** `manga, monochrome, greyscale, screentone, clean ink lineart,
  shonen, dramatic lighting, high detail`.
- **The stump is the #1 thing FLUX will fight us on** — models love to draw two hands.
  Expect to re-roll hand panels and/or fix with inpainting. For tricky panels, generate
  the pose then inpaint the left hand into a stump.
- **Panels vs. pages:** generate individual PANELS, not whole pages. Page layout +
  gutters + speech bubbles/lettering are a separate deterministic step (done in code,
  never by the model — AI lettering is garbage).
- **Priority panels to test first:** 9.2 (flight splash), 12.1 (temple reveal), 1.1
  (stump close-up — proves we can force the missing hand). If those three work, the
  chapter is generatable.

## AUTHOR DECISIONS (locked 2026-07-14)
1. **Page count: 22 pages (roomy).** We have room — do NOT over-compress the backstory.
   Let the sygl/Posy/Fredrick flashbacks breathe (the current 14-page script expands to
   22 by giving pages 3–6 and the flight/temple more panels and beats, not by padding).
2. **Caption voice: DRY / WRY — match the finished prose.** Bal is observant and quietly
   self-deprecating; keep the book's voice in every caption box.
3. **Stump itch/pull is NOT a recurring visual motif.** Show it where the prose calls for
   it in Ch.1 (the itch, the hard "pull" toward the tomb) but do NOT invent a repeating
   symbol/hooked-line that readers track across chapters. Draw it plainly, in-scene, then
   let it go.

### Re-pagination note (14 → 22)
The scripted 14 pages are the spine. To reach 22, expand (don't pad): the flight (P9–10)
earns 2–3 more panels of real peril; the temple reveal (P12–13) earns a full extra page
of dread-detail; the sygl-world flashback (P3) and the decision (P6) each get more room so
the isolation lands. Final page map to be drawn up when we lock panel art.
