## 0. CALIBRATION (from STATE.yaml + canon)

- **Genre:** Adult dark fantasy — mythic progression / street-thief-to-god. Gritty but earning hope (Blood Song / Locke Lamora texture, NOT full grimdark).
- **Word target:** 90,000 (hard floor 85,000). Σ chapter targets budgeted to ~98,000 (see `outline.md`).
- **Dialogue target:** 30–45% band is for romance; this is dark fantasy — aim **~25–35%** (crew banter + interrogation/lore scenes carry dialogue; the awakening and Domain sequences run interior/narrative).
- **Macro-structure:** DIPTYCH (two Parts across a 7-year time-skip), Part Two built as four jittered movements. Selected in `outline.md` §Macro-Structure — deliberately NOT 3-act / 20×5k / quarter-mark.
- **Emotional arc:** rough "W" — the awakening high at ~18%, a trough at Mouse's revelation, a false-win big score, the Domain despair-trough, the ascension crest, a bittersweet settle. ~8 oscillations.

### Engagement Type (ranked, from STATE)
```
engagement_type:
  primary:   progression/ascension payoff (55%)  — earn every increment of power at a cost
  secondary: underdog loyalty / found family (30%) — the few who SEE Theran; the wound engine
  tertiary:  conspiracy mystery — who killed the gods (15%) — Mouse's fate is the thread-end
```
Tension to manage: **progression** wants Theran to get stronger; **loyalty** wants him to protect the people who are literally his power source. The cost-economy (§7) fuses them — every use of divine power spends the people he loves. Do not let ascension become a clean power-fantasy escalator; each rung costs something the found-family engine makes the reader feel.

## 1. PREMISE

**Structural premise:** *Theran, a hunted street orphan whose shadow-affinity marks him as a "quirk" — the founder of a brand-new bloodline — must survive an order that erases new gods before they can root, or else vanish like every quirk before him (Mouse), but to gain the power to fight back he must become the new God of Shadows, whose strength is drawn from the very handful of people he loves.*

The forged premise's inputs (from STATE) are binding:
- **Irony engine:** Theran's power comes from mortal connection, but he survived the streets by needing no one; godhood asks him to spend the few people who ever saw him as fuel. The more god he becomes, the more he must risk the humanity that made him worth saving.
- **Escalation ladder:** hunted urchin who hides what he is → hunted quirk the Assay has marked (like Mouse) → a god of almost nothing who must grow a bloodline while the order tears at his connections.
- **Central question (spine):** *What is a god willing to spend on the people who made him — and is a power that eats the ones you love worth having?* Only the last page (and the series) may answer it.

**Concept Pitch Test:** "A hunted orphan thief discovers his stolen power makes him the new God of Shadows — but a god's strength is drawn from the handful of people he loves, and an order that murders new gods is already hunting them." Pitchable. Risk flag: "new god" arc is familiar; differentiation lives in the *cost economy* and the *domestic conspiracy* — foreground both early.

**Book 1 vs. series line:** Book 1 = origin + first crack. Ends with Theran **chosen and stabilized as the new God of Shadows**, the **Artificer God's domestic conspiracy uncovered but unresolved** (the Assay broken locally, the God's design intact), and **Utarra looming offstage** (named, felt, never on-page). Deferred to later books: the invasion, confronting the Artificer God directly, reawakening other murdered domains, the full cost of a growing bloodline.

---

## 2. THEME AS QUESTION

**Primary:** *Can a power that draws its strength from the people you love ever be used without spending them — and if not, is it worth having?*

**Secondary (loyalty/erasure):** *What do you owe the people who saw you when you were nothing — and does becoming something erase the person they saw?*

Never stated. Theran arrives at a working answer (a god who refuses to spend recklessly — hope earned, not clean) but the question stays open into the series. **Theme presence target: ~80%.** Chapters 5 (re-establish/heist joy) and 11 (bloodline-lore) let the theme recede.

## 3. CHARACTERS

### Theran (protagonist, close-3rd POV throughout)

**Surface:** At 10, the youngest and lightest-fingered of the night urchins; at 17, a quiet, precise thief who works the dark and is never quite where the guards look. Reads as unbothered, self-contained, dry.
**Wound:** Hunger and *erasure* — not abuse. He grew up as a thing the city stepped over; people who mattered to him (Mouse, others) simply *stopped existing* and no one marked it. The terror underneath is not pain; it's disappearing without a trace. (Per guardrail: wound = hunger/erasure + loyalty, NOT shame/abuse.)
**Lie:** *If I need no one, I can't be erased through them* — safety is being unseen and unattached. He mistakes solitude for armor.
**Want:** Survive; feed himself and Jeren; be too slippery to catch.
**Need:** To let himself be *seen* and *held* by the few — because his power (and his humanity) literally depend on connection. The Lie is the exact thing godhood cannot afford.
**Arc:** From "attachment is a liability" → "attachment is the only thing that makes the power (and me) worth anything" — while refusing the order's inverse lesson (spend people as fuel). Ends the book having *chosen* to be a god the hard way.
**Voice markers:** Short, concrete, understated. Counts and measures things (steps, coins, exits). Dry deflection instead of fear. Never names a feeling directly; describes the body instead. Silence as a verb.
**Contradiction:** A thief who steals to feed others but flinches from being thanked. Ruthless about exits, sentimental about a dead friend's chipped tooth.

--- CHARACTER CHAOS (Literary/dark-fantasy → full profile) ---
**Irrelevant obsession:** Knots. He re-ties every purse-string and lace he can reach and has strong, unsolicited opinions about a properly dressed knot vs. a lazy one. Surfaces mid-crisis, mid-Domain, unjustified.
**Cognitive distortion:** Catastrophizing via *counting* — he assumes any unaccounted variable (a stranger seen twice, a coin short) means the whole plan is already dead, and pre-mourns it.
**Unprompted memory:** The specific weight and warmth of a stolen meat pie he once split three ways with Mouse and Jeren, steam on his knuckles. Surfaces in sterile/cold places (the Assay's halls, the sealed Domain). Mutates each time — the third share is sometimes Mouse, sometimes no one.
**Failed emotional management:** He laughs — wrong, at the wrong time — when he's most afraid (during the awakening, in the Domain, at the reveal). It's involuntary and he can't explain it. Not climactic; it leaks in mundane beats.
**Distinguishability:** cover the name — the counting, the flat dry deflection, the body-not-feeling description. See voice-dna.md.

### Jeren (deuteragonist — the one who sees him)

**Surface:** Older urchin (older than Theran; ~19 in the main arc). Loud where Theran is quiet, warm, quick to laugh, quicker to swing. The crew's heart.
**Wound:** Was left once too. Copes by *keeping* people loudly — you cannot vanish if someone won't shut up about you. (Deliberate mirror to Theran's silence.)
**Lie:** *If I hold on hard enough, no one I love disappears.* (Mouse disproves it; he never recovered.)
**Want:** Keep the crew fed, whole, together; keep Theran from disappearing into whatever he's becoming.
**Need:** To accept he cannot hold everyone — to let Theran change without reading it as loss.
**Arc:** From "hold on" → "let go without abandoning." His endangerment is the fulcrum of the cost-economy test (Ch.18).
**Voice markers:** Talks over silences. Jokes as load-bearing structure. Uses names constantly (says "Theran" more than anyone). Never says "goodbye."
**Contradiction:** The bravest of them, terrified of empty rooms.

--- CHARACTER CHAOS ---
**Irrelevant obsession:** A running, entirely invented feud with a baker three streets over whom he's never actually met, narrated as ongoing saga.
**Cognitive distortion:** Mind-reading — decides he knows exactly what Theran is feeling and acts on it, usually wrong.
**Unprompted memory:** The one time his mother laughed; he can't reproduce the joke and it eats him.
**Failed emotional management:** Goes silent — his silence is the alarm; when Jeren stops talking, something is very wrong.

### Mouse (the engine — dead/vanished; present in memory and revelation)

**Function:** Mouse is the book's engine, not a live character. In Draft A he "got caught... no one had seen him since." Canon resolution: **Mouse was a quirk too** — a founding bloodline that never got the chance to root — and the Assay took him. His vanishing is the mundane-looking front of the conspiracy. His fate is the thread Theran pulls (Ch.9 revelation) and the mirror of what Theran will become or fail to become. Theran's whole Lie ("needing no one is armor") is a scar Mouse left.
**Rule:** Mouse never speaks in the present. He appears only in unprompted memory (the pie, the chipped tooth, a knot he could never tie right). The re-read architecture hangs on him.

### Wren (bloodline ally / lore — NOT a master, NOT an assassin)

> Differentiation guardrail: there is NO assassin-master apprenticeship. Wren is a fading-line **seer**, an equal-outsider, who explains god/bloodline mechanics. She does not train Theran to kill; the streets and the Domain do the teaching.

**Surface:** A Spirit-line woman, mid-thirties, running a failing incense-and-fortunes stall in the market — half-charlatan, half-real. Orphan of a murdered god's fading line.
**Wound:** She *felt* her god die — the day the Spirit Domain went silent she went half-deaf to the world. Survivor's numbness.
**Lie:** *The gods are dead and the sealed can't be reached — so hope is a con you sell to others.*
**Want:** Be left alone; sell just enough truth to eat.
**Need:** To believe a Domain can wake again — Theran is the proof that undoes her cynicism.
**Arc:** cynic → reluctant believer; she is the first "worshipper," the first deliberate mortal connection Theran chooses, which teaches him the cost economy in the flesh.
**Voice markers:** Speaks in half-finished proverbs she undercuts herself. Ironic, weary, precise about the unseen.
**Contradiction:** Doesn't believe in the gods she can literally sense.

--- CHARACTER CHAOS (case-study depth) ---
**Irrelevant obsession:** Cataloguing bird-death omens she openly admits are nonsense but records anyway.
**Cognitive distortion:** Sunk-cost — keeps the failing stall because leaving would admit the years were wasted.
**Unprompted memory:** The taste of the Spirit-line's communion herb, gone from the world since the sealing.
**Failed emotional management:** Weeps at the smell of incense she sells daily; can't stop, doesn't explain.

### Corvane (antagonist — mortal instrument of the domestic conspiracy)

> Differentiation: Book 1's antagonist force is the DOMESTIC conspiracy — the Artificer God's mortal order — NOT an invading Godking. Utarra looms offstage.

**Surface:** A **Master Assayer** of *the Assay* — the artificers' appraisal guild that publicly authenticates enchanted goods (the Singing Siren answers to them). Polished, patient, genuinely courteous. A civil servant of erasure.
**The Assay (the order):** Fronts as the guild that assays magic items; secretly the Artificer God's hand in the city — it finds new bloodlines ("quirks") before they can root into gods and *reclaims* them. The night curfew and the "string of burglaries" (Draft A) are the Assay's cover for sweeps. **This recontextualizes the opening (re-read reward).**
**Wound/Lie:** Corvane genuinely believes he prevents a second cataclysm — a new god is a crack that could let Utarra back in. He erases children to keep the peace and sleeps fine. He is not sadistic; he is *reasonable*, which is worse.
**Want:** Reclaim the quirk (Theran) cleanly, quietly, before it stabilizes.
**Need (unmet — he refuses it):** To see that the order he serves committed the original murder.
**Voice markers:** Bureaucratic mercy — apologizes while doing harm; speaks of children as "unregistered lines" and "reclamations." Never raises his voice.
**Contradiction:** Keeps meticulous, tender records of every child he's erased — including Mouse. He remembers their names when no one else does. (Devastating on re-read.)

--- CHARACTER CHAOS (antagonist) ---
**Irrelevant obsession:** Correct grammar in others' paperwork; corrects a death-warrant's punctuation.
**Cognitive distortion:** All-or-nothing — a partially reclaimed quirk is, to him, a total failure; no half-measures.
**Failed emotional management:** A single, private moment of nausea he masters instantly — reading Mouse's file aloud.

### Bevin (found-family elder / fence — MINI PROFILE)
**Role in Theran's life:** The fence who moves the crew's takings; a gruff surrogate uncle who feeds strays and pretends not to.
**His OWN problem:** A gambling debt to a rival crew that has nothing to do with Theran and nearly gets Bevin killed mid-book — his chaos, his stakes.
**One irrational behavior:** Refuses to sell anything blue; won't say why.
**Voice marker:** Prices everything, including feelings ("that guilt's worth about two coppers, put it down").

### The High Lord Ferrick (MINI PROFILE — mortal power, curfew author)
**Role:** Imposed the curfew (Draft A). Not the villain — a frightened man the Assay leans on; the curfew is the Assay's leash, not his cruelty.
**His OWN problem:** Terrified of being blamed for the disappearances he half-suspects are not burglaries.
**Irrational behavior:** Hosts lavish feasts during the curfew he enforces.
**Voice marker:** Passive constructions — never "I ordered," always "it was decided."

**Distinguishability test:** run cover-the-name on every speaking character; matrix pre-solved in voice-dna.md §3.

## 4. EMOTIONAL ANCHORS (per chapter — concrete, with mandatory surprise)

See `outline.md` for the per-chapter anchor + surprise fields. Global anchor-type diversity target: rotate visual / auditory / gestural / tactile / verbal so no three consecutive anchors share a channel. Signature recurring tactile anchor: **warmth in the dark** (the "warm embrace" of shadow from Draft A) — introduced literal, evolves into the cost-economy's cruelty (the warmth is other people's life).

## 4b. SHAREABLE MOMENTS (≥4 mapped)
1. **The awakening (Ch.2–3):** the stolen artifact wakes a power in him that was never supposed to exist — he becomes the crack in a locked world.
2. **Mouse's file (Ch.9):** the man hunting him keeps loving records of every child he's erased, and Mouse's name is in the ledger.
3. **The god of nothing (Ch.15–16):** he becomes a god — and learns a god is only as strong as the people who love him, so he's the weakest god alive.
4. **The cost (Ch.18):** to save Jeren he has to choose whether to spend the very connection that is his power.
5. (Bonus) **The dead god's silence (Ch.15):** he goes to meet the old God of Shadows for answers and finds a corpse that will never speak — he is nobody's heir; he's first.

## 4c. EMOTIONAL RESIDUE (designed backward from the ending)
Ten minutes after finishing, the reader should be **quietly afraid of their own attachments** — turning over the thought: *a power that runs on the people you love is the only power worth having and the cruelest one to hold* — and unable to stop wondering who in their life they'd be willing to spend. Bittersweet, not clean. The final chapter (Ch.21) is architected to deposit exactly this: Theran, newly a god of almost nothing, choosing the slow honest road, Utarra's shadow on the horizon.

## 4d. CULTURAL VOCABULARY
- **"quirk"** (author-given) — a person who founds a new bloodline; the book teaches the reader to feel its double meaning: miracle and death-mark. Introduced Ch.1 offhand, deepened by Mouse (Ch.9), owned by book's end.
- **"reclamation"** — the Assay's euphemism for erasing a quirk. By the end the reader flinches at the word. Introduced Ch.7–8.

## 4e. IDENTITY EFFECT (≥1 per 3–4 chapters)
Core: *"I'm the kind of person who refuses to spend the people I love, even for power."* The reader roots for Theran's harder road and feels braver/kinder for it. Also *"I'm not alone in being unseen"* — the erasure wound flatters no one but recognizes the overlooked reader.

## 4f. RE-READ ARCHITECTURE (3–5 planted)
1. **The curfew & "string of burglaries" (Ch.1):** mundane on read 1; on read 2, the Assay's quirk-sweeps. Mouse wasn't caught by a baker — he was reclaimed.
2. **"almost as if they were a warm embrace" (Ch.1, Draft A line, preserved):** first read = a boy who likes the dark; second read = the shadow Domain already reaching for its heir, and the horror that warmth is drawn from the living.
3. **Corvane's courtesy (first Assay scene):** reads as menace; on re-read, genuine grief — he remembers the names.
4. **Theran flinching from thanks (Ch.5–6):** reads as prickliness; re-read = the Lie that will nearly cost him his godhood.
5. **A knot Mouse could never tie right (memory):** throwaway; pays off when Theran ties it correctly at the ascension — the dead friend finished in him.

## 5. SYMBOL & MOTIF SYSTEM (organic; 30–40% of detail is texture, NOT symbol)

**Warmth-in-the-dark** — First: Ch.1, shadow as "warm embrace." Evolution: the awakening makes it literal; the Domain reveals the warmth is *life drawn from mortal connection*. Final: Ch.21, Theran feels the warmth and now knows whose it is, and rations it. Theme: the cost economy made sensory.

**Knots** — First: Ch.1, Theran re-tying purse-strings. Evolution: control over small things when everything's uncontrollable; the knot Mouse never mastered. Final: tied correctly at the ascension — mastery and grief fused. Theme: what we can and can't hold.

**Ledgers / records** — First: the Assay's appraisals; Bevin pricing everything. Evolution: Corvane's ledger of erased children; godhood as a ledger of connections/costs. Final: Theran begins his own "ledger" — the names he refuses to spend. Theme: what a life is counted as, and by whom.

**Locked doors / seals** — First: the shopkeeper locking the Singing Siren (Draft B). Evolution: the sealed Domains, the curfew, Theran's closed heart. Final: an unsealing that is also an opening of the self. Theme: erasure vs. being reachable.

## 6. VOICE DEFINITION (seed = author's Draft A/B prose; full spec in voice-dna.md)

```
Vocabulary level:   4/10 — plain, concrete, street-level; occasional reach only in the Domain
Sentence rhythm:    Mixed, leaning short; action clips, interiority lengthens; fragments under stress
Formality:          Casual → Conversational; Corvane sections go Measured/Formal for contrast
Humor presence:     Dry/subtle (Theran's deflection; Jeren's warmth) — never quippy/Whedon
Emotional register: Restrained — the body, not the feeling; opens raw only at chosen moments
Distinctive features: counting/measuring interiority; naming the exit; shadow described by warmth/weight not just darkness; hunger as a lens
Anti-patterns:      NO analytical simile chains; NO metacognition ("he realized he was realizing"); NO emotional-temperature reports ("a wave of X washed over"); NO quippy banter; NO Weeks-coined caste vocabulary (wetboy etc.)
```
**Voice under pressure (mandatory):** when Theran is overwhelmed the prose **goes flat and starts counting** — sentences shorten, syntax numbs, he inventories concrete things (steps, coins, exits, knots) instead of feeling; the wrong laugh leaks in; present tense occasionally cuts into the past-tense narration. Corvane under pressure goes *more* courteous and grammatical, not less. Voice-bank samples must include both.

## 7. GODHOOD COST-ECONOMY RULES (canon resolution — binding)

Resolves the notes' open questions and the guardrail "godhood-with-cost-economy, not immortality."

1. **A god's power = the sum of its living mortal connections** — worshippers + members of its bloodline. No connections, no power. A brand-new Shadow God with a bloodline of one (himself) is **the weakest god alive**.
2. **Bloodline magic (reconciled):** every member of a line has access to that line's **whole domain** of magic, but each manifests **one random signature strength** within it. Shadow domain (general): move unseen, near-silent motion, night-sight, shadow-cloaking, brief shadow-step. Theran's **signature**: *melding* — his body can become shadow briefly (the "warm embrace" made literal). As founder/god-candidate he's abnormally strong, but see cost.
3. **The cost:** divine power is drawn from the *vitality of his connections*. Small acts cost him alone. Large acts **draw on the life/health of the people bonded to him** — recklessness literally burns Jeren, Wren, the crew. This is the wound weaponized: the man who thought needing no one was armor now holds a power that eats the ones he needs.
4. **Growth is slow and moral:** to become a real god he must *grow the bloodline* (awaken new quirks, be worshipped/loved willingly) — but every new connection is a new hostage the Assay can take, and a new life he could spend. There is no clean escalator.
5. **The symbol of resonance** is a **one-time trigger** (per guardrail): it awakened Theran's latent line at 10 and marked him to the Assay, then went inert. It is NOT a bonded companion. The old God of Shadows is **dead and silent** — Theran is a **new** god, not an inheritor of a voice.
6. **Immortality is NOT the prize.** Theran remains killable; a god can die (the pantheon proves it). Godhood is power-with-debt, not safety.

## 8. OPENING STRATEGY

**Chapter 1 = "The ordinary made strange" + slow-burn hook**, revising Draft A into Draft B. Open on the mundane craft of a night pickpocket (Draft A, alley/drunk) rendered so precise and cold it feels *wrong* — a ten-year-old's competence at erasure — then let the street-rumor of the resonance artifact (Draft B, Singing Siren) pull the score forward. Preserve the author's line "almost as if they were a warm embrace" as a planted re-read reward. Do NOT open with the "competent professional meets anomaly" default; the strangeness is the child's ease in the dark and the curfew's real meaning humming under it.
