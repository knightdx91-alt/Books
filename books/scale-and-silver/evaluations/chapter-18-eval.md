## The two judgement gates asked for

### 1. Does the graphic scene hold Bishop voice + stay character-driven?
**Yes.** The four load-bearing anchors the task named all survive the expansion and in fact do the
heavy lifting inside the explicit passage:
- **Cold-into-heat contrast:** made literal and kept meaningful — "the one place on her whole cold body
  that was never cold." The heat is never generic; it is HIS dragon-heat against HER vampire-cold, and
  the prose keeps insisting they never even out. Motif payoff, not filler.
- **The leashed dragon:** "a broken sound in his throat with the dragon somewhere far down under it and
  none of the dragon anywhere in his hands." Restraint-tension (voice-dna §2) IS the erotic engine, as
  specified — the held-back hotter than the released.
- **Amelia's agency / "no one's to be done to":** explicit but never passive-receptive. She crosses the
  fire, names it, guides him in, sets her own pace, takes her own pleasure. The rescued-damsel default
  is refused at the most vulnerable beat in the book.
- **The arithmetic set down:** the scene's spine. The sex is the CONSEQUENCE of a character decision
  (renouncing her one survival tool), not a substitute for one.

Register check: plain declarative Bishop sentences dominate even in the graphic run ("He did not stop."
"There was no flinch."). Direct words ("cock," "clit," "cunt," "fucked") are used sparingly and land as
plainness, not shock — consistent with §3 ("more graphic than Bishop… stay in deep POV, stay
emotionally loaded, keep the restraint-tension. Never mechanical, never anatomical-for-its-own-sake").
POV discipline holds: the act is rendered from inside whichever strand we're in; no head-hopping
mid-scene; braided seams are hard breaks. **It does not tip pornographic.** The paragraph most at risk
(¶109, the manual/oral beat) stays honest by living in her sensation and her naming ("she said his name
in a voice she did not recognize as her own"), not camera-work.

**No sexual-violence content; feeding-hunger is deliberately held AWAY from the intimacy** (¶131, the
"I won't feed on you" rule) — canon-correct, and it doubles as the chapter's cleanest agency beat.

### 2. Pacing vs neighbours
