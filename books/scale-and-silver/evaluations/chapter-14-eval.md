## The Three Targeted Questions
1. **Does the father's creed land as EARNED, not a lecture?** Mostly yes. It is dramatized through a specific dead swimmer (not abstraction), Korvan resists it twice ("That's not an answer about the war"), and it is anchored in the Ch.3 river memory the reader already carries. The ONE soft spot: para 67 runs the full political calculus at length and briefly tips toward exposition-as-monologue. It is rescued by Korvan's rebuttals and the explicit refusal to resolve.
2. **Does the argument refuse to resolve (both right AND wrong)?** YES — explicitly and beautifully (para 69): "could not for the life of him find the place where the man was wrong, and could not make himself agree that he was right... they were never going to add up." This is the chapter's spine and it is executed with literary control.
3. **Does the Selwyn snap read as CHARACTER, not melodrama?** YES. It is seeded (the banked temper across Ch.3/Ch.8, the earlier Ch.8 wounding of Selwyn), and — critically — Selwyn's response DEFUSES the melodrama by naming the mechanism and refusing the exit. The snap becomes thematic proof, not a tantrum.

## Optional Polish (Floor already ≥ 8.5 — no mandatory PATH TO 8.5)
1. **Prose/register (para 67):** Split the father's longest monologue with one physical beat mid-speech (the fire, a hand on the roan-cold air, Korvan looking away) so the calculus lands as spoken pressure rather than an unbroken lecture. Same words, one breath inserted. Moves Prose toward 9.0.
2. **Pacing/mirror-shape:** The two scenes share the "loyal figure refuses to be let go" contour. Differentiate the Selwyn scene's OPENING beat so the reader doesn't feel the rhyme too early — e.g., let Selwyn talk to the mare longer before turning, so scene two enters through action, not through another confrontation stance.
3. **Em-dash:** At ceiling (10). Convert one to a period on the next pass for headroom.
4. **Echo-chamber guard (#18):** Consider seeding ONE detail in the Selwyn scene that is pure texture and NOT about hands/holding/letting-go, to break the thematic saturation.
