## VERDICT (one paragraph)
This is the book's agency pivot executed at a genuinely high level, and it clears the gate. The
central design problem — how to make a caged girl's escape read as *self-determination* rather than
tantrum or rescue — is solved cleanly through the reason-expired logic: Amelia argues her mother to
a standstill on the merits ("Keeping me alive is not a duty. It is a want"), loses the argument
because "the cage was not made of argument… It was made of love," and then *chooses anyway*, going
flat and tactical rather than defiant. The Queen lands exactly as specified — warm, doting,
devastating, never cold-villain — and the line "I know you better than I have known anything in my
life. That is why I can do this to you" is the chapter's dagger. Maren's yes is earned and paid for,
her voice holds (negatives, strangled hands, two spoons short), and "one word, in a year" is a
top-tier anchor. The escape beats (spit-boy near-miss, blood-vial parting, first sky) are real SCENE
and carry the agency claim in the body, not in narration. The only real weakness is the pipeline's
known tic — scene-end **narrated-meaning codas** that tell the reader what the moment meant after the
scene has already shown it (the final line is the worst offender). Floor 8.5, submission-viable;
tighten the codas to push toward 9.

## PATH TO 9.0 (Floor already ≥ 8.5 — this is optional lift, not a gate requirement)
The single lever is the **narrated-meaning coda** tic. Each of these scenes has already SHOWN its
meaning; the trailing sentence then states it. Cut or convert to image/action:
- **¶171 (final line)** — "made all the difference in the world and none at all." This is the
  chapter's weakest sentence and its last. End instead on the body/image already there: her own
  two feet, the hood, the turn east. Let the walking be the meaning.
- **¶74** — "It was the first act of her whole managed life that no one had arranged for her…"
  The spit-boy crossing already proved this. Trim to the "small fierce nameless thing" beat and stop.
- **¶63** — "the cage was not made of argument. It was made of love, which does not answer to
  reason" — KEEP the first two sentences (load-bearing thesis), cut the explanatory tail after "bars."
- **¶55** — the love-pulling-both-ways realization is powerful but narrated; consider trimming
  "and understood, with a grief that had no bottom" to let the image of the warring face do it.
- **Over-packed sentences:** split the longest in ¶9 (the "held her the way you hold a thing you
  have just watched fall" sentence runs long) and ¶82's 246-word paragraph has one sentence that
  should break for a short punch (Bishop's stack-then-land move).
