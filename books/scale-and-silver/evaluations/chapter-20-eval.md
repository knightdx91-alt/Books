## SPECIFIC CHECKS REQUESTED

**Is Maren's voice kept DISTINCT from Amelia's?** YES — clearly, and this is the chapter's strongest craft achievement given both are "controlled women who count." They split on the matrix exactly as voice-dna prescribes: Maren counts to REFUSE and speaks in negatives ("did not ask," "did not put her hand to the paper," "did not let herself"); she reads the world as social secrecy (carts that don't talk). Amelia counts to SURVIVE and reasons in spatial/clinical arithmetic ("the cold part had the wheel, and the cold part did not do feeling. It did arithmetic"); she reads the world as bodies and hearts ("Eleven, standing where she had slept"). Cover-the-name passes on any paragraph. No head-hopping — the four hard breaks are absolute.

**Is the casualty-list beat earned or sentimental?** EARNED. It avoids sentiment by three means: (1) it's filtered through Maren's constitutional counting rather than narrated as grief; (2) the devastating detail is administrative, not emotive ("indented to save ink"); (3) the restraint is characterized, not authorial ("her face did not do a thing a face could be marked doing"). The turn from war-as-abstraction to Sib is the correct thematic pivot for the court strand.

**Is Amelia's forbidden act canon-consistent with Ch.13 and NOT power-fantasy?** YES on both. Consistency is made explicit: "It was the exact reach she made to still a heart, the reach she had made over the drover, over the seven" (¶77) — holding IN vs stilling is the same mechanism inverted, and the text names the treaty-adjacency precisely ("Blood put into a dying human and held there by a will... She had just done the near neighbor of that," ¶95). It reads as HORROR, not triumph: "the ease was the whole terror... she was afraid of what she was going to do" (¶91, ¶133). The ease-as-dread framing is exactly what keeps it out of power-fantasy.

**Does subverting the arithmetic-device land or feel like a cheat?** LANDS. It is not the writer dodging the device — the device is named, then consciously overridden by the character, and the override IS the arc beat ("All her life she had done the arithmetic before she did anything... For the once that mattered, she did not," ¶73). Skipping the signature is the payoff of the signature, per voice-dna §4b ("the strongest beats are when she stops counting"). Not a cheat.

**Are the ford/salt-road logistics understated (not an info-dump)?** YES. The convergence point surfaces organically in both strands (Maren learns "where the war thought she was, which was somewhere up past the ford," ¶109; the grandmother gives the salt road as "the only coin I've got, which is a road," ¶121). It's delivered as gift and rumor, not exposition. Clean plant.

**Head-hopping within a strand?** None. Single POV per section, hard breaks.
