## The four flagged concerns — verdicts
1. **Adversarial forced proximity — LANDS.** The friction is real, not softened. "you are the worst thing that has ever happened to me" (43); "two people who would each be a good deal safer with the other dead in a ditch" (35). The "both — I used you and I meant it" beat (51) is EARNED: it refuses the clean-anger AND the swoon, and the reciprocal structure (his accusation → her "Both") is the anti-insta-love guardrail working on the page. The knife-pact keeps tenderness costly — "I'll hate you for making me carry that as well" (103). No fated-mates drift.
2. **Competence-respect — EARNED ON-PAGE.** Her fieldcraft: "I heard his boots change… The step gets heavier when a man's decided a thing" (29). His social craft: the flint as "a way to be wrong that cost him nothing" (29). Each names the other's specific skill; respect is shown, not asserted. The Bricc near-search is a genuine shared-competence set-piece with a real out (the cart holds exactly what a cart should hold, 19–21).
3. **Hunger near-slip — LANDS, no melodrama.** Dramatized through action (reaches for the drover, catches the grain sack instead, 83–85), motivates the knife-pact directly, and the mechanic stays consistent. The "cold clear discipline" reassert (87) keeps it from tipping.
4. **Court cutaway — EARNS ITS PLACE.** Dramatic irony (the Queen grieving a daughter she thinks safe, 121), Maren's cost, the Queen's first war maneuver (133). The invented last-message is a strong beat — it contradicts Ch.9's "no message but the one about the year and the one word" and is framed as theft ("a theft dressed as one," 131). Maren's voice (negatives, silver-counting, "the one true thing," 135) holds. Braid does not fracture — three clean sections, each with its own arc.
