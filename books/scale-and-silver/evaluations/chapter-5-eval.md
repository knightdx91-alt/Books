## MOMENTUM VERDICT — the headline question
**Delivers the accelerator — with one honest caveat.** This is the external-momentum payoff the front-of-book slow-burn needed:
- **Spatial movement + a clock:** she crosses the threshold, moves through the crowd, and the whole chapter runs on a get-in/get-out timer (she must be gone before the dark thins).
- **A real tension spike, not a reported one:** the silver-hair near-miss (¶81–89) — a court vampire catches the one strand that names her and starts to *remember a rumour about the Queen and a—* — is the strongest plot-tension beat in the book so far, and it's carried by restraint (Amelia does not use her blood; she holds pace and lets the crowd close the gap).
- **A propulsive hook:** the close (the man entering the garden she can't hear) pulls straight into Ch.6.
- **Caveat:** the first third (¶19–39) is dense interior wonder-processing before any plot tension engages — the engine really starts at the eavesdropped war-talk (¶63) and doesn't spike until the near-miss (¶81). This is *justified* for a first-crowd-of-her-life set-piece and it lands the emotional payoff (the wine beat), but it means the chapter front-loads rapture and back-loads propulsion. Acceptable as built; do not let Ch.6 open on more processing.

## EMOTIONAL CORE — do the two beats land or over-reach?
**Both land. Minor over-reach only in restatement, not conception.**
- **"Wine, my lady?" (¶41–49):** lands, and is the emotional peak of the book to date. "No stranger had ever, in the whole of her life, looked at her and not been told she was a mistake... A stranger with a tray had just proved it to her in three seconds and would never know he had done the largest thing anyone had ever done for her." Concrete, body-carried (eyes stinging, stood against the wall), and it is the exact assignment — a stranger fails to correct her for existing. No over-reach; this is clean.
- **The cold mother on the dais (¶71–77):** lands hard on the Ch.1 contrast — the warm woman who begged on the private stair is here "no more warmth in her now than in the stone she sat on," and Amelia cannot cross the floor to her. The cruelty she "had not counted on." Strong. **The only over-reach is redundancy:** the realization "her mother lives in this every hour / does this every hour of every day" is stated at ¶69 AND again at ¶75; the closing ¶111 similarly re-states "both of those were true at once and she was going to have to carry them both." Trim one of each — the idea lands the first time; the second dilutes it.

## IS AMELIA'S VOICE WEARING THIN? — straight answer
**First signs of wear, not failure.** Cover-the-name vs Korvan is still intact; the register is still vivid. But this is her third chapter and it reaches for the established signature kit several times, and two callbacks are near-verbatim:
- **Counting:** "forty-one steps she did not need to count and counted" (¶9) — the counting tic, third outing.
- **Acoustic-rating fires TWICE this chapter:** the hall (¶51, "a room that ate sound... you could say anything in and be swallowed whole") AND the fountain (¶109). The fountain use is load-bearing (it's the exposure engine — keep it). The ¶51 hall-rating is the *habit* re-firing and also re-explains the established acoustic rule from Ch.1–2 (see fingerprint flag). One acoustic-rating per chapter; give this chapter's to the fountain.
- **Near-verbatim callback:** "did the arithmetic and stayed" (¶105) ≈ Ch.1's "did the sum and stayed"; plus "very good at leaving no mark" (¶105) and "her blood woke at her wrists." These are her identity, but stated in the same words across chapters they start to read as a tic rather than a self.
**Recommendation:** ration the acoustic-rating to the plot-bearing use, and freshen the arithmetic/leave-no-mark phrasings. Do this now, at chapter 3-of-many for this POV, before the signatures calcify. Not a gate issue yet — a maintenance issue caught early.
