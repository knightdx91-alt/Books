## Canon & Brief Compliance (the six flagged items)
- **(a) Outlast, not argue:** PASS. "She gave him her silence... and did not spend a word." She has learned he cannot be argued; she withholds instead. Loric is defeated by the Queen's indifference, not rhetoric or combat.
- **(b) Named cost in one blow, shown-in-body:** PASS. Both die from the same flat cast of iron. Rendered through the hand on the face, the almond-cake exchange, and Maren's even button-count — zero named emotion, all body/object.
- **(c) Reader-only reveal (Queen dies never knowing Loric's name):** PASS and lands hard. "she did not learn why, and did not learn why... the man beside her beneath her notice to the end." Registers as the deepest cut against Loric — even her death denies him.
- **(d) Amelia AUTHORS the outcome:** PASS. She kills Loric by her own decision, gives the field orders ("Lower the standards... Then stop the frames"), and the host kneels to her. No damsel beat anywhere; she is the agent of every turn.
- **(e) Canon spelling/detail:** PASS. Silver hair ("the silver-haired woman," "silver girl"); "No witch. Only me." No Atlantis lore-dump; dragon memory not surfaced here (situational restraint held); no sexual violence; the camera cuts on the killing cast ("She turned her head... when she turned back the two of them were down").
- **(f) Anticlimax risk in the containment beat:** MANAGED, not tripped. The flatness of Loric's death is deliberate and thematically load-bearing ("It was not a great thing... It was the smallest motion she owned, and it changed nothing"). The chapter pre-empts the deflation by naming it as the point. This is the riskiest choice in the chapter and it is earned.
