# Pacing check — Chapter 4 ("The Man Who Only Asks")

- **Function:** builds the antagonist and the political stakes. Loric rendered as *reasonable/
  kind* before monstrous (tidy boots, "only asks questions," the sole person who treats unshifted
  Korvan as fully human) — kindness as the loaded weapon; the re-read poison is planted. The
  clans show as a real political body (Doran's hardliners vs Bret's loyalists; the not-arguing;
  the chief's slipping grip) and the leadership-challenge "loses either way" lever is stated.
- **Scene/summary:** camp night, dialogue-heavy, with internal movement (fire to fire, up to the
  wall). Less external motion than Ch.3's travel — a talkier chapter by design (politics).
- **Emotional-arc slot:** Korvan's temptation (his father names him "the softest post in my fence";
  Loric works him; Enna counter-weights). Rising toward the banquet.
- **Voice:** continues Korvan; distinct dialogue registers (Loric soft/agreeing; the chief
  proverbs+strategy; Doran fury; Selwyn obvious-wisdom; Enna dry-old).
- **WATCH:** two Korvan chapters back-to-back (3 then 4); Ch.5 returns to Amelia (banquet), so POV
  re-alternates. Ch.4 is the quietest/talkiest so far — justified (antagonist build) but the front
  of the book is now three of four chapters low-external-action; the banquet/exposure engine
  (Ch.5-7) needs to deliver.
- **Length:** 5,018 words — even with neighbors. Hook: "The clans would not sleep in the castle."
  Pull: the trap set, the banquet tomorrow. POV ending stays inside Korvan (no omniscient bleed).

**Verdict:** PASS. Antagonist + political engine established; Loric's menace-by-restraint suits the
register. Gates clean. Pending: book-evaluator Genesis Floor (≥8.5).
