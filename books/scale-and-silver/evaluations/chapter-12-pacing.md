# Ch.12 "The Burned Village" [K] — pacing check

**Verdict:** PASS. The war's first concrete cost, and the moral low point of Act II-A — a heavier,
more atmospheric chapter placed correctly as the pressure between the forced-proximity ignition
(Ch.11) and the power-awakening turn (Ch.13).

- **Slot in arc:** Act II-A (~40%). After the adversarial ignition, this is the chapter that makes
  the WAR concrete and stakes-real before Ch.13's hunters/power-awakening. Correct dark-middle slot;
  the emotional register is grief + isolation, the right low.
- **Subversion on-page:** "compassion deepens the suspicion" — Korvan does the one good thing done in
  Aldstkey (buries their dead) and is feared MORE for the strength it takes; the reeve asks the chief
  to keep him off the dead. Dramatized (the boy's "something's wrong with him"; the reeve scene),
  not stated.
- **The war's first cost reframed:** not a battle — neighbors burning neighbors over a rumor (the
  exact fear Loric stokes; "it did not need him, only needed to be started"). The propaganda's logic
  made flesh; ties directly to Ch.10–11's whisper campaign.
- **Structure:** village/horror → burying (boy, reeve, chief's private grave beat) → the beast
  (feeding the hidden monster off the murdered street — the doubleness) → the night (first shift-
  wrongness). Four movements, escalating to the seed.
- **Shift SEEDED, not sprung:** the sleep-wrongness (heat, the ache, the warm-river/steam dream, the
  body-inventory in the dark, the shameful hope "let it be now") plants the dragon without naming it
  — the payoff is held for Ch.14 per outline. Catastrophized-shift device used once (within cap).
- **Relational beats earn their place:** the chief's private "you were the best man on this green"
  (pays off the Ch.8 father-thread, ties to the dead mother the healers wouldn't touch); Orry's
  clumsy shame at the fire (the clan's fear hardened by watching strangers reach it too); the goat
  handover ("it cost me / it's set down") deepens the adversarial-tender bond.
- **Show-vs-tell:** 13% dialogue (appropriate for a grief/atmosphere chapter, up from a 2% first
  draft via the reeve/chief/Orry/goat scenes), filter 8.3/1k, named-emotion 0.0, abstraction 3.4.
  Village horror shown in concrete detail (chosen houses, the bundle, the mill house), not narrated.
  No scene-end meaning-coda — the chapter closes on the too-hot/too-cold hands through the plank.
- **Hook & pull:** Hook = "They smelled it a mile off... nobody said what it was." Pull = the shift-
  wrongness + the hands through the board, "the fear of exactly the two of them."
- **Length vs neighbors:** 5,032 words. Cluster holds. No chapter >2× another.
