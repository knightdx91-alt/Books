## REQUESTED CONFIRMATIONS (a)–(e)

**(a) Re-staged father-son contact — PLAUSIBLE AND EARNED, not convenient.** Korvan does not stumble into his father; he chooses the war-train, crosses a lit country to reach it, and comes down "half a mile off, on the bald shoulder of the hill above the horse-lines, where the pickets did not reach." The camp goes to bedlam; the chief walks up ALONE because he is "the one man who had walked toward it… the one who had guessed whose shape it was," and "had been half waiting for this since the drove-road." This is seeded directly in the chief's Ch.14/21 characterization (the man who rides out himself, "because I couldn't send another man"). The contact is the product of two established choices (Korvan's return + the chief's habit of walking toward the thing), not authorial convenience.

**(b) Third path is a GENUINE subversion, and Korvan keeps agency — CONFIRMED.** He explicitly refuses the instrument ("Be what I painted. Fly for us." → "No.") and the runaway ("Go dark. Wait it out… Come up in ten years." → "No… That's not it either."). The father is drawn believing "there were two doors, only ever two. The instrument or the runaway." Korvan names a third — go dark, cross to the capital unseen, take Loric off the board "at the hour I choose." Agency is foregrounded and made literal in the close: the shift is "called by his own hand for the first time and not by fear." Matches the outline's Ch.22 beat and subversion exactly (a quiet handoff; the pragmatist blesses the clarity he couldn't afford).

**(c) Dragon-memory stays situational/in-the-body and NOT Atlantis-linked — CONFIRMED.** It arrives "sideways, in the meat, called up by the beacons on the ridges the way a smell calls a room you have not stood in for years." Content: some of the old ones "had moved… under the black, choosing the dark hour… surfacing only where no fire looked." It is a facet of the already-established cold-stone memory (Ch.15+), triggered by the physical present (beacons/holding-still), no history-dump, and carries zero connection to Atlantis or vampires. Canon-clean (STATE resolved_author_decisions: "dragon ancestral memory does NOT connect to Atlantis"). Slightly over the ~80-word lore-guard across two paragraphs but woven into action, not a block — acceptable.

**(d) Bishop voice + no named emotion — HOLDS.** Plain declarative, short-punch cadence, menace/feeling by restraint. Emotion is body-shown: "his throat had shut," "his eyes burned," "the warmth of it, his father's warmth still in the wool, went into him worse than any word could have." The one place an emotion is stated — "his father was relieved" — is earned as a PERCEPTION anchored to the body ("His father's shoulders came down… a hand's breadth"), which is exactly Bishop's technique (name the read, show the tell). No emotional-temperature reports. Voice-under-pressure signature present in the dragon-days (syntax simplifies, the man "thinned out… like smoke off a dead fire").

**(e) No contradiction with Ch.18 union timing or Amelia's absence — CONFIRMED.** Amelia is off-page, "walking south to the court" — consistent with her Ch.21 self-exile and the Ch.20 salt-road-south plant. There is NO union in this chapter (Korvan is alone throughout); the Ch.18 first-explicit beat is untouched and unreferenced-as-repeated. The chapter ends with Korvan turning east toward her — runway to the Ch.25 yearning / Ch.26 reunion, not a premature reconvergence. No timeline conflict.
