## The Four Flagged Concerns — verdicts
1. **THE CHOICE — LANDS.** Costly, motivated, adult. The knife-edge is explicit and *felt*: "One word bought him back the whole of his life, and the price of it was folded small and shaking under a tree." He is warned by a father who loves him (line 33) and does it "with his eyes open." Critically, the deciding factor is NOT attraction — it's "be quiet I'm thinking… the exact register two people use who are on the same side of a hard problem." The "instinct went quiet toward her" seed pays off (line 93) without over-explaining. Not naive, not pure pull — the pull is named and *demoted* ("Not the pull, though the pull was there, low and constant and shameful under everything").
2. **BOTH AGENCY / ANTI-TWILIGHT — HELD.** She is not rescued: she chose the lion's camp on cold logic and walks in on her own risk ("betting my life," "I would not recommend the making of it"). He names her cost to his face ("it's heavy, and it has got your face on it") and she "did not flinch from it and did not argue it, which was worse than either." Adversarial-tender edge intact; no fated-mates leakage.
3. **NAME-EXCHANGE — PAYS OFF, NOT TOO NEAT.** It earns Ch.6's withholding precisely: "In the garden telling you was the dangerous thing. Out here the dangerous thing's already done." The neatness is deliberately refused — he *declines* to take hers back ("Give it to me some night when it's just a name"), which keeps the beat asymmetric and leaves a thread for later. Strong.
4. **SHOW-VS-TELL — mostly clean, with the standing exception.** Suspicion is dramatized (Wren, the ford eavesdrop, the father). BUT the scene-end narrated-meaning coda crept in twice: line 123 ("understood that he had just done the thing that could not be undone… because it was the first choice of his whole managed shapeless life") and line 141 (the chapter-closer explaining that shape no longer matters, "only what he would carry, and where, and against how much"). Line 141 also names emotion outright: "He was more afraid than he had ever been. He was also… steadier." Plus line 33's proleptic authorial intrusion ("not knowing, then, that in twenty-four hours…"). These are telling, and they are the exact tic the book flags.
