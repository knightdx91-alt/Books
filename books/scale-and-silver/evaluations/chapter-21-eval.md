## CONFIRM ITEM 1 — the new father-son scene (voice / POV / Amelia agency / integration)
**HOLDS on all counts.**
- **Bishop voice:** plain declarative with short punches and menace-by-restraint — "The chief did not
  hurry the last of it. He never had."; "no fear in his face, which was its own kind of respect." The
  chief speaks in his card (sideways via land/weather, verdict after the pause, no apology). Clean comp match.
- **Deep Korvan POV:** anchored throughout — "Korvan watched the whole weather of the man change";
  "Korvan felt the air around her change." Single POV, no head-hop, hard break into the crossing.
- **Amelia is NOT a damsel:** her agency is foregrounded before she says a word. "Korvan… knew that if
  she wanted them there would be four men on the stones and no one left to carry the tale," and the
  chief names it to her face — "You could. I can feel you thinking it… You won't. …you don't spend
  blood you'll only pay interest on." She is shown *choosing* restraint (the same choice she makes with
  the three hunters at the crossing). Full agency, not rescued-passive.
- **Integration / pacing:** seamless. The chief's refusal-that-is-help ("go up… Above the ford the
  driven-out use a shelf… Cross there") is the direct mechanical cause of the exposed-crossing ambush
  that follows — the two scenes lock. Net add ≈ 460 words (5,141 → 5,600), in band with neighbors
  (Ch.19 6,421 / Ch.20 5,358), no >2× disparity. It does NOT sag the hinge: it adds a *second* rupture
  (father-son) that rhymes with the main one (Amelia-Korvan) and pays off the Ch.14 argument, so it
  escalates rather than delays. Emotional slot not overrun — the torn-apart still lands as the chapter's
  peak.

## CONFIRM ITEM 2 — the "did not do things that came to nothing" echo → **KEEP (intentional resonance)**
The chief: "Looking again was a hand reached out, and **he did not do things that came to nothing.**
Korvan understood, watching him, where Amelia had learned it, though the two had never met until this
road." Amelia (close): "…that **she did not do things that came to nothing.** Looking up was a hand
reached out for a thing that was not going to be there. Now she did it with her whole self."

**Verdict: KEEP as deliberate father–Amelia mirror.** It is load-bearing, not a tic:
- Korvan **explicitly notices** the mirror in-text and is himself the bridge between two people who
  "never met until this road" — that noticing is the point; it seats his two anchors on a shared creed.
- The phrasing **varies** (he/she) and the two uses do opposite emotional work — the chief's is a wall
  (won't look back), Amelia's is its breaking (does the futile thing "with her whole self"). That
  inversion is the payoff, not repetition.
- It is NOT the style-gate failure mode: `style_check` stays clean, and the only other manuscript hit
  for "came to nothing" is Ch.10's *different* construction (the chief on a rumor that "came to
  nothing"). This is a distinct idiom used twice on purpose.
- **One housekeeping note (non-blocking):** because voice-dna retired verbatim cross-chapter phrases,
  log this as a SANCTIONED deliberate mirror in `feedback/style-flags.md` / the korvan ledger so a later
  voice-wear pass doesn't mistake it for calcification.
