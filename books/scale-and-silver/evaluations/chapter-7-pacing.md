# Ch.7 "Heads I Win" [A] — pacing check

**Verdict:** PASS. The TP1 detonation — correctly the fastest, highest chapter of the front act.

- **Slot in arc:** TP1 (~24%). After the Ch.5 rapture and Ch.6 slow-burn, this is the turn the
  first act has been loading. The whole book pivots here: Amelia's wish (to be seen) becomes the
  catastrophe; the treaty cracks; the war is set. Correct high point for the slot — the emotional
  low/terror peak of Act I.
- **Scene vs summary:** ~95% continuous real-time scene (single unbroken set-piece in the hall).
  The two short analytic passages (the treaty logic; the chief's trap) are load-bearing for TP1
  clarity and are kept brief and grounded in Amelia's watching, not narrator lecture.
- **Escalation curve:** builds without sag — re-entry (want) → Loric's soft approach → the hood/
  silver reveal → the room turning by kind → the Queen's mask → the "one lie could save it" beat →
  Loric's unanswerable question → the room fracturing → the blood-magyk restraint (agency) →
  disavowal-as-rescue → walk-out. Each beat raises stakes; no plateau.
- **Tempo variety:** long dread-interiority is broken by clipped dialogue (Loric's questions, the
  Queen's command) and by short hammer-sentences ("Only one. There were no others." / "No one told
  him he was wrong."). Not the single meditative tempo that dinged Ch.6.
- **Dialogue share:** lower than Ch.6 (correct — this is action/spectacle, not a two-hander), but
  Loric's lines carry the menace and the Queen's carry the gut-punch.
- **Hook & pull:** Hook = "She should have gone straight up." Pull = the war beginning "without a
  sound, to turn" + Loric's pity (a thread, not a bow).
- **Length vs neighbors:** 5,004 words. Cluster: Ch.1–6 = 5081/5014/5043/5021/5024/5150. On-model;
  no chapter >2× another.
- **Not-a-victim guardrail:** honored on-page — the blood-magyk wakes and she *chooses* restraint
  (would prove Loric's point); strength shown as decision, not rescue.

No fix needed before finalize.
