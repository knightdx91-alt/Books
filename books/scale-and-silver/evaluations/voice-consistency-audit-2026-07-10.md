# Whole-Book Voice-Consistency Audit — *A Bond of Scale and Silver*
**Auditor:** book-evaluator (voice pass only) | **Date:** 2026-07-10
**Scope:** All 29 chapters, 151,724 words. NOT a scoring or continuity pass — voice consistency
and POV differentiation only. Comp: Anne Bishop, *The Black Jewels Trilogy* (per `voice-dna.md`).

**Method:** Ch.1 (Amelia benchmark) and Ch.3 (Korvan benchmark) read in full. Deep comparative
sampling — opening + mid-scene — of Ch.5, 7, 11, 13, 15, 18, 22, 23, 24, 25, 26, 27, 28, 29,
plus targeted mechanical greps across the whole manuscript for the retired phrases and the
migrating devices.

---

## OVERALL VERDICT: **YES — consistent and well-differentiated**, with one managed convergence to watch.

The Bishop register holds cleanly from Ch.1 to Ch.29. Amelia and Korvan are distinct on the page
every time I sampled — the cover-the-name test passes at narration level, not just dialogue.
Supporting-cast speech signatures (Loric, the Chief, Selwyn, Maren, the Queen) are stable across
every appearance sampled. There are **no CRITICAL findings.** The single systemic thing worth an
editor's eye is a *deliberate* voice convergence between the leads in the back half — Amelia's
"the sum / arithmetic" and acoustic-room-reading devices migrate into Korvan's POV — which the
prose explicitly owns each time ("her kind of arithmetic," "It was the fountain again"). It reads
as earned theme, not accidental bleed, but it is dense enough to flag as a WARNING.

---

## 1. GLOBAL BISHOP REGISTER — HOLDS (Ch.1 → Ch.29)

Plain-declarative default, deep POV, emotion-in-the-body, menace-by-restraint: all present and
stable across the arc.

- **Restraint-carried menace** is intact end to end. Loric's Ch.7 "Whose are you, girl? It's a
  simple thing" and his Ch.28 "You could still turn round. No one on this hill would stop you"
  are the quiet-threat Bishop register unchanged across 21 chapters.
- **Emotion shown in the body** holds even at the climax: Ch.28 "it cost her nothing to hold it
  now, because there was nothing left in her to spend on holding it"; Ch.29 the beeswax-and-cold-
  stone smell doing the grief-work with no feeling-word attached.
- **Short-punch-after-long-build** rhythm is live throughout (Ch.27 opens "They settled it in the
  last of the dark, and it took nine words.").

### Front-to-back figurative drift — VERDICT: **EARNED, not voice-thinning.**
The measured downward trend (early ~3–4 fig/1k → back half ~1–1.3/1k) reads as intentional
tightening, not a bare/thinned voice. The back-half chapters (22, 25, 27, 28, 29) are plainer
because the *content* has moved into war, dragon-dissociation, and Amelia's clinical-under-overload
register — exactly the two conditions `voice-dna.md` says should *flatten* the prose (Amelia numbs;
Korvan-as-dragon loses the man). The figurative language that remains is pure Bishop-plain ("hills
came one behind another like grey water with no far shore," Ch.22; "the year came apart in his
chest," Ch.26) and the body-emotion never drops out. This is deceleration matched to the darkening
story, not a loss of voice. No chapter sampled drifts OFF-comp toward purple/ornamental, and none
reads mechanically under-felt. The one place the plainness is most exposed — the dragon-only
stretch of Ch.22 — is deliberately de-peopled and still lands emotion through the body ("the man
buried far down under the animal cared and could do nothing"). Keep it.

## 2. POV DIFFERENTIATION — STRONG (the big one)

**Amelia** (Ch.1, 5, 7, 13, 24, 28, 29): counting/measuring, acoustic-reading, arithmetic-of-cost,
clinical control that *flattens further* under overload. Benchmarks fully intact:
- Ch.5 "forty-one steps she did not need to count and counted"; Ch.7 "the cold clear part that
  went on working while the rest of her fell"; Ch.28 "She counted the knot... It was the thing her
  body did when there was too much."
- **Voice EVOLUTION done right:** the acoustic device changes *meaning* across the arc exactly as
  `voice-dna.md` §4b demands. Ch.1 the locked room "return[s] a whisper at four parts in ten" (a
  cage measured for fear of being overheard); Ch.29 the coronation hall is "built so that nothing
  said in it could ever be a secret again" (the same device, inverted meaning). This is the
  strongest single proof the voice was developed, not looped.

**Korvan** (Ch.3, 11, 15, 18, 22, 25, 27): body-first, fire/craft opinion, deflecting dry humor,
syntax that fragments under overload, the dangerous-undertone/tender-action split.
- Ch.25 "He knew it the way he had known the third door on the hill, in the meat before the head"
  — cognition located in the body, unmistakably his.
- Deflecting humor survives to the reunion (Ch.26 "I have the whole eastern sky for a clock... I
  win.") and correctly *thins* in the dragon/crisis beats (Ch.22).
- Dangerous-devoted split intact: Ch.26/27 his actions are tender (palm-up offering, "Come out of
  the wet") while the narration carries the cold undertow ("however this ends, and it ends badly,
  our kind always ends badly").

**BRAIDED CHAPTERS (11, 25, 26, 27, 28) — no destructive bleed.** In the hard-section-break
braids the two heads stay separable. In Ch.11 the same scene has Amelia acoustic-reading in
*dialogue* ("I heard his boots change... The step gets heavier when a man's decided a thing")
while Korvan narrates body-first — a clean contrast inside one page. Ch.26 (2nd explicit scene,
the highest bleed risk) keeps Korvan body-led and Amelia sum-led even in intimacy.

See §3 for the one convergence that is deliberate but worth watching.

## 3. SIGNATURE-DEVICE CALCIFICATION vs CONVERGENCE

The mechanical `voice_wear_check.py` passed; qualitatively the devices still feel **alive** (they
change meaning rather than repeat — see the Ch.1→29 acoustic evolution above; Amelia counts
*carriages* in Ch.1, *ways out of a square* in Ch.23, *standards and quiet men* in Ch.28). The
retired phrase **"never once" is clean** — zero occurrences in all 29 chapters. Good.

**The one systemic finding — WARNING: the "sum / arithmetic" device has diffused across POVs.**
Amelia's cost-arithmetic framing appears in Korvan's own narration repeatedly in the back half:
- Ch.22 "He did the sum then. Her sum. He made himself do it the cold way, the way she would have."
- Ch.25 "he had known her well enough now to walk the whole cold sum of it in the space of a breath"
- Ch.27 "he... had done her kind of arithmetic against his own grain"

This is *mostly a feature*: every marquee instance is explicitly flagged as **borrowed from her**,
and the convergence is the stated theme ("the two of them thought the same way," Ch.22; "the same
trick in them," Ch.25). The falls-as-fountain acoustic reframing in Korvan's Ch.26 POV ("It was
the fountain again") is the same owned borrowing. Treat as earned, but note two watch-points for
any future line pass:
- **Spot-check Ch.21** (highest raw density of sum/count language in a Korvan chapter). On the read
  it is benign — nearly every hit is Korvan *observing Amelia* count, or Amelia's dialogue ("the one
  outcome I will not do the arithmetic for"), not Korvan's own voice adopting the device. No fix
  needed; logged so a future editor doesn't "correct" it blindly.
- **Guard Korvan's own dominant devices** (fire/craft, body-first cognition) stay *primary* in his
  chapters so he never becomes a vessel for her cognition. In the sample they remain primary — keep
  it that way in any Book-Two carryover.

**Minor diffusion — MINOR:** "counting when anxious" now appears for Amelia *and* Maren in the same
chapter (Ch.23: Amelia "counted the ways out of the square"; Maren "counted the pikes... her hands
did not do well empty"). This is *within* Maren's voice card (she counts/hoards when afraid) and the
two are still separable by their surrounding markers (Amelia = acoustic + arithmetic-of-cost; Maren
= negatives + idle-hands), so cover-the-name still passes. Just note the counting *gesture* is now
closer to house-style than to Amelia's exclusive fingerprint.

## 4. CHARACTER-VOICE CONSISTENCY IN DIALOGUE — CONSISTENT

- **Loric** (Ch.3, 7, 23, 28): mild-courtesy menace holds every appearance — disclaims agency,
  phrases accusation/threat as a reasonable question, never a direct demand. Ch.3 "I only ask
  because it seems no one else does"; Ch.7 "I'd rather be troublesome than troubled"; Ch.28 "I'd
  see to it. You've done enough today." The "There she is" bookend (Ch.7 → Ch.28) is a deliberate,
  effective echo, not a tic.
- **The Chief** (Ch.3, 15): answers sideways via weather/land, short verdicts after long pauses.
  Ch.3 "Wind's backing round east... there's no use you and me standing here arranging the weather."
  Consistent and distinct from Loric's room-soft evasion.
- **Selwyn** (Ch.3, 11): obvious-as-hard-won-wisdom + object-hoarding intact ("Here's the thing
  about that fire... Some are better than others"; the coat of "things nobody else wanted").
- **Maren** (Ch.23): speaks/thinks in negatives and double-negatives ("She told herself she would
  not find it here... She had told herself a great many things... that had turned out not to be so").
  On card.
- **The Queen** (Ch.1): warm/unguarded to Amelia, the command dropped ("I made you out of nothing
  and love and spite"). On card. (Her world-facing imperial register is referenced not staged in
  the chapters sampled — consistent with the outline; no wobble seen.)

## 5. TONAL / REGISTER MODULATION — HELD INTENTIONALLY

The half-notch court-formal vs road-plain split is present and controlled, not random. Road/mountain
chapters run plainer and rougher (Ch.3 fire-talk, Ch.18 the hare fight, Korvan's fragments); court
and ceremonial chapters run half a notch more formal and measured (Ch.23 the list-reading, Ch.29
coronation). The Ch.29 hall register is audibly more elevated than the Ch.18 shieling register while
staying the same voice — deliberate modulation, correctly executed.
