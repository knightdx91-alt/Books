## The Five Special Checks
1. **Recognition, not insta-love — PASS (strong).** Attraction is explicitly recognition-first: "Something in him leaned toward her that had nothing to do with wanting anything from her… the whole animal fact of him turning toward the one warm thing in a dark field." No fated-mates, no destiny, no swoon. Guardrail 1 held.
2. **Concealment holds — PASS.** He reads the silver hair as merely fair ("silver-fair… the way an old coin is silver"), lands on "someone's daughter," and never approaches vampire/Queen's-daughter. The strong tells (treaty word-for-word, six languages, "watched from far off," "a room I wasn't the secret of," the wrist scald, sure-footed in the black) are consistent with a high-value hidden person, but Korvan is exhausted, incurious by choice ("I've stopped being greedy for other people's answers"), and has no frame in which a vampire sits alone in a garden. Plausible. **Minor risk:** "the treaty, word for word, and six languages" is the sharpest tell; a suspicious reader could argue a hill-clan boy might at least *wonder*. His decided incuriosity covers it — but see fix #3.
3. **POV discipline — PASS, one minor blemish.** Stays inside Korvan throughout; Loric is rendered only as Korvan can perceive him (the nod, the stepping-back). The one wobble is the future-narrator intrusion: "He did not yet know what that quiet was going to cost him." That is knowledge Korvan cannot have — a retrospective-narrator flourish, Bishop-tolerable but a small break from strict deep-close-third. Flag, not fail.
4. **Loric = reader-dread vs Korvan's misread — PASS (excellent).** Reader knows Loric from Ch.4 as the patient war-maker; the "grave courteous nod… a man you know slightly and mean to know better" is chilling precisely because Korvan explains it away ("Someone crossing to another door") while his body won't obey ("his stomach did not uncurl"). Dramatic irony fully armed; the fountain seed (Loric *sees*, never *hears*) is honored on the page. This is the chapter's best structural move.
5. **Voice-wear / distinctness — PASS.** Korvan stays body-first, craft-framed ("filed it the way he filed a thing built well"), deflective ("I'm a strange everything. Ask the marshal"). Amelia stays measured/clinical with flat certainties ("It's the true way," "It's a specialty," "You learn the shape of everything and the weight of nothing"). No retired phrases ("never once" absent); "clan of one" is the deliberate seed, not a tic. Korvan's fire/craft opinion fires once (within cap). No calcification.
