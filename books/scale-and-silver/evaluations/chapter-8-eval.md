## One-paragraph verdict
This is a near-excellent Act-I-close: a decompression chapter that does the hardest thing in the book so far — it makes the war a *bureaucratic tragedy* rather than a romantic doom, exactly per foundation §0 rule 3 and Irony Engine #3. The chief's "bearable door" logic is airtight, in-character (proverbs, weather, long pauses, *knows the bond was clean and buries Korvan under the war to protect him*), and dramatizes "defeat dressed as resolve" without ever stating it as a slogan. The relief-then-shame engine is the chapter's triumph: honest, disobedient, human, never authorial — Korvan's relief "did not obey him," and that is the body-rebel beat the whole voice-spec is built to produce. The Ch.6/7 re-reading deepens rather than recaps (the name-reversal is a close-the-book sentence), and cover-the-name integrity holds — he learns *what she is* only now. Two things keep the floor honest: (1) a real POV bleed at para 105 where the narration reaches into Amelia's Ch.7 experience, self-flagged but omniscient; and (2) a reflective loop across the final third that restates the same relief/shame/carry-it-alone beat four to five times, softening pace and the chapter-end pull. Both are line-level fixes.
