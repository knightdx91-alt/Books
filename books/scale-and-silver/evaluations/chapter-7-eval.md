## Verdict (one paragraph)
This is the book's first true detonation and it lands. The exposure works exactly on the engine
the foundation demands — Loric produces no proof, only an unanswerable public question, and the
moon-silver hair plus the room's own acoustics do the rest ("proof was beside the point... all he
had to do was ask the question out loud and stand back and let the silence answer it"). The
not-a-victim mandate is honored at the one place it could have failed: her blood magyk wakes and
she *chooses* restraint for a motivated reason (using it "would prove every word the grey man had
been too careful to say"), and that choice is framed as the most deliberate act of her life, not
convenience. The Queen's warmth-to-mask turn is devastating and in character — "I don't know the
girl" answered by "someone will bring the girl to me," the disavowal and the rescue folded into
the same four words. Loric gets war structurally without a cheap win (a hill-clan man made the
accusation, so the trap is the chief's), and his closing pity reads as earned grief-horror, not a
tell. Genesis Floor is 8.5 (Originality/Theme/World-building hold the floor; all three are genre-
strong, not weak). PASS. The only real notes are minor POV-discipline loosenings and light voice-
wear on the arithmetic/counting register — polish, not surgery.

## Answers to the five flagged questions
1. **Perception, no proof — plausible?** Yes. The mechanism is explicit and airtight: the hair is
   the one uncounterable signifier ("Only one kind of thing was born with the moon in its hair"),
   and the room's acoustics (planted in Ch.5/6 as the fountain's inverse) are weaponized — "the
   room itself was on his side." The detonation is carried by *silence answering*, not evidence.
2. **Not-a-victim / motivated restraint?** PASS and this is the chapter's best structural save.
   The magyk "woke now, unasked" and "most of the ways it went ended with bodies on the floor" —
   she is established as lethal, then *chooses* to hold it because releasing it would confirm
   Loric. Agency from power, not passivity. One soft spot: the closing "Hands closed on her arms"
   risks a led-out beat, but it's cushioned (gentle house guards, she is *summoned* to her mother,
   "her legs had decided to work again") and follows the agency beat, so it reads as her walking
   toward the reckoning, not being dragged. Keep an eye on it; do not let it tip.
3. **The Queen's disavowal-as-rescue?** Lands, devastating, in character. The warmth "simply going
   out of it, feature by feature, like heat leaving a stone" is love-as-control made visible, and
   "bring the girl to me" is the rescue hidden inside the disavowal "where only the two of them
   would ever find it." Exactly the foundation's Rosalia.
4. **Villain integrity?** Intact. War is structurally forced (a hill-clan man made the charge → the
   chief is trapped between war and a leadership challenge). Loric is not "given" a win; the room's
   own logic collects for him. His pity is earned per §5/§ irony engine (grief-horror, not
   gloating) — "its absence was the worst thing his face could have done."
5. **POV discipline / voice-wear.** Two minor loosenings (see below). Voice distinct from Korvan.

## POV-discipline flags (the two loosenings)
1. **Final line** — "in a hundred rooms across the great house, the machinery of a war... had
   already begun, without a sound, to turn." Amelia cannot perceive a hundred rooms. This is a
   graceful chapter-ender but it steps a half-inch outside strict close-third into near-omniscient
   summary. It's the exact "narrated-meaning scene-end" tic the CLAUDE.md front-50 note warns
   about. Acceptable as a stylistic close, but it IS a loosening — decide deliberately.
2. **Retrospective flash** — "an expression she would puzzle over for years and only understand
   much later, when she had learned what he had lost and how." Legal in close-third-past (the
   narrating self knows the future), but combined with #1 it softens the "strict close-third, no
   bleed" claim. One such flash per chapter is fine; two in the same closing movement is the max.
