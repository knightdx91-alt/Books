## FRONT-OF-BOOK PACING CALL (straight answer, as requested)
**The front is running slow, and Ch.4 is where the risk becomes real — but it's fixable without restructuring.**
- Chapters 1, 2, and 4 are all low-external (interior/relational), and Ch.4 is the quietest of the four AND the second consecutive Korvan interiority chapter with the same structural skeleton. Three of the first four chapters ask the reader to stay for voice and character rather than event. That is survivable in adult romantasy — the voice is strong enough to carry it — but it is at the ceiling of what the front can bear before a Casual/Devourer reader stalls, and Ch.4 currently sits *at* that ceiling rather than under it.
- **Does the antagonist-build earn it?** The *content* earns it — Loric is the engine of Ch.5–7 and this chapter loads that gun. But the chapter earns it at the wrong *length and repetition*: the quadruple menace-restatement means the reader pays the "quiet chapter" tax twice. A leaner Ch.4 (cut two of the four explanations, add one physical/external beat) would earn its keep cleanly.
- **The call:** Do NOT add a fourth quiet chapter. Keep this one, but make it the leanest version of itself, and make sure **Ch.5 opens on external momentum** (Amelia entering the banquet is the natural accelerator — spend it). If Ch.5–7 land the banquet/exposure hard, the front reads as a deliberate slow-burn build, not a sag. If Ch.5 is also quiet, the book has a front-third problem. Right now it's a watch, not yet a wound — but Ch.4 is the hinge, and it should be tightened before finalize.
