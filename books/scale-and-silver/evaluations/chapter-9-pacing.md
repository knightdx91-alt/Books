# Ch.9 "The Reason Is Gone" [A] — pacing check

**Verdict:** PASS. Opens Act II-A on the book's pivotal AGENCY beat; confrontation → decision →
action, no sag.

- **Slot in arc:** first chapter of Act II-A (~28%). After TP1 (Ch.7 exposure) and Korvan's
  aftermath (Ch.8), this is Amelia's aftermath AND her turn from hidden-by-others to
  self-determining. The "passive-protagonist fix" the whole re-architecture was built around lands
  here: the cage-reason (treaty) expires with the war, and she *chooses* to break out. Correct high-
  agency slot; the emotional low (parting from mother) and the forward kick (escape) both fire.
- **Three-movement shape, escalating:** (1) mother-daughter confrontation — love-as-cage, the reason
  debate, the Queen's "No"; (2) Maren — the cost, the promise, the yes; (3) the escape — the spit-boy
  near-miss, the riders already on the roads, out the gate into open sky. Each movement raises stakes
  and none plateaus.
- **Scene vs summary:** high scene throughout — the descent that was first drafted as summary is now
  a real near-miss set-piece (the sleeping spit-boy she must cross the light past). No reflective
  coda; the chapter ends on forward motion ("Then she took the first step, and the world let her
  take it" / walked out on her own two feet).
- **Dialogue share:** 45% — the highest in the book; both the Queen and Maren scenes are dramatized,
  not narrated. Directly answers the show-vs-tell drift.
- **Note (earned):** show_tell abstraction runs elevated (~6.5/1k, over the 6.0 line) — this is a
  philosophical argument (duty vs. want, reason vs. love) whose spine is the title. Deliberate and
  Bishop-consistent (rulers argue in concepts); everything else on the show/tell panel is strong
  (filter 5.9, named-emotion 0.2, dialogue 45%). Flagged for the evaluator to judge, not padded away.
- **Hook & pull:** Hook = "They took her not to a cell but to her mother's own rooms, which was
  worse." Pull = out the gate, hunted, uncaged, "made all the difference in the world and none at all."
- **Length vs neighbors:** 5,053 words. Cluster holds (Ch.1–8 ~5,000–5,150). No chapter >2× another.
- **Canon:** cage-reason-expires hinge on-page; Queen warm-but-controlling (love as cage, not cold);
  Maren's cost paid; the blood-vial = mother's non-heritable magyk handed on as her one possible
  gift; Loric's hunters already moving. Amelia ACTS — not rescued, not a victim.

No fix needed before finalize. (Optional later: watch that "Amelia argues in abstractions" doesn't
become her default register — give the next Amelia chapter more sensory/scene ballast.)
