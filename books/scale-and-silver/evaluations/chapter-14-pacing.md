**Verdict:** PASS. The dialogue-driven emotional hinge before TP2 — the father-son argument that pays
off the Ch.3 swim-memory plant as the chief's whole creed, and the Selwyn snap that shows the
garden secret corroding Korvan. Correct high-talk, low-action slot: a breather of human pressure
placed between the Ch.13 power set-piece and the Ch.15 shift.

- **Slot in arc:** Act II-A close (~46%), immediately before TP2 (first shift, Ch.15). After the
  physical peak of Ch.13 (the tarn), this chapter turns inward — two long two-handers (chief, then
  Selwyn) instead of a set-piece. Right call: the reader needs the relational stakes locked before
  the transformation lands.
- **Swim-memory payoff:** the Ch.3 plant ("he let go so the boy would learn to hold himself up")

  thing that drowns you, not the water. Dramatized as argument, not narrated. The creed and the war
  become the same gesture: letting the son go under on purpose.

  agree he was right" — the outline's mandate (mentor-father both right and wrong) is met and left
  deliberately unbalanced. No tidy reconciliation.
- **The Selwyn snap:** Korvan turns his father's own move (take your hand away so they learn to swim)
  into cruelty toward the one person standing for him — and Selwyn refuses to be pushed off. Pays off
  Ch.3's friendship; gives Korvan the "you did just exactly what one of ours does" line he has wanted
  his whole life, said to Selwyn instead of received. Earns its emotion in body/action.
- **Plot engine:** the Ch.13 hunters' message has reached camp (Bricc/reeve's men) — the noose

  mountain"); Selwyn supplies the game trail + horse. Korvan now MUST get Amelia clear by noon.

  round to full, or near it" sets the TP2 timer.
