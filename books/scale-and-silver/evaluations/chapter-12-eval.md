## The Four Special Checks Requested

1. **The subversion (compassion deepens suspicion).** Lands DRAMATIZED. The boy's "Something's wrong with him" (37) echoes the "something's wrong with her" that burned the village — the mechanism is shown, not explained. The reeve's "keep him off... the dead" (49) is the theme made into a scene, not a coda. The village burning itself is a genuinely fresh, earned reframing of war's cost — no bodies dwelt on, the camera cuts (the bundle "he made himself not look at again," 11). Not sentimental, not gratuitous.

2. **The shift seed.** Restrained and situational — heat, ache, the warm-river/steam dream, "something down his spine unfolded" (111) — the dragon is never named. The "let it be now" (117) *deepens* Korvan (it makes his lifelong want monstrous to himself) rather than over-explaining the shift. Consistent with Ch.8's "instinct went quiet"/swim seeds; the father's-hand-leaving-the-belly memory is the through-line. Catastrophized-shift device fires **once** (the night sequence + morning inventory read as a single instance) — within the ≤1 cap. Set up cleanly for the Ch.14 payoff.

3. **Moral complexity (the goat).** Reads as the intended doubleness, not gratuitous. Korvan's inner arithmetic — "I am a man who buried children today and now I am stealing the last living thing off their street to feed a vampire" (87) — holds both truths without resolving them. Amelia's "I want it set down... that it cost me" (93) is earned by Ch.11's "it's not a person" rule and her hunger-clock; her "I buried a village today as well" gives her an independent moral interior, not a reaction to him.

4. **The chief.** The grave beat pays off Ch.8's father-thread cleanly. "You were the best man on this green today" (79) and the hand-on-the-neck that he "did not take away again at once" (79) advance the Ch.8 restraint. The dead-mother account ("the healers stopped coming... I tended her myself," 71) is **consistent** with Ch.8 (a wasting "held too long that turned inward"; healers frightened off) — it enriches without contradicting. No canon breach.

## Show-vs-Tell / POV / Voice-Wear
- **Dramatized, not narrated:** horror (chosen houses, the untouched neighbors), propaganda (the self-propagating word), and the shift are all shown. No lore-dump.
- **Scene-end codas:** Sections 1–3 close on image/action/body (the father-motif "put it down," the warm print on the neck, the walk into the dark) — clean. **Section 4** closes on the hot/cold hands (image) but the final clause "over a village that had burned itself down over the fear of exactly the two of them" restates theme — the one place a faint narrated-meaning note creeps in. Earned and image-anchored, so acceptable; flagged as the sole coda-risk.
- **Strict close-third** held throughout; the Amelia lines come through Korvan's perception of the canvas.
- **"Hold two things" motif kept OUT of narration** — correctly. The doubleness is re-voiced fresh ("Relief and grief with no seam between them," 115).
- **Retired phrases:** none present. "put it down" (not the retired "down, not away"); no "never once"; no "at his edge." Device caps respected.
