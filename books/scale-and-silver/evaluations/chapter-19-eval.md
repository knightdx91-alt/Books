## The specific checks you asked for

- **Bishop voice + deep Korvan POV:** HOLDS. Body-first, plain, menace-by-restraint. The cold
  undertow runs under the tenderness (the dragon "coming up toward the surface unasked"). One
  drift: the chapter's *opening* three paragraphs are long rolling summary — more "and…and…and"
  shape than Bishop's clipped median. Polish, not a fail.
- **Is the dragon-can't-hide reversal earned?** YES. It's not stated and dropped; it's built
  from the byre writ, then re-derived in the climb ("there would be a writ on the byre door of
  the last steading in the world"). The reader arrives at it a half-beat before Korvan does.
- **Is Loric's move concrete and in-character?** YES — the standout. Sealless writ, pays double,
  demands public display, and Korvan reads the uncostable emotional motive correctly: "It was not
  strategy… This wanted the Queen to look at the ruin of the thing she'd meant to fly on her
  banner, and grieve." Aligns exactly with canon (Loric wants the Queen to *suffer*, cannot be
  bought). No cartoon villainy — it costs him money to buy grief. Excellent.
- **Selwyn dramatic irony without the secret leaking?** YES. Selwyn offers to guard the man
  against the dragon, not knowing they are one throat — "he was offering to guard a man against
  the man himself." The secret holds; the closest to a leak ("You were wrong") is a protective
  deflection that *keeps* the secret, not one that spills it. Landed cleanly.
- **Amelia kept non-damsel while off-page?** YES, emphatically. She *chooses* the split, does the
  strategic math, and is framed as the deadlier watch. Agency guardrail satisfied.
- **Info-dump on ancestral/dragon lore?** NONE. Canon held.
