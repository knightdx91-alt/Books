## 2. THE CRITIC — developmental editor / agent repping adult romantasy & fantasy

*Structure, originality, prose discipline, market position. Judged against adult-romantasy comps.*

The credibility arc that began with R2's feeding fix and R3's Selwyn/power repair is, as of this edit, complete.

Two professional notes on the *how*, because they're the honest residue. First, the seed is a forecast, not an event — R3's fix list asked for "a bleed she *can't* catch in time," a twin; what was delivered is a projection inside a reflection. It does the credibility work (stated-before-use is the standard), but it's a promissory note honored rather than a precedent shown. ## 5. THE DEVOTED READER — emotionally invested in the couple, the cost, the ending

*Cared about all of them again. Reports what wrecked me, and this round something was given back to me.*

I asked for one thing in round 3. I said Maren — the buttons, "I'll not be made sorry I came," the warmest person in this cold book — deserved her own stroke and not a half-share of the Queen's. I'd braced for the answer to be "that's the point, war doesn't apportion." Instead the book did something better than moving her death: it **gave her death back to her.** The reckoning in the ford now reads: "Maren it had not taken fast — Maren it had left the time for, and Maren had used that time to forbid her the reach, to spare her the trying and the finding out, telling her with a plain last breath that it would not hold." Do you understand what that does? It makes her death *an act*. The iron shared its cast, but Maren didn't share her dying — she spent it the way she spent every hour of her life, keeping Amelia. "I'll not have you learn twice in one hour that it won't" was already in the scene; now the book itself knows that's what she did, and names it as different in kind from the Queen's death and from Selwyn's. I went back and reread the button count with that in my head and it's bigger now. The count comes out even because *she* made it come out even. That was my fix, and it landed, and I'm crying about a paragraph edit, which is the correct response to a good one.

What I still hold, and will keep holding: **Amelia never breaks.** Ch.28: "She did not weep... there was nothing in her yet that could." Ch.29 gives me the voice going "for the length of one word, thinner than she had told it to be," against his throat, and the trade named out loud — "I would give the crown back tonight to sit in it one more hour with the two of them on the far side of the bolt" — and that's the closest the book will ever let me stand to her grief, and I've made a kind of peace with standing there. Canon; cost; I log it every round because a grief-reader will feel it every read.

And the mercy ledger: with Selwyn dead (right call, still mourned — "the boy went out under her hands regardless," and the clansmen who "talk about him a great deal. About what a downed one ought to do," which is the book being kind in the only register it has left), the close is very dark for a comfort-reader. Rosalia, Maren, Selwyn, Loric — the only warmth that survives is the mews, and the mews is enough for me, barely, on the strength of "a thing that chooses you is worth ten thousand things that were only ever going to."

**Blindspots/issues:**
1. **Ch.28–29 — Amelia never breaks.** Unchanged and canonical; the Ch.29 thin-voice beat remains the single crack. The grief-reader stays outside the largest loss, by design, and feels the glass every read.
2. **Ch.28 — the iron itself is still shared.** I got Maren's death back in kind; a wish survives that the cast hadn't taken them in one stroke. It's a wish now, not a wound — downgrading it myself.
3. **The mercy ledger at the close.** With Selwyn gone, the book ends with almost no survivals a devoted reader was allowed to keep; the mews scene and the chief's "You needed the one that was coming" carry the entire warm load of the last chapter. They hold it. Barely.

**Verdict:** Buy, press it on everyone, and now I cry at Ch.28 for one more reason. **9/10** (R3: 8.5, **+0.5**). The Maren fix is why.
