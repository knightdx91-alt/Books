## 1. THE DEVOURER — adult-romantasy superfan

Five rounds I've logged the same complaint and this is the one where they finally handed me the thing. The Ch.21 open is *exactly* the beat I've been begging for since round one — the two of them under the one blanket, "her cold length fitted along the warm of him," her mouth at his throat "not hungry, not asking, only laying it there, the small hard claim of a woman who had spent her whole life forbidden to want a thing where it could be seen," and him making himself "learn the exact shape and weight and chill of her along him, the way a man fixes in his mind a thing he... will soon have to do without." Heat cadence is unchanged (two scenes, back-loaded) and I'll keep logging it as taste — but the felt intimacy of the whole book just went up without breaking the two-explicit canon, which is the trick I didn't think could be done at this stage. Nothing I love got flattened by the de-tic; the Ch.21 throat-claim is still the best quiet page in the book.

**Verdict:** Buy. **9/10** (R4: 8.5, **+0.5**). They finally bought the couple the beat I've asked for since R1, and the "Mine/Hers" close is the possessive claim my shelf wants. Held off the top only by the still-bittersweet, not-same-life ending — which I now accept is the book, not a flaw.
