## What was evaluated and why

The book was finalized with every chapter at Genesis Floor ≥ 8.5. A post-completion revision
session then made changes that were never formally re-scored: a new mid-book couple beat (Ch.19),
a skill-gap seed + power-canon scoping (Ch.20), the Selwyn-death rework + Maren-distinct-death +
power-canon reframe (Ch.28), the Selwyn death report + ruling beat + time-thinning (Ch.29), and
ten light de-tic / timeline / thread-closure edits (Ch.11, 15, 17, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25, 26, 27).

This report gives the four heavily-changed chapters a **full formal re-eval** (7 dimensions,
4-reader simulation, anti-AI scan, Tomorrow Test, PASS/FAIL vs Floor ≥ 8.5, delta vs prior
scores) and the ten lightly-changed chapters a **floor-holds verification**. A prior evaluator
run was stopped mid-task; its partial re-eval deltas (in `chapter-19/28/29-eval.md`) were read
but **not trusted** — every claim here was re-derived from the current HEAD text. One of that
run's clearance claims proved wrong (see the Ch.29 headline finding).

**Canon honored while judging** (not scored as defects): silver hair; "magyk"; no sexual
violence; Amelia dangerous-not-victim; power = vast-but-untrained (the limit is inexperience,
never a convenient ceiling); dragon memory situational, not Atlantis-linked; the two explicit
scenes (Ch.18, Ch.26) are deliberate and category-appropriate.

## The finding, precisely
The Selwyn-death rework was propagated to Ch.29 in exactly ONE place (¶65–67, the report and
ruling — which are excellent: on-voice, consistent, and "went out under her hands regardless"
ties the death to the craft-gap canon cleanly). But Amelia's two interior recollections of the
ford were never swept:

1. **¶57** (the no-reprisal ruling's rationale): "…a stranger's blood staying where she told it
   to stay, easily, when it had not stayed for the two she had made of and been raised by."
   — In the new canon the blood did NOT stay for the stranger either.
2. **¶93** (the grief inventory before the beeswax confession): "She had held a stranger's
   wound in a ford and it had held, and she had made herself hold the whole ugly figure of
   that, the one power she owned useless for the two she loved and easy for a man she had
   never met…" — The revision log itself states the intended new figure: "the arithmetic is
   now total loss." This paragraph still computes the old figure.

This is the same defect class the interim re-eval caught in Ch.28 (¶167/169/171) — outcome
reworked, scene-tail swept, **cross-chapter recollections not swept**. The stopped evaluator
run checked ¶65 and declared Ch.29 clean; it did not grep the chapter for the ford.

Note the fix is not mechanical deletion — both passages get STRONGER under the new canon:
- ¶57's ruling logic improves: the ford failure + the nothing-changing kill both feed "I've had
  enough deaths that changed nothing" more honestly than a success did.
- ¶93's inventory becomes the total-loss figure: held her mother's heart — would not stay; held
  Maren's hand — forbidden the reach; held a stranger's wound — too green to catch it. Three
  hands, nothing held. That is the arithmetic the crown sits on, and it is bleaker and truer
  than the old version.
