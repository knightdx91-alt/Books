## ACT I — THE CAGES & THE EXPOSURE (Ch. 1–8, ~0–28%)

### Ch.1 — "Contraband" [A]
Amelia, hidden her whole life, decides she will be at the banquet. Establish her cage, her want
(to be *seen*), and — crucially — the *reason* for the cage: her mother hides her so they don't
trigger the treaty violation. Plant the silver hair (plainly), the counting/acoustics habit.
Mother-daughter scene: love vs control; surface "be careful," real "you must not exist."
**Subversion:** the forbidden dream is the most ordinary thing — to attend a party.

### Ch.2 — "The Servants' Passages" [A]
Amelia's hidden life in full, through the two people who love and cage her. **(a) The Queen trains
Amelia in blood magyk** — a private, tender ritual, because the gift was never supposed to be
heritable and there is no one else alive who *can* teach her; the mother who must cage her by day
is the only one who can hand her power in the dark. Warm, intimate, and quietly frightening (what
Amelia can already do). Plants her hard-won control (pays off Ch.13 surfacing, Ch.24 by-her-will).
**(b) Maren** (keeper, the human the Queen won't feed on, closest thing to a friend) and the
machinery of secrecy — the servants' passages, how Amelia will slip the watch. Establish Maren's
own dread and torn debt, the silver-recounting tic, and the Queen's untouched-servant quirk in
plain sight. **Subversion:** the handmaid has her own fear before she serves any plot function;
and the "training montage" is really a mother and daughter stealing the one hour a day the cage
lets them be simply that.

### Ch.3 — "Unshifted" [K]
Korvan, ~19–20 and still no animal, travels to the vampire banquet in the chief's retinue — an
anomaly among the about-to-belong. Introduce the chief (his only family), Selwyn, and Loric
(reasonable, watchful). Plant fire pedantry, the swim memory, the father-son pair as a pair of
two. **Subversion:** the heir is the one thing his culture has no respect for — empty-handed.

### Ch.4 — "The Man Who Only Asks" [K]
Arrival at the vampire castle; Loric works the camp. Build Loric as *reasonable* before he is
monstrous — patient, tidy, "only asking questions"; he is the only one who treats unshifted
Korvan as fully a person, and it reads as warmth. Show the clans as a political body and where
the chief's support is thin (the leadership-challenge lever). **Subversion:** the villain is the
kindest man in the camp.

### Ch.5 — "Among People" [A]
Amelia sneaks into the banquet. The world on display (vampires, humans, the pact); she is
overwhelmed simply to *be among people*, unseen-then-briefly-seen without being corrected for
existing. She slips to the garden to breathe. Plant the fountain that covers sound.
**Subversion:** rapture where the reader expects infiltration tension — the danger is invisible
to her because she's happy.

### Ch.6 — "The Garden" [K]
Korvan, escaping his own humiliation, finds Amelia in the garden. They talk. The bond —
recognition of shared exile (both people with no place). She names her aloneness (seed for the
"clan of one"). The fountain covers their words: Loric will *see*, not *hear*. **Adult charged
pull** — the first ungovernable draw, recognition-first not desire-first (guardrail 1). Loric
spies them at the end. **Subversion:** not love-at-first-sight — recognition of shared exile.

### Ch.7 — "Heads I Win" [A] — **TP1 (~24%)**
Loric publicly exposes Amelia at the banquet. The accusation engine fires; no proof needed. The
chief is trapped — declare war or face a challenge he'll lose. The Queen's face shows nothing,
and that nothing is the worst thing. **Subversion:** being seen — her wish — is the catastrophe.

### Ch.8 — "What a Reasonable Man Does" [K]
The chief declares war rather than face the challenge; Korvan, publicly linked to the vampire
girl, is folded into suspicion. They leave for home. The chief's reasonableness was the lever
(irony stated). Korvan feels relief it's over, then shame at the relief; snaps at Selwyn (small),
regrets it. **Subversion:** the war-declaration is a defeat dressed as resolve — everyone loses,
exactly as Loric designed.

## ACT II-A — THE CAGE BREAKS, THE ROAD BINDS (Ch. 9–14, ~28–48%)

### Ch.9 — "The Reason Is Gone" [A] (court)
Post-exposure. The Queen moves to hide Amelia *deeper* / prepare to send her away — the
disavow-to-protect that reads to the world as guilt. But Amelia sees it: **the treaty reason for
hiding is dead** — the war has already started; the only reason left to hide is to survive. With
no inherited reason left to obey, she **chooses** to break the terms of her own protection and
get out. Maren is the one who helps (her cost). The smuggling-out; Loric's hunters already
moving. **Subversion:** she isn't plotting escape from a villain — she's walking out of *love's*
cell, and love is indistinguishable from a cage.

### Ch.10 — "She Comes to Him" [K] → she seeks him out
Korvan on the journey home under public suspicion: whispers, a child who won't look at him,
Loric's people painting his bloodline sympathetic. Then **Amelia finds him** — hunted, out of
options, she has sought out the one person at the banquet who looked at her like a person and not
like contraband. Her choice, her risk (a strong active beat for her: she crosses enemy ground to
reach him). Korvan's costly choice in return: turn her in (safety, a way back to his people) or
**hide her from his own father and the retinue** — the girl the chief declared war over. He hides
her. Now the secret is doubled: the garden meeting AND a living vampire concealed in his father's
own train. **Subversion:** she isn't rescued — she walks into the lion's camp on purpose because
it's still her best odds; and his first big choice damns him further, and he makes it anyway.

### Ch.11 — "Two Targets" [K+A] braided
Forced proximity begins in earnest, and it's **adversarial**. He resents that her existence is
the pretext bleeding his people; she watches the propaganda make him a monster; neither trusts
the other. They must move together because apart they're both dead. First real friction and first
grudging competence-respect. Court cutaway seam via a short [C] section: Maren covering Amelia's
absence, the Queen's first maneuver. **Subversion:** the "meet cute" is two people who'd each be
safer if the other were gone.

### Ch.12 — "The Burned Village" [K]
A road stage where the war's first cost is concrete — a village already raided; the propaganda
has outrun them; open hostility for the first time. Korvan helps dig out the dead and earns
nothing — they fear him more for the strength. Amelia, hidden nearby, watches him be good for
no reward. First faint wrongness in Korvan's sleep (seeds the shift without naming it).
**Subversion:** compassion deepens the suspicion.

### Ch.13 — "Blood Answers" [A]
Hunters corner them, and Amelia's inherited blood magyk answers — **and it is strong.** Not a
frightened girl saved by luck: she is formidable, and the hunters learn it (she is why natural-
born vampires were thought impossible AND feared). The cost is control — it surfaces uncontrolled,
and she goes cold and clinical (voice-under-pressure) handling what she's capable of. **With
Korvan there to see it**, the "shouldn't be heritable" power becomes visible proof of the
accusation — the more powerful she is, the more damning. Korvan sees she is genuinely dangerous —
and does not run; if anything it levels them. **Subversion:** her power-awakening is real might
*and* incrimination at once — strength that condemns her.

### Ch.14 — "Let Go So You'd Learn to Swim" [K]
The father-son argument, staged on the road when Korvan's path rejoins the retinue's for a night:
Korvan demands the chief should have acted; the chief explains survival-to-fight-later; the swim
memory pays off as the creed. The chief reveals he *knows* the bond was genuine and allows the
propaganda anyway (tragic). Afterward Korvan snaps viciously at Selwyn — the garden secret,
carried alone, leaking sideways. He has to get Amelia away before the retinue finds her.
**Subversion:** the mentor-father is both right and wrong, and the scene refuses to resolve which.

## ACT II-B — THE HINGE (Ch. 15–18, ~48–62%)

### Ch.15 — "The First Full Moon" [K] — **TP2 (~50%)**
On the first full moon after his birthday, Korvan shifts — into a **dragon** — calendar
coincidence, not magic — **with Amelia the only witness.** Clan of one; no elders, no traditions.
First trickle of dragon ancestral memory (situational, NOT Atlantis-linked). The silver moon.
Bewilderment and grief, not power-fantasy — he wanted to *belong* and became the loneliest thing
alive. **Subversion:** the epic dragon reveal is a catastrophe, not a gift.

### Ch.16 — "The Morning After a Body" [K]
Human again. What it means sinks in; the dragon's flight was seen — rumor races toward Loric.
Now Korvan is unmistakably the monster the propaganda promised, and Amelia is unmistakably the
witch. Proximity cuts both ways: they've seen each other's worst hour, and each is now the exact
thing the other's people fear. The danger of the dragon draws hunters closer. Second, sharper
memory-fragment (grief of a dead clan). **Subversion:** no mastery — only consequence.

### Ch.17 — "Banner" [C] (Queen / Maren, court)
Word of the dragon reaches court; Amelia's magyk and Korvan's dragon are made into a war-banner —
"the vampire witch and her dragon" — by her *own* side. The Queen maneuvers and every reasonable
move feeds Loric. Maren's torn loyalty sharpens; she's made to carry a message that could damn or
save Amelia. **Subversion:** her own side weaponizes her exactly as her enemies do.

### Ch.18 — "What the Road Made" [K+A] braided — **FIRST EXPLICIT SCENE (~62%)**
Deep in forced proximity, the adversarial edge wears through to something real — earned by
everything survived, not by attraction-at-first-sight. The bond turns genuine and charged, and
**the first full explicit scene lands here** — Bishop's voice and build with a step past her on
graphic detail (deep POV, emotionally loaded, the dangerous-devoted restraint doing the work).
Two equals choosing each other — she is powerful in her own right, not taken or led. Dark
intensity underneath: what they're becoming, what it will cost. **Subversion:** the turn is a
decision, not a swoon — two dangerous people choosing to stop being enemies.

## ACT II-C — NO NEUTRAL GROUND (Ch. 19–21, ~62–72%)

### Ch.19 — "A Dragon Can't Hide" [K]
Korvan tries to opt out — to be no one's weapon — and learns a dragon cannot disappear; both
sides want or fear him. Loric makes his first move to claim or destroy the dragon-asset. Selwyn
offers, plainly, to come with him — and Korvan can't bring anyone where he's going, and can't
even say why (the secret still his alone). **Subversion:** he declines both confidant and
escape; the marked carry it alone.

### Ch.20 — "The Shape of the War" [C/A] (court + Amelia's first act of will)
The war turns from rumor to front line; the human cost becomes concrete — Maren's home village
named on a casualty list at court. Amelia (still on the road) does something small, forbidden,
and effective to help — and it terrifies her that it worked. She crosses fully from enduring to
acting. Plant the logistics that make the Ch.27–28 climax possible. **Subversion:** her first
action is small and illicit; the success scares her more than failure would.

### Ch.21 — "Discovered" [K] → **TORN APART**
Two things collide. Korvan gets a fragment of clan-lore (an old keeper or relic site): the dragon
clan was **not killed — outwaited** (seeds the climax logic; still not Atlantis-linked, one
person's uncertain telling). And **the chief discovers Korvan has been hiding Amelia** — the
doubled secret from Ch.10 detonates. Father-son rupture at the worst moment. Amelia does NOT get
caught or dragged out: she is the most dangerous person in the camp, and she proves it (blood
magyk, not helplessness) — but staying means destroying Korvan in front of his own people, so she
**chooses** to go, on her own power. She leaves to end the thing herself, walking toward the
reckoning at court / with Loric on her own terms (runway to Ch.23–24). **Subversion:** the split
isn't a capture — the powerful one leaves by choice to protect the one who sheltered her; the
bond is torn exactly when it became real.

## ACT III — WHAT YOU CHOOSE (Ch. 22–29, ~72–100%)

### Ch.22 — "Survive to Fight Later" [K] — **TP3 for Korvan (~76%)**
The chief asks Korvan to be *useful* — to let the dragon be leveraged. Moral clarity asks him to
refuse. Korvan chooses to **act where his father only waited**, naming a third path (neither the
chief's instrument nor a runaway). Dragon memory trickles the lore that makes it possible. The
chief is *relieved*, not betrayed. **Subversion:** a quiet handoff — the pragmatist blesses the
clarity he couldn't afford.

### Ch.23 — "The Cause of Every Death" [C/A] (court)
Loric engineers a public moment framing Amelia and Korvan as the *cause* of every death so far.
The Queen finally acts — and it's the wrong save, making it worse. Maren's choice comes due; she
acts (or refuses) at a cost, giving Amelia's public ordeal a private human price. Loric, winning,
hears his father's laugh, and it curdles the win. **Subversion:** truth is offered and changes
nothing — the thesis made literal.

### Ch.24 — "Seen on My Own Terms" [A] — **TP3 for Amelia**
Amelia stops hiding — chooses to be visible deliberately, accepting she'll be misread, denying
Loric the secret he weaponizes. Her blood magyk used by her will for the first time. Now a
**lethal, defiant act**, not a granted wish. Mirrored against Ch.1. **Subversion:** the public
reveal is bleak control and sacrifice, not triumph.

### Ch.25 — "Toward the Same Fire" [K+A] braided
Both commit to the same theater of war; the torn-apart threads begin to close the distance. The
strongest **yearning** beats — each moving toward the other and the climax, a near-miss that
cranks the ache. Loric's net tightens on both. **Subversion:** the book stretches the distance at
the act break instead of rushing them together — the wanting is the engine.

### Ch.26 — "The Reunion" [K+A] braided — **EXPLICIT PAYOFF**
They reunite at the war's edge, no longer the people from the garden, and choose each other
deliberately. **The full open-door / explicit intimacy beat lands here** — Bishop's voice and
build with a step past her on graphic detail (deep POV, emotionally loaded, restraint-tension,
earned by the whole arc). The dangerous-devoted dynamic fully realized: lethal to the world,
his hands careful with her. **Subversion:** not romantic-reunion catharsis nor doomed parting —
a clear-eyed mutual choosing, love as a decision by two people with nothing prefab to inherit it
from.

### Ch.27 — "Outlasted (I)" [K] — climax pt 1
The battle joins; the dragon enters the war on Korvan's authored terms; the plan to *contain*
Loric (not defeat-by-argument — impossible by canon) is set in motion. The dragon's nature
(outlasting, enduring — Ch.21 lore) shapes the strategy. Heavy cost begins. **Subversion:** the
dragon does not win the day — it buys time; the chief's creed turned into a weapon by the son who
rejected it.

### Ch.28 — "Outlasted (II)" [A] — climax pt 2
Loric is stopped/contained — outlasted at unrecoverable cost; the war is redirected, not undone.
**The named cost is paid in the same blow: the Queen (Rosalia) AND Maren both die.** Amelia loses
her mother and the one person who truly knew her at once. **Reader-only reveal:** the Queen dies
**never knowing Loric's name** — even her death denies him what he wanted (she never suffers
*knowing why*). Loric's grief breaks through once, then is buried forever. And as Rosalia's
natural-born heir, **Amelia is left the throne** — the hidden girl about to rule openly.
**Subversion:** no catharsis of justice, and the "victory" hands Amelia a crown that costs her
everyone who made her a person.

### Ch.29 — "What We Choose" [A, with K coda]
Aftermath. **Amelia takes the throne** — the first natural-born vampire Queen, the contraband
girl now ruling openly. It is the want granted *and* a new cage: she is finally seen, and more
alone than ever, having paid in her mother and Maren. She inherits the crown but **chooses** how
to wear it (inherit vs choose, resolved). Korvan claims the dragon as his own authored place — not
"the Queen's dragon" (a slot) but hers by choice, and she his: two outsiders now at the center
they were once exiled from. "The clan of one is now two." A world redrawn (a natural-born Queen, a
living dragon) — arc complete, strong sequel runway. **Residue:** unsettled by how little truth
mattered; the crown sits on the girl no one was allowed to see.
