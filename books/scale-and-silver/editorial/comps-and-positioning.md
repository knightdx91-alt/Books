# Comps & Positioning — *A Bond of Scale and Silver*

> Standalone adult romantasy · ~154,000 words

## Positioning line

**Adult romantasy in the dangerous-devoted register of Anne Bishop, made defensible on the page** — a dark, plain-spoken fantasy romance with two hunted leads, a chosen (not fated) bond, two earned explicit scenes, and a bittersweet, crowned-and-caged ending. Per the round-5/6 beta panel, the couple carries present-tense intimacy across the whole arc (meeting, road, first scene, separation-trough yearning, reunion, and a possessive claim close) without breaking the two-explicit canon or the intentional bittersweet close: *"adult romantasy, defensible on the page."*

## Shelf / audience

- **Category:** Adult romantasy (fantasy romance) — dual-POV, slow-burn forced proximity, spicy back-half (two explicit scenes), dark intensity with **no sexual violence**.
- **Shelf:** with Broadbent, Jensen, Hashem, Gong — dark/political fantasy romance; adjacent to the Bishop dark-fantasy backlist for voice.
- **Reader:** adult romantasy readers who want danger real and restraint doing the work; who prefer a chosen bond over fated-mates and a bittersweet, earned ending over a tidy HEA; drawn to dangerous-not-victim heroines, morally grey courts, and hunted-on-the-road slow burn.
- **Age of leads:** ~19–22 (adult register, not YA).

## Comp titles

1. **Carissa Broadbent — *The Serpent and the Wings of Night* (2022/2024)** — Adult vampire romantasy with a dangerous, hunted heroine and a lethal-but-devoted love interest; matches the dark tone, adult heat, and vampire-court danger.
2. **Danielle L. Jensen — *A Fate Inked in Blood* (2024)** — Political fantasy romance with a powerful, dangerous woman at the center of a war she didn't choose; matches the war-and-court spine, grounded weathered world, and dangerous-heroine agency.
3. **Sara Hashem — *The Jasad Heir* (2023)** — Hidden royal with forbidden, dangerous magic, hunted for what she is; matches Amelia's hidden-heir-with-contraband-power premise and the secret-identity tension.
4. **Anne Bishop — *The Black Jewels Trilogy*** *(voice comp)* — The dangerous-devoted lead (the "Daemon" technique), plain declarative menace-by-restraint, dark intensity without gratuitous violence. Cited as **craft/voice** register, not a current sales comp.
5. **Chloe Gong — *Immortal Longings* (2023)** *(optional / mood)* — Adult fantasy romance with lethal leads and grim political stakes; useful for the grown-up, high-danger tone if a third recent sales comp is wanted.

> **Query pairing:** lead with **Broadbent × Jensen** (both recent, adult, in-category, successful-not-mega), plus the Bishop **voice** line. Hold Hashem/Gong in reserve to tailor per agent's MSWL.

## Positioning cautions

- Do **not** comp *From Blood and Ash* (the working title *Treaty of Blood and Ash* was dropped for that clash) or other mega-bestsellers as primary — agents want "the next rising author," not "the next Maas."
- Lead the pitch with the **premise hook** (first natural-born vampire + dragon clan-of-one) and the **chosen-not-fated / bittersweet** differentiators; these are what separate it from destined-mates romantasy defaults.
- The **154k word count runs long for a debut** — flag it honestly and signal willingness to trim toward category norms.
