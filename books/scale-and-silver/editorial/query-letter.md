Dear [Agent Name],

Because you're seeking adult romantasy with dangerous women, morally grey courts, and a slow burn that earns its heat, I'm hoping you'll connect with **A BOND OF SCALE AND SILVER**, a standalone adult romantasy complete at **154,000 words**.

Amelia is the impossible thing the treaty between vampires, shifters, and humans never imagined: the first vampire *born*, not made, in a thousand years — the Queen's secret daughter, hidden her whole life behind a bolt, cherished and caged in equal measure. She carries blood magyk that should not be heritable, and no proof exists that she was born rather than made. Under a treaty that forbids making new vampires, that ambiguity is a war waiting for a spark. The spark is Loric, a schemer poisoned by a grief the Queen doesn't even remember causing, who exposes Amelia at a banquet to force the shifter chief into a war he cannot win either way.

So Amelia runs — and so does Korvan, the chief's late-shifting son, who becomes the one creature more hunted than she is: a dragon out of dead legend, a clan of one, no elders and no traditions. Thrown onto the same road, two exiles learn each other while the war closes behind them. What grows between them isn't fate; it's a choice, and the cost of finally being seen comes due at a crown neither of them wanted.

**A BOND OF SCALE AND SILVER** will appeal to readers of Carissa Broadbent's *The Serpent and the Wings of Night* and Danielle L. Jensen's *A Fate Inked in Blood*, with the plain-spoken menace and dangerous-devoted register of Anne Bishop's Black Jewels. It is dual-POV, with two explicit scenes, dark intensity without sexual violence, and a bittersweet, earned ending rather than a tidy happily-ever-after. I'm aware the word count runs long for a debut; the manuscript is polished and I'm glad to discuss trimming toward category norms.
