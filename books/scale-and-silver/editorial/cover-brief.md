# Cover Brief — *A Bond of Scale and Silver*

> Standalone adult romantasy · ~154,000 words
> For the cover designer. Visual language, not narrative. Direction, not an image spec.

## Emotional Concept

The single feeling the cover must evoke: **cold intimacy under a hunter's moon** — two dangerous, lonely things drawn together in a world that wants them dead. Menace and tenderness in one frame. Not a swoon; not a battle. The viewer should feel a held, watchful stillness with heat banked underneath — power held on a leash, a secret about to be seen. Adult, dark, and grown-up: this is romantasy for readers who want the danger real and the restraint doing the work.

## Color Palette

- **Primary — Cold silver / moon-grey** (#C7CDD6 to #8A93A0). Amelia's silver hair and the silver full moon; the dominant cool light of the whole cover.
- **Secondary — Deep night blue-black** (#0A0D16) paired with **charcoal slate** (#2A2E38). The dark the two of them move through; the field the silver cuts against.
- **Accent — Blood red, used sparingly and deliberately** (#7A1220 — dark arterial, not candy). The blood magyk; a single restrained note, never a splash.
- **Warmth accent — low ember / dragonfire gold** (#B5762E), rare and banked — one warm break against all the cold, the dragon and the fire between them.
- **Mood:** cool and dark overall, high contrast, one or two intentional warm/red breaks. Moonlit, not sunlit. Literary-dark, not neon.

## Visual Elements

**Must include (choose a strong focal hierarchy — do not cram all of these):**
1. **Silver hair / the moon** — a woman's cold silver hair or the full silver moon as the luminous anchor; the single brightest thing in a dark frame. If a figure appears, she is composed, watchful, powerful — never a posed damsel.
2. **The dragon** — a clan-of-one dragon, best as silhouette, coiled shape, a wing across the sky, or a single eye/scaled curve at the edge; solemn and dangerous, not a roaring action centerpiece.
3. **Blood-magyk motif** — a restrained thread of dark red: a drop, a fine line of light at the fingertips, a thread through the title. Sparse.
4. **Cold court vs. open road** — an optional split of texture: cut stone / iron / crown-cold on one side, wild moonlit country on the other (the book's two poles). Subtle, not literal diptych.
5. **Scale-and-silver pairing** — the title's two halves (dragon scale, silver light) can braid into the central emblem for a symbol-forward treatment.

**Must avoid:**
- A ballgown clinch / romance-cover posing. Amelia is dangerous in her own right; if figures appear they are equals, contained, not glamorized or rescued.
- A roaring-dragon, swords-crossed, high-action montage. The book's power is restraint.
- Bright/saturated "gamer fantasy" or high-medieval palettes. Keep it cold, adult, literary.
- Fated-mates / glowing-bond clichés (no tether of light between two lovers). The bond here is chosen, not destined — don't sell fate.
- Spoiler imagery (no throne-and-corpses, no crowning scene — the cost is the read, not the sell).

## Typography

- **Title treatment:** strong serif with a cold, high-contrast edge — refined but weighty. Consider a faint silver-to-red or silver-to-ember gradient echoing scale-and-silver. *A BOND OF SCALE AND SILVER* dominant; no series line (standalone). An author-name line sits smaller beneath.
- **Author name:** moderate, debut-appropriate prominence, clean, secondary to the title.
- **Genre signals:** dark cool palette + a single luminous element (moon/silver hair) + a dragon silhouette + serif title = the adult-romantasy / dark fantasy-romance shelf (Broadbent / Jensen register), distinct from breezy YA-fantasy sans-serif.
- **Adult-shelf signal:** enough darkness and restraint to read grown-up; a small spice/heat cue is optional per retailer norms but the cover should sell danger and intimacy, not teen adventure.

## Visual References (for FEEL, not content)

- **Carissa Broadbent, *The Serpent and the Wings of Night*** — cold, dark, dangerous vampire-romantasy mood; symbol-forward, adult.
- **Danielle L. Jensen, *A Fate Inked in Blood*** — grounded, political, weathered-world fantasy romance; a dangerous woman at the center.
- **Anne Bishop, Black Jewels reissues** — dark, solemn, menace-by-restraint; power treated as consequential, not decorative.
- Take from these the **cold-dark palette, iconography-over-illustration, and adult restraint** — not their specific objects.

## Avoidances (summary)

- No spoilers (no throne, no crowning, no on-page deaths).
- No fated-mates bond imagery; no rescue/damsel framing.
- No clutter: one luminous focal idea (silver hair/moon, or the scale-and-silver emblem) beats five competing elements.
- Keep warmth (ember/red) deliberate and rare against a cold, watchful whole.
