# Synopsis — *A Bond of Scale and Silver*

> Standalone adult romantasy · ~154,000 words
> Present tense. The 2–3 page version is **spoiler-complete** (reveals the ending) for agents/editors.

---

## One-paragraph synopsis (~150 words)

In a world bound by an uneasy treaty between vampires, shifters, and humans, AMELIA is the impossible thing the treaty never imagined: the first vampire born, not made, in a thousand years — the Queen's secret daughter, hidden her whole life for the war her mere existence could start. When LORIC, a schemer poisoned by old grief, exposes her at a banquet to force the shifter chief into a war he cannot win, Amelia runs. So does KORVAN, the chief's late-shifting son, who becomes the one thing more hunted than she is: a dragon out of dead legend, a clan of one. Thrown together on the road, two exiles learn each other while the war closes behind them. By the end, Amelia is crowned the first natural-born Queen — seen at last, and caged again — having paid for it in the two people she loved most.

## One-page synopsis (~500 words)

AMELIA has never been allowed outside. The first natural-born vampire in a thousand years — a child no vampire was thought able to bear — she is the cherished, contraband secret of her mother, Queen ROSALIA, who loves her fiercely and cages her completely. Amelia carries inherited **blood magyk** that should not be heritable at all, and no proof exists that she was born rather than made; under the Treaty that forbids making new vampires, that ambiguity is a war waiting to happen. Rosalia trains her in private, tender rituals to control the gift, and teaches her the one rule that matters: *never a person.*

At a banquet meant to hold the fragile peace, Amelia meets KORVAN — the shifter chief's son, an outsider shamed by his failure to shift long past the usual age. In a garden where a fountain swallows their words, two lonely equals recognize each other. Then LORIC, a shifter nursing a private grief the Queen doesn't even remember causing, exposes Amelia publicly: proof or no proof, the chief must now declare war or lose face he cannot afford. Amelia's reason to hide dies with the peace — so she stops hiding to survive, and runs.

On the road home under suspicion, Korvan shifts for the first time on the first full moon after his birthday. He becomes a **dragon** — a clan of one, no elders, no traditions, the loneliest creature alive — with Amelia the only witness. What should be an epic gift is a catastrophe: now she is unmistakably the witch and he the monster the propaganda promised. Hunted by both sides, they cross a brutal country together, and the adversarial edge between them wears through to something chosen and charged. They are not fated; they are two exiles who decide.

Loric's nets tighten. When the chief discovers what Korvan is sheltering, father and son rupture; Amelia sends Korvan into the sky by her own choice rather than be a thing he's rescued. Apart, each turns toward the war Loric has engineered at a river crossing. Amelia walks back into the open and names herself before Loric can — denying him the secret that is his whole engine — and kills, by her own will, for the first time.

At the crossing, the dragon does not win: Korvan endures, drawing the enemy's iron onto himself so Amelia can reach Loric. She defeats Loric not by argument but by outlasting him — and the true blow is her mother's indifference: Rosalia dies with her whole attention on Amelia, never learning Loric's name. In one stroke of Loric's own war-engine, both **Rosalia and Maren** — Amelia's only friend — are killed.

As Rosalia's natural-born heir, Amelia takes the throne: the hidden girl now rules openly, seen at last and more alone than ever, the crown a new and finer cage. Korvan refuses to be *the Queen's dragon* and claims the shape as his own — hers by choice, and she his. The clan of one is now two. Loric is outlasted, the war redirected, the peace uneasy. The ending is bittersweet by design: two banks of the same water, together by choosing, never the same life.

## Full synopsis (2–3 pages, spoiler-complete)

**Setup.** The world runs on an uneasy Treaty among vampires, shifters, and humans. Vampires descend from Atlanteans and rule humans through a feudal "protection" pact under a Queen who wields **blood magyk**; the Treaty forbids the making of any *new* vampire, and is silent on natural-born vampires because none were thought possible. AMELIA is that impossibility: the first vampire born in a thousand years, secret daughter of Queen ROSALIA, hidden behind a bolt her whole life. She has silver hair, formidable inherited blood magyk that by every rule should not be heritable, and one want — to exist openly. Rosalia is glacial to the world and helplessly warm to Amelia, a mother whose flaw is love-as-control; she personally trains Amelia (she is the only living being who can) and drills the rule that keeps the magyk clean: *never a person.* The household loves Amelia; she is a cherished secret, not a shame.

Elsewhere, KORVAN, the shifter chief's only son (mother dead, no siblings), is an anomaly: a rare late shifter, still human well past the age his people change, carrying the outsider's ache. His father the chief is his whole family — a pragmatist whose creed is *survive to fight later*, taught through a childhood swim where he let the boy go under to make him hold himself up. LORIC, a shifter whose father the Queen killed in the last war — one kill among many that she does not even remember — moves among the courts. His motive is grief, not gain; he cannot be bought or negotiated. He wants the Queen to *suffer.*

**Inciting incident.** At a banquet meant to hold the peace, Korvan and Amelia meet in a garden and recognize each other as fellow exiles — not fated-mates, two lonely equals. Loric exposes Amelia publicly. Proof is unnecessary; the accusation alone traps the chief, who must declare war or accept a challenge he'll lose. He declares war. Korvan, publicly linked to the "vampire witch," goes home under suspicion.

**Rising action.** With the peace broken, Amelia's reason to hide is dead; she now hides only to survive the hunt, and flees the castle over her mother's control, carrying a vial of Rosalia's blood — the one gift her mother can send. On the road, Amelia seeks Korvan out and he hides her from his own father's war-train — a secret that damns him further, made by choice. On the first full moon after his birthday, Korvan shifts into a **dragon**, a clan of one, with Amelia the only witness: not triumph but catastrophe. The untrained dragon kills; the whole valley sees it fly; rumor races to court, where Amelia's own side weaponizes her as "the vampire witch and her dragon." Her handmaid and only friend MAREN — the one human Rosalia won't feed on — is sent north carrying a message that is both warning and arrow. Across a hunted, adversarial road (11–20), the two wear through to a chosen, charged bond; the first explicit turn is a decision, not a swoon. Loric's two nets — a silver-hunt and dark-of-moon crews armed with heart-stopping whale-iron — close on them both.

**Climax.** When the chief discovers what Korvan is sheltering, father and son rupture on an exposed crossing; in a whale-iron ambush, Amelia sends Korvan up and away by her own agency (non-damsel), and they are torn apart. Each turns toward the war Loric has built at the Elver crossing. Amelia refuses both a Crown-sealed rescue and a friend's plea to flee; she walks into the open, names herself before Loric can, and — inverting her mercy earlier in the book — kills by her own will. At the crossing the roles invert: Korvan becomes the loud shape, going up in the open to draw Loric's whole iron net onto himself so Amelia can cross unseen; the dragon does not win — it endures, absorbing two irons, the father's *survive to fight later* creed turned at last into Korvan's sharpest weapon. Amelia reaches Loric and does not argue him down (he can't be); she gives him silence and keeps her eyes on her mother. She outlasts him — and his true defeat is Rosalia's indifference: the Queen dies with her whole attention on Amelia, almond-cakes and *you're always cold*, never once looking at Loric, never learning his name or his grievance. A single flat cast of Loric's own whale-iron kills **Queen Rosalia AND Maren** together. Loric's grief cracks once, then buries itself; Amelia stills his heart afterward, deliberate anticlimax — the killing is only tidying. She saves a wounded ally by holding a torn vessel shut, the grotesque arithmetic being that her power worked for a stranger and was useless for the two she loved.

**Resolution.** As Rosalia's natural-born heir, AMELIA TAKES THE THRONE — the first natural-born vampire to rule, the contraband girl now seen openly. It is her want granted as a new and finer cage: she inherits the crown but chooses how to wear it, refusing a succession proclamation that would "prove" her born, and overruling her own council to spare Loric's men and the far bank. Korvan, down but alive, refuses the slot of "the Queen's dragon" and claims the shape as his own authored place — hers by choice, and she his. **The clan of one is now two.** Loric is outlasted and contained; the war is redirected, not undone; the peace is uneasy and paid for in Rosalia and Maren. The couple ends *two banks of the same water* — together because they chose, never the same life.

**Thematic resonance.** The book asks what it costs to be *seen on your own terms* in a world built to keep you hidden, and whether a thing chosen is worth more than a thing you were born or fated to. Amelia gets everything she wanted — to exist openly, to rule — and it arrives as loss: truth, in the end, changes almost nothing; the crown sits on the girl no one was allowed to see. Against a genre of destined mates and tidy triumph, *A Bond of Scale and Silver* insists that love and agency mean nothing without cost, and that being finally visible is its own kind of exile. The novel is a complete, self-contained story.
