# Voice Comp Analysis — Anne Bishop, *The Black Jewels Trilogy*

> Built 2026-07-08 from the author's epub (studied in-session; the copyrighted text is NOT
> stored in this repo — only this craft analysis). This is the grounding for `voice-dna.md`.
> We match Bishop's **craft traits**, not her world-vocabulary or tics: the author's story in
> Bishop's register, not a pastiche.

## Why this comp
The author wants the Black Jewels *feel*: the prose, the dangerous-but-devoted male lead, and
the dark intensity. There is real thematic overlap — Blood magic ranked by power, a
matriarchal court, a powerful Queen, and the "who gets to decide what you are" theme all map
onto this book's blood magyk, Queen, and outsiders.

## What actually makes Bishop's voice work (traits adopted)

1. **Plain, declarative sentences.** Her intensity is NOT ornament. "Daemon concentrated on
   breathing." "He felt her coming." "Fear hammered at him." Subject-verb, concrete, unshowy.
   This resolves the author's "too flowery" note: matching Bishop means writing *plainer*.
2. **Short punch after the long build.** She varies length and lands a short flat sentence for
   impact. Signature rhythm.
3. **Deep close-third POV.** Every scene lives fully in one head; interiority is plain thought,
   not purple narration.
4. **Emotion shown through the body, rarely named.** Held breath, locked hands, a heart
   "remembering its rhythm," bracing to stay upright.
5. **Menace through restraint.** The chilling lines are the quietest: "When the time came, the
   bed would be his." The threat is implied and understated.
6. **Figurative language rare and simple.** ~once a scene, plain ("a rabbit to Daemon's fox").
   Never clever/ornamental.
7. **Dialogue carries scenes** and is character-distinct; formal court register vs casual.
8. **Occasional adverb dialogue tags** ("he said quietly, seriously") — she is not strict about
   this; we relax the near-zero rule to "sparing."

## The dangerous-but-devoted engine (Daemon → Korvan)
Bishop splits it: the man's **actions** toward the beloved are tender and controlled while the
**narration** carries the cold, lethal undertone. The danger is literal and earned; the
restraint is the tension ("he had taken them farther than he had meant to"). Adopted for
Korvan-the-dragon — with the agency guardrail that Amelia is dangerous in her own right, so
she never becomes the rescued damsel.

## Heat register
Bishop's intimate scenes are **sensual and fairly restrained**, not hardcore — heat built
through rhythm (stacked "Kissed her until… Kissed her until…"), deep POV, emotion, and the
dangerous-devoted restraint. **Author decision:** keep Bishop's voice everywhere but write the
back-half consensual scenes **more explicitly than Bishop does** (modern spicy) — still deep
POV, still emotionally loaded, still restraint-tension, earned placement.

## Darkness register
Dark intensity via war, grief, the hunt, cost, and Loric's grief-horror — **no sexual
violence** (author decision). Bishop models the cut-away: her worst content lands through
restraint and aftermath ("He couldn't say the word 'rape.'"), never on-page spectacle. Do the
same.

## What we deliberately do NOT copy
- Her world-vocabulary (Jewels, Craft, Territories, "Hell's fire / Mother Night," Warlord
  Prince, etc.) — those are hers.
- Her specific darkness (graphic sexual abuse) — out of scope for this book.
- Any character names, ranks, or the Blood system's mechanics.
