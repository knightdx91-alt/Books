## Operations NOT Applied (and why)
- **Simile Surgery / Pattern #11 (heavy pass):** Skipped as a cut operation. Density is
  already 1.1/1k (cap 4.0; literary-clean), and the chapter's similes are the *characterizing*
  Outlander kind ("looked at me the way a man looks at a bill he wasn't expecting"; "said the
  word the way another man says *ghost*"; "the yard rearranged itself around him the way a room
  rearranges around the largest piece of furniture"), not the banned analytical/explanatory kind.
  None extend to unpack a feeling. Stripping any would over-correct a chapter already in the
  clean range. Verified all inserted prose kept similes raw (none added).
- **Irrelevant Thought Injection / Emotional Control Break / Dialogue Mess:** This is a
  fitted-silence, competence-as-courtship chapter, not a chaotic one. The Writer already carries
  the sanctioned human-noise (the cracked involuntary laugh at the horse's headstall; "I loved
  the smell of your muck-heap first"; being gossiped about by a twelve-year-old in a dead
  language). Injecting chaos would fight the tone. Emotional control already breaks on the page
  where it should (her voice going up at the ends, "it undid me more than a speech"), and Valens's
  knot/strap-tic is the intended mechanism, not something to disrupt.
- **Precision Deflation:** The precise numbers here (forty-three bales, "forty-one librae," a
  thumb's width up the arm, the horse "four, maybe five… bishoped to read seven") are
  load-bearing — they ARE the competence being witnessed and the Devoted-Reader detail. Deflating
  them would gut the anchor scene. Left intact.
- **The Missing Paragraph / The Ugly Sentence:** An ugly, rhythmically-flat sentence already
  exists ("It was the least a man has ever said to me and it undid me more than a speech."; "Huh,"
  he said). No predictable paragraph to cut — the "why he was harder to be near after" paragraph
  (p159) reads as an earned re-read reward, not a summary.
- **Negation Pattern Break:** No define-by-negation *sentence openers* found in the body (per the
  standing WATCH item, already rationed by the Writer). Nothing to break.

## Strongest Disruption
Op #1 + #2 working as a pair. The blister is not decoration: it externalizes the whole chapter's
engine — she is being *watched* while she earns the yes, and the beat is that she refuses to let
the wound show him. Paying it off on the dark climb home (with the hunger and the bread) grounds
the day's triumph in a tired, hungry, sore body, which is exactly the Gabaldon "bodily realism
punctures grandeur" move the voice-DNA calls for, and it adds the length the chapter wanted
without a syllable of padding.

Low. All three edits are additive/structural rather than tonal, and none touches the anchor,
the dialogue, or the dread of the man-in-the-good-cloak beat. The only judgment call was the

perfect, and it reads naturally in the retrospective register, but flag it for the evaluator's ear;
if it snags, "had nothing to do with any of it" is a clean fallback.
