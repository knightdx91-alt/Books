# Foundation — Land of the Guardians

Derived from `research/series-bible.md` (Part 2) and the author's recorded reading
(`research/authors-reading-prologue-ch1.md`). This is the settled ground the outline
and drafting build on. Anything marked **(DECIDE)** is an open author decision in
`STATE.yaml`.

## Logline
When their parents vanish on the morning they should have come into their magic,
two human twins flee across a world that fears magic — and collide with an elven
heir, a dragon-blooded trader, and a young shapeshifter — into the only resistance
that can stop High King Alten from consuming the last of the world's dragons.

## World: Ne'veran
- One fantasy world; **High King Alten** is the saga's unifying villain.
- Thematic spine (every book): **power, sacrifice, and consumption.**
- **Magic is feared.** Persecution is **bottom-up** — village mobs and witch-hunts,
  not top-down decree. Children can't control their abilities.
- **Men's and women's magic differ** (Wheel of Time-style), so mentors are same-gender.
- **Dragons** have disappeared; only the elves may know their fate. They are the last
  remnants of the original five elementals (one per element). When a dragon dies its
  power crystallizes into a **dragon heart**; implanting one grants that elemental
  power. **Alten hunts hearts** to accumulate power — cannibalizing the sacred legacy.

## Cast (principals)
**Human twins — Aeron & Selena, 16** *(the male twin was "Knight" in the bible / "Xavier" in the reading — both are used in the author's other books, so he is renamed **Aeron**, canon 2026-07-12.)*
- Parents fail to wake them on their 16th birthday (coming-of-age / magic unlock).
- A dagger pins a parchment to the kitchen table: **"Gorlak."** Pulling it triggers a
  hologram from their mother: if they see it, she and their father are dead; they are
  not the twins' real parents; the High King hunts the twins.
- Aeron finds a sword engraved with a **black dragon** (meaning unknown until he meets
  Reagen and his **blue-dragon** sword).
- They flee with mentors **Alverin** (for Aeron) and **Raveena** (for Selena).
- Goal: find the elves; seek allies.

**Elven twins — Katy & Reagen, 16** — children of Queen **Rosalia**.
- Katy reads the main prophecy book in the crypt-library; an **hourglass** has appeared
  beside the main-line prophecy — it has begun. Bells ring: the kingdom is invaded.
- Rosalia portals Reagen in, instructs them, sends them to find the "flying twins."
- **Reagen's blue-dragon sword** — old stories say it can call a blue dragon.
- **Rosalia's sacrifice (HARD-SET, uncuttable):** she summons the ancient cloaked
  creature bonded to her ("You know the cost"), lies on a stone slab; the whole kingdom
  falls into irreversible slumber until the true heirs reclaim the throne. A blue rose
  on her breast, "Long live the queen." The creature is the last visible **Guardian**
  (source of the title). Rosalia dies → Katy must become queen (this is the canon Son
  of None inherits). Reagen is Katy's **consort (DECIDE)**, not co-ruler; Katy is the diplomat.

**Seiss & Wolf**
- **Seiss** — apprentice trader; trains telepathic horses. His dying master passes him
  the master-trader medallion and a ship.
- **Wolf** — Seiss's best friend, head of the **Moonwalker** clan. "Wolf" is name +
  title, granted to the one *not* cursed with uncontrolled transformation (full control
  at will); others change in anger or at the full moon. At 16, remarkable control —
  possibly able to control other Moonwalkers mid-change, and feared for it.
- Arc: leave for the big city (Alten's seat), get conscripted into Alten's army,
  sabotage from within, help people escape.

## Structural spine
- **Cold open (fixed):** Alten interrogates a man — *"I know you know where they are,
  just tell me and the pain will stop."* Seeds the hunt for the twins' parents. (This is
  the prologue the author reads in `research/authors-reading-prologue-ch1.md`.)
- **Three converging storylines** (+ the villain thread), all tied to Alten.
- **Convergence:** the four groups ally into a unified resistance. Late arcs: seek the
  dragons via elven knowledge; reclaim the elven throne to wake the kingdom.
