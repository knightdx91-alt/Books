# Outline — Land of the Guardians (working / pre-architect)

A first structural pass from `foundation.md`. This is **not yet the binding
scene-by-scene roadmap** — the architect pass turns it into numbered chapters once
the open author decisions in `STATE.yaml` are resolved (especially the one-book vs.
two-volume split, which sets where this book ends).

## Act structure (three converging lines + villain thread)

### Prologue — Alten's interrogation (fixed cold open)
The author's recorded prologue. Alten and his masked torturer question a captive about
"where they are." Establishes the villain, the stakes, and the hunt for the twins' family.
→ Source prose: `research/authors-reading-prologue-ch1.md`.

### Act I — Three ordinary worlds break on the same day
- **Aeron & Selena:** the un-woken birthday; the dagger and "Gorlak"; the mother's
  hologram; the black-dragon sword; flight with Alverin and Raveena.
- **Katy & Reagen:** the prophecy hourglass; the invasion bells; Rosalia's instructions
  and **sacrifice**; the kingdom's slumber; the twins sent to find the "flying twins."
- **Seiss & Wolf:** the master's death and inheritance; the pull toward the big city;
  Wolf's dangerous control set up.

### Act II — Roads that bend toward each other
- Each pair crosses a Ne'veran that fears magic (village-mob persecution beats).
- **Black-meets-blue:** Aeron and Reagen recognize each other's dragon swords — the
  major recognition moment; the parties begin to merge.
- Seiss & Wolf infiltrate Alten's army; sabotage from within; first real cost.
- Villain thread: Alten closes on the parents / the twins; first dragon-heart escalation on-page.

### Act III — The unified resistance, and the flight
- **Convergence:** all four groups ally into one resistance.
- Turn toward the dragons (only the elves hold the knowledge); the cost of calling one
  is paid for the first time.
- **The truth about Alten:** the heroes learn he cannot be killed the normal way — his
  power and his life are the **dragon hearts grafted into his body** (see series bible
  PART 0.6). Killing his body just lets him re-knit.
- **Climax — take a heart / drive the flight:** the heroes take or shatter **one heart**,
  cracking his distributed life. Cornered and destabilized, Alten dumps the overload of
  his remaining hearts to **tear a gateway and flee** — landing in an unknown world
  (World N, Father's sacred world — he does not know it). The gateway collapses; the
  heroes hold no heart-power and **cannot follow.** They win the world but lose the man.
- **Denouement:** reclaim the elven throne / wake the kingdom; Katy crowned; the
  resistance knows Alten is loose in some other world and unfinished.

## Ending decision — ONE complete book (recommended, tentatively set)
End *Land of the Guardians* on the **flight climax + waking the kingdom** — a full,
satisfying arc for a debut/on-ramp, not a boat-ride cliffhanger. *The Dragon Isle*
becomes **Book 2** (the island, earning the dragons' trust, the twins calling their
dragons, Wolf's Moonwalker payoff) only if the drafted material earns the length.
The heart-take that drives Alten's flight can live at the end of Guardians (if one
book) or be the Dragon Isle climax (if split) — the flight itself is the fixed beat.
