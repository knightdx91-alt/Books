## Chapter 1 (draft)

Walking down the street, Nathan stopped in front of the display window for Cat's
Curiosities. The items were really a hodgepodge of items from old tarot decks to a
shrunken head. Cat's Curiosities had been here for a few years. Its owner, Cat
Williams, having bought the building when she moved to town. It was a large storefront
with a second floor that Cat lived in. Cat herself seemed an oddity to most. She was a
small mousey woman with curly black hair, green eyes that seemed full of laughter, and
she had the perfect body. In Nathan's eyes anyway. Yet everyone that had approached her
had been rebuffed, very politely. That was not going to stop Nathan of course. Nathan's
wife had left him three years ago and Cat was the first woman since his wife that he
honestly loved being around and talking to. Things always seemed better when he was with
her. That's why he was now standing in front of her shop at nine at night on a Saturday.
With a deep breath he pushed open the door and strode inside.

The inside of the shop always reminded Nathan of an antique shop or a thrift store, in
the way that you never knew what kind of items you might find in the store. Cat prided
herself on never having the same item twice, claiming that every item she had was one of
a kind in its own right. In all the hours Nathan had spent in the shop over the past six
months, he could honestly say he'd never seen a duplicate item. In Nathan's opinion, that
was one reason the shop did such good business, because not knowing if you'd ever find one
of those items that caught your eye again was a good reason to buy. Especially if the item
really called to you. Cat was sitting behind her sturdy cherry wood counter leafing through
a book catalog when he came in, the tinkling bell announcing his arrival. Cat looked up at
the sound, her eyes lighting up when she saw him. Nathan smiled at the look on her face as
she came around the counter and giving him a quick hug. "Didn't think you'd see me today
did you?" Nathan asks once they parted. Cat settled back behind her counter leaving Nathan
to grab a chair from the back. Once he was settled in front of her she replied, "No. I
didn't think you were getting back until sometime next week." Nathan shrugs, "We got done
with the job early and I wanted to get home. How have things been here?" Cat looks around at
her store and smiles, "It's been great. I've had some new stuff come in and had to let some
stuff go." Cat said the last with a touch of sadness. Nathan had asked before why she had
seemed a little sad when she sold stuff because to Nathan that was the whole reason to have a
shop. For people to buy your stuff. Yet Cat told him that everything here couldn't be
replaced and it just made her a little sad knowing she'd never see it again.

"You know," Nathan says looking around, "Even with how much we talk, I don't think I've ever
asked you how you get everything you sell. You always seem to have something new and
interesting but no one knows where you get them. Do you go to estate sales or thrift shops?"
Cat takes a sip from the porcelain cup that was still steaming on the counter, looking at him
over the rim as she did. Her eyes twinkling with mischief. "I could tell you like I tell
everyone else, that it's a trade secret, but you're more open minded than most." Cat sets the
cup down carefully. "What do you know of the multiverse?" she asks. Nathan sat back stunned,
not expecting a question like that. "Uh, not much honestly just that the theory is that there
are many different versions of our universe." Cat nods, "That's the theory, and in fact it is
correct, but there is so much more to it. Scientists say that every decision, whether big or
small creates a branching universe. I'm talking about something so small like if you chose to
wear sandals over sneakers, all the way to a universe where World War two never happened. No
one has ever been able to accurately say how many other universes there are. They don't even
try." Nathan sat back in his chair stunned. "You're saying that there really is a multiverse?"
Nathan asks. Cat nods, "Yep. Not many people are aware of it though."

Nathan sat there quietly for a few minutes thinking about her revelation when something
occurred to him. "Wait, does this mean there are versions of me in every universe?" Cat looked
amused at his question, tilting her head slightly, "My my, don't we think highly of ourselves.
To answer your question, no there aren't. Remember, they are not mirror copies of this world.
Only the truly powerful have a presence in every world. Honestly, that's better though. It
allows you to move around them easier, without getting pulled into the life you were living on
that world. I've seen plenty of people get killed that way." Nathan stared at Cat, "Why were
they killed?" Cat grimaced slightly. She had been hoping this wouldn't come up but now that it
had she had no choice but to answer. "Travelling the multiverse isn't forbidden per se," Cat
says hesitantly, "but there are rules. Not drawing unwanted attention to yourself is the biggest
one. The powers that be do not want it widely known that there is a multiverse, let alone that
there is a way to traverse them. You get one chance, if you do not heed their warning when an
emissary approaches you, then they send out a team to wipe you out." Nathan swallowed hard, "If
that's the case why are you telling me? You're violating the rule now." Cat smiles, her white
teeth a stark contrast to the jet black lipstick she wore, "Because there are things the
Watchers have forgotten. I plan on reminding them just who I am and what I can do. Among other
things." she says waving her hands as if they were not important. "Anyways, the important thing
for you to take away from all this is that yes there is a multiverse and yes you can walk it."
Cat was still smiling at him while Nathan tried to calm his mind. He had always thought there
was more out there than people believed, but there actually being a multiverse just seemed like
science fiction to him.

## What the opening establishes (for the architect)

- **Nathan** — POV. Divorced ~3 years ago; travels for work ("the job"); has spent ~6 months
  as a regular at the shop; genuinely in love with Cat, not just attracted. Ordinary-man anchor.
- **Cat Williams** — small, curly black hair, green eyes "full of laughter," jet-black lipstick,
  mischievous. Owner of **Cat's Curiosities** (curio/antique shop, lives above it). Politely
  rebuffs every suitor. Sad to sell items she "can't replace." She is clearly a multiverse
  traveler who sources her one-of-a-kind stock from other worlds — and something more: she's
  "truly powerful" (has a presence in every world), and has a personal history/grudge with **the
  Watchers** ("things the Watchers have forgotten... reminding them just who I am and what I can do").
- **The multiverse rules (canon so far):**
  - Every decision branches a universe (trivial → world-historical). Uncountable worlds.
  - Worlds are NOT mirror copies. Only the "truly powerful" have a presence in EVERY world.
  - Having no counterpart in a world lets you move through it without being "pulled into" that
    life; people who DO have counterparts there get killed that way (mechanism TBD).
  - **The Watchers / "powers that be"** police secrecy. Traversal isn't forbidden, but drawing
    attention is. One warning via an **emissary**; ignore it and a team wipes you out.
- **Tone:** grounded contemporary/urban fantasy; slow-burn reveal via conversation; romance thread.

## Open questions the draft leaves (architect / author)

- What makes Cat "truly powerful"? What is she, really?
- How does traversal physically work (the shop's items as anchors/keys?)? Does Nathan get to travel?
- The "pulled into the life you were living" death mechanism — how does it work?
- Where is the story going — is Nathan drawn into Cat's campaign against the Watchers? Genre lane
  (cozy-ish urban fantasy vs. escalating multiverse thriller)?
