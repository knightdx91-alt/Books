Hazel, named that by her father because of her constantly changing hazel eyes, was late. Which in and of itself was not a new occurrence, but this time it honestly wasn't her fault. You see, after stepping off the 9:15 bus heading to silver street, she had seen a guy that looked really familiar so she called to him. "Hey! Don't I know you?" The guy turned, as if surprised to see her and took off running. Now, normally, this is the part where most people would just shrug and go about their day, and on another day that's what she would have done, except she saw him melt into the shadows as if they stuck to him. finding this way more interesting than a 10 am calculus class, she takes off after him. Having run track in high school and running most mornings, Hazel kept in pretty good shape, so she shouldn't have had much trouble keeping up with him except he seemed to jump between the shadows in the alleyways. Taking a chance, she flying tackles him, but instead of hitting the brick wall behind him they both tumble through the shadow, appearing on another sidewalk. Still holding on to him, Hazel looks around bewildered, "What the hell?" she says. "I'll appreciate it if you'd get your knee out of my side," a voice below her says. looking down she finally gets a good look at the guy she had been chasing.

# DEVELOPED SPINE — LOCKED (author decisions, 2026-07-13)

The architect builds the full blueprint on top of this. These are settled; do not contradict.

## Premise engine — shadows are crossings between PARALLEL WORLDS
- Shadows are crossing-points between **parallel universes**. A rare few can step *into* a shadow
  in one world and *out* of a shadow in another. This is **NOT** a secret society tucked inside our
  world — it is literally **another universe reached through shadow**.
- **The only limit is RARITY.** Only the **shadow-blooded** can cross. There is **NO physical or
  mental cost**, **NO predators/entities in the dark**, no burn, no toll — crossing is **clean and
  safe**. The worlds stay secret purely because so few can walk between them.
  - **DESIGN CONSEQUENCE (binding on the architect):** because travel is frictionless, the story's
    stakes/teeth must be loaded onto **(a) the destination world's conflict** and **(b) the mystery
    of Hazel's origin** — NOT onto the mechanic. Do not invent a hidden cost to manufacture tension;
    put the tension in the plot and the parallel world.

## Hazel (POV)
- College runner (ex-track, keeps in shape), ordinary contemporary-world life. Named for her
  **constantly changing hazel eyes** — which are secretly the **outward tell of latent shadow-blood**
  (no one, including Hazel, ever knew what the shifting eyes meant).
- She is **latent shadow-blood herself** — unknowing. She has never crossed until she flying-tackles
  the stranger through a shadow; the act **awakens her dormant ability**.

## The shadow-walker (love interest — name TBD, architect proposes)
- A **shadow-blooded native of the parallel world**, in our world for his own reason.
- He looks **"maddeningly familiar"** to Hazel because **they are the same kind** — shadow-blood
  recognizes shadow-blood (a pull/resonance she misreads as familiarity).
- **Romance: yes, he is the romantic lead.** **Slow-burn co-lead** — a strong B-plot braided with the
  mystery/danger (Kate Daniels texture), not the sole engine.
