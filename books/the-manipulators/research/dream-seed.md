# The Manipulators — Dream Seed (raw origin material)

> Source: the author's dream, transcribed 2026-07-12. This is the ORIGIN SEED for
> the book, not chapter one. It is dream-logic — vivid images, compressed exposition.
> The architect reverse-engineers a consistent world, power-system, and story from it.
> Prose here is NOT canon voice; it is a mood/idea reference. Do not paste it into the
> manuscript.

## Core images the dream delivered (the parts worth keeping)

- A man (**Kevin**) wakes strapped to a dentist-style chair, head bandaged, amnesiac.
- A rescuer (**Jake**) — long blond hair, green eyes, black tee, cargo pants — kills a
  scalpel-wielding doctor with a surgical tray and frees Kevin.
- **The shifting building**: corridors and walls reform as fast as they're destroyed.
  A "race against the building itself." Guards appear inside hallways that didn't exist
  seconds earlier. This is the standout set piece.
- Jake can **fly** and blast walls (via a strange **watch** on his wrist?).
- Escape into an exercise yard; sirens; doctors + guards with rifles flood in.
- Jake flies off and tells Kevin the only way out is to fly — Kevin must remember how.
  Kevin runs, "hops like an idiot," is cornered, closes his eyes, jumps —
- The jump detonates: an explosion craters the yard, throws the guards, and Kevin wakes
  **floating in the stratosphere**, Earth curved beneath him, breathing fine.
- Reunion in the sky. Jake explains the mythology (dream dumped it all at once):

### The mythology the dream handed over (to be rebuilt, not necessarily kept verbatim)

- **Kevin Jones**, 22, no family. Met Jake six years ago in **Panama City**.
- **Kevin's power is unique**: he can **absorb other people's powers and then give
  those powers to someone else.** He took **The Reach**'s power (a beggar-gang leader
  with shallow mind-control / suggestion) and used it to save Jake; Kevin later *gave*
  Jake abilities (flight) so Jake could survive. Jake "used to be normal."
- Powered people exist — "not common, not uncommon."
- Kevin vanished two weeks ago; Jake searched, found him in **a facility**.
- The facility is one place where **they try to create Manipulators** — people who can
  **bend reality** (to a limited extent). The shifting hallways were Manipulator work.
- Manipulators are **bound by rules** — maybe imposed by the organization, maybe by
  "some kind of cosmic force." Nobody's sure which.
- Ends with Kevin resolving to go back down for the answers to his past.
  "This was only the beginning."

## Open questions the dream did NOT answer (for the architect / author)

- **The power system's exact rules** (the engine of the whole saga):
  - Does absorbing a power REMOVE it from the original owner, or copy it?
  - Can Kevin hold more than one power at once? A limit?
  - When he *gives* a power away, does he keep it? Lose it? Is the gift permanent?
  - Range, contact, consent — how does absorption physically happen?
  - What is Kevin's OWN baseline power vs. borrowed ones? (Flight was clearly available
    to him — did he give it to himself earlier, or is it his?)
  - The cost. Every good power system taxes its user. What does stealing/giving cost Kevin?
- **The Manipulators' binding rules** — concrete, learnable, exploitable by the plot.
- **Why the amnesia** — mechanism (head wound? the facility? a power?) AND purpose.
- **Jake's watch** — device, or just a dream prop? Is his flight from Kevin's gift?
- **The organization** — name, goals, scale, why they want manufactured reality-benders.
- **The theme underneath the action** — what the saga is actually about.
