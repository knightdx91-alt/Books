# Voice DNA — LitRPG — James Mason (Book 1)

> Executable voice spec. The Writer writes FROM this; Dialogue-Polish and Hook-Craft check
> AGAINST it; the Editor/Evaluator/Disruptor enforce it. Calibrated to the drafted prose in
> `research/source-notes.md` (the canon voice benchmark) and to progression-fantasy commercial
> conventions. Genre: LitRPG / progression fantasy (self-insertion primary).

---

## 1. GLOBAL NARRATIVE VOICE

**POV / tense:** Third-person limited on James, past tense (matches drafted prose). Occasional
close-in free-indirect thought without italics for most, italics reserved for Dapple's mind-speech
and system text.
**Vocabulary level:** 3/10 — plain, contemporary American. The ONE deliberate register shift is
**system text**, which is clinical, terse, bracketed — a flat second voice.
**Sentence rhythm:** Mixed, leaning short. Action clips to fragments; wonder gets one or two longer
breathing sentences; system screens are bulleted/bracketed and flat. No long subordinated periods.
**Formality:** Casual-conversational. Contractions throughout. Profanity sparse and *reactive* only
("oh fuck this" when startled, never as decoration).
**Humor presence:** Frequent but DRY — deadpan understatement, James-arguing-with-menus, Dapple's
sass. Never quippy ensemble banter; jokes are deflections, not performances.
**Emotional register:** Restrained by default. Grief is WITHHELD and leaks through objects/smells,
never named. Wonder is allowed to be sincere — do NOT ironize the good parts; the reader came for the
climb.
**Distinctive features:**
1. **The deadpan-man / delighted-wolf duet** — the core texture of the book.
2. **System text as a genuine second narrator** — flat, literal, sometimes the joke, sometimes the
   reveal.
3. **Legible progression** — stats, levels, class math rendered CLEAN. The artistry is in pacing the
   reveals, never in obscuring numbers with prose.
4. **Sideways grief** — the biggest feelings arrive through the smallest concrete things.
**Anti-patterns (the narrator NEVER):**
- names James's feelings ("a wave of grief," "he felt afraid");
- lets James wax lyrical about his own competence;
- gives Dapple idioms a wolf couldn't plausibly know;
- turns a stat-screen into a metaphor or a stat-block into purple prose;
- front-loads lore the scene didn't ask for (every rule attaches to something seen/smelled/done);
- competence cascade (James solving everything smoothly) — his wins are uneven, costly, often lucky.

**VOICE UNDER PRESSURE (mandatory):**
- **James overwhelmed** (grief, terror, the vortex, near-death): the prose goes **flat and literal —
  it retreats INTO the mechanical.** He counts things, reads the system text instead of feeling,
  narrates objects (the pen, the boarded window, the number on the screen). Sentences shorten to
  fragments; humor cuts out entirely; he gets MORE precise and LESS human — until one small sensory
  detail (a smell, a warmth, a weight) breaks through and the fragment finally lands. That break is
  the only "emotion" the page shows.
- **Dapple overwhelmed** inverts: she gets **louder, faster, more insulting, more manic** — never
  quieter. When she DOES go silent (Ch.11, Ch.21), it is the loudest thing in the book, precisely
  because it breaks her pattern.
- **Wren overwhelmed:** she **laughs at the wrong moment** and can't stop, and buries fear in
  logistics ("we'll grieve later, we've got a floor").

## 2. PER-CHARACTER VOICE CARDS

#### James Mason (protagonist / POV)
- **Vocabulary band:** working American; trades/logistics plainness; no fantasy vocabulary he
  wouldn't have (he calls things by Earth names until corrected).
- **Syntax fingerprint:** short declaratives; blunt practical questions; clips to fragments under
  stress; rarely subordinates.
- **Rhythm:** statements and blunt questions; almost never exclaims except reactive profanity.
- **Verbal tics:** "Uh—" as a stall; deadpan understatement at the worst moment ("Uh, nice
  squirrel"); argues with system menus literally; asks "How exactly…?" / "So what's the catch?"
- **Never says:** anything lyrical about himself; therapy-speak about his own feelings; fantasy
  jargon he hasn't been taught; a sincere boast.
- **Sample line:** "There's no lock. There's no hinge I can see. And it still won't open. I hate
  this box."

#### Dapple (bonded wolf cub, Ancient — mind-speech, italicized) [CANON VOICE — do not alter]
- **Vocabulary band:** young, breezy, slangy; wolf-literal — describes via SMELL and pack-logic;
  no human idioms she couldn't know (says "smells wrong," not "seems off").
- **Syntax fingerprint:** short, bouncy, exclamatory; mock-incredulous questions; one sudden grave
  short sentence when it counts.
- **Rhythm:** fast, interrupting, tangential (derails onto smells); questions used to mock.
- **Verbal tics:** "Duh." / "yep." / "uh huh." ; incredulous "You've never heard of the system?!" ;
  ranks smells unprompted.
- **Never says:** human sayings ("piece of cake," "read the room"); anything solemn for long;
  admits fear directly (she gets louder instead).
- **Sample line:** *"Duh, all enlightened animals can. The guild smells like old fear and good soup,
  by the way. Can wolves not talk where you're from?"*

#### Wren (ally / party scout-archer — NOT a love interest)
- **Vocabulary band:** guild-registered climber; quotes regs and beast-stats from memory; gambler's
  slang; broke-person's practicality.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** rapid, clipped, comma-light; imperatives; numbers and odds in her speech.
- **Rhythm:** fast statements; rhetorical questions she answers herself; deflects sincerity into
  logistics.
- **Verbal tics:** recites rules ("Floor cap's four, waiver's standard, exit's one-way up"); a
  wrong-moment laugh under stress; names her knives.
- **Never says:** anything that sounds like flirting; a slow sentimental sentence held to the end
  (she cuts sincerity off with a task); "we can't."
- **Sample line:** "Rare class means untested. Untested gets me killed. Convince me you'll hold a
  line that isn't yours to hold, and you're in."

#### Master Orin (dojo instructor, Hallowmere — secondary)
- **Vocabulary band:** ex-climber, economical, technical about bodies and forms.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** imperatives; refuses to explain "why."
- **Verbal tics:** "Again. No. Again." ; re-oils clean weapons mid-sentence.
- **Never says:** praise he hasn't decided you earned; his own fear out loud.
- **Sample line:** "Ancient class. So what. Show me you can stand up twice."

#### Guildmaster Thessaly (Hallowmere guild head — secondary)
- **Vocabulary band:** formal, bureaucratic, careful; buries warnings in procedure.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** hedged, subordinated, longer than anyone else's — the one measured voice.
- **Verbal tics:** pours tea she never drinks; answers a dangerous question with a form.
- **Never says:** a plain warning; your grandfather's name without lowering her voice.
- **Sample line:** "Registration's routine. The title, less so. Sit — no, sit — and let me pour
  before you say that name again."

## 3. VOICE DIFFERENTIATION MATRIX (cover-the-name test, pre-solved)

| Character | Sharpest distinguisher | Contrast vs nearest character |
|---|---|---|
| **James** | deadpan understatement + argues literally with menus; never lyrical about himself | vs Wren: James is *slow, blunt, dry*; Wren is *fast, clipped, odds-quoting* |
| **Dapple** | italic mind-speech, smell-taxonomy, "Duh," wolf-literal, no human idioms | vs everyone: only non-human voice; only one who ranks smells and can't use sayings |
| **Wren** | rapid rule/odds recitation + wrong-moment laugh; deflects into logistics | vs James: both deflect, but James deflects with *silence/dryness*, Wren with *tasks/numbers* |
| **Orin** | terse imperatives, "Again. No. Again.", won't explain why | vs Thessaly: Orin is *shortest*, Thessaly is *longest/most subordinated* |
| **Thessaly** | hedged bureaucratic longueurs; buries warnings in procedure; untouched tea | vs Orin: opposite pole — most formal/measured voice in the book |
| **System text** | bracketed/fenced, clinical, flat, literal; no affect | vs all: the only non-character "voice"; never uses contractions or emotion |

If Writer output blurs James↔Wren (the closest pair, both deflectors), sharpen: James goes *quieter
and drier*; Wren goes *faster and more numeric*.

## 4. ANTI-PATTERN BUDGET (per 1,000 words — Disruptor enforces; matches style_check.py)

- **Explanatory/analytical similes** (Pattern #11, the #1 AI fingerprint): **≤4 / 1k** (commercial
  band; style gate hard limit). Prefer concrete image over "like a X, as if Y."
- **Em-dashes:** ≤~10 / 1k (style gate).
- **Adverbs in dialogue tags:** near-zero (drafted prose already uses "said/asks" plainly — keep it).
- **"It was as if / almost as though":** ≤1 / 1k.
- **Metacognitive narration** ("she realized she was realizing," "he noticed himself noticing"):
  ≤0.5 / 1k — effectively banned; James never narrates his own cognition this way.
- **Emotional temperature reports** ("a wave of sadness washed over him," "fear gripped her"):
  **0 — banned.** This is the single most important cut for THIS book; grief/fear are shown through
  objects, smells, fragments, and the flat-under-pressure voice, never reported.
- **Competence-cascade sentences** (hero smoothly solving in sequence): watch and break; wins must be
  uneven, costly, or lucky.
- **Lore front-loading:** every rule must attach to an on-page action, sight, or smell — no
  free-floating exposition paragraphs.
- **NO cross-chapter repeated phrase** (style gate) — add deliberate motifs to `tools/style_check.py`
  ALLOWLIST: "Legend's Progeny", "Threshold-Walker", "Beast Tamer", "iron-hide wolf cub", "the
  system", "shared stat", stat names, "conditions not met", "wolfy grin", plus recurring motif lines
  once finalized (the box/door image, "smells like the box did", the coffee refrain).

## 5. BENCHMARK SAMPLES (canonical targets)

Until a formal voice-bank/ folder is populated, the drafted prose in `research/source-notes.md` IS
the benchmark. Three canonical target passages, by mode:

- **CONTROLLED (global default voice):** drafted pg.5–6 — the "status" reveal and stat block. James
  dry and literal, Dapple sassy, system text clean. This is the baseline everyone measures against.
- **BREAKING (voice under pressure):** to be written to spec in Ch.11 (the spice-stall grief) and
  Ch.24 (the climax) — the flat-mechanical-then-one-sensory-break pattern. Benchmark seed: drafted
  pg.4, the vortex blackout, where James narrates his locked muscles and the swirling symbol instead
  of his fear.
- **IRRELEVANT THOUGHT / human noise:** to be written to spec — James's coffee grievance mid-wonder
  (Ch.5) and Dapple's smell-ranking derailing an exposition beat (Ch.3/5). Benchmark seed: drafted
  pg.7, "Uh, nice squirrel," — reactive deadpan noise in a tense beat.

> `sample-02-breaking.md`, `sample-03-irrelevant-thought.md` and add 7–12 more short targets, ≥3
