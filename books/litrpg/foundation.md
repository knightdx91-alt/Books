## 0. AUTHOR DECISIONS — PROVISIONAL (author may override any of these)

Two decisions were left open in STATE.yaml. I have made both so the book can be built.
**Both are flagged; changing them ripples mainly into lore chapters (Ch.9–10) and the
series shape, not the drafted opening.**

**DECISION A — the grandfather's origin & the portal lineage.**
*Chosen:* Grandpa Richard(s) **was originally from Seminova**. There he was a legend known
as **Corin Vale, "the Threshold-Walker"** — one of the very few outside the sanctioned
**Portal Mage's Guild** ever to wield a portal ability, because his own growth class let
him **acquire (copy/steal) abilities** from those he defeated or bonded. He acquired the
forbidden portal ability, crossed to Earth, and hid. He tried to raise James's father into
the inheritance; the father **refused Seminova** (the source of the falling-out) and stayed
human. So the portal ability — meant to pass to the father — was sealed in the **card inside
the box**, keyed to bloodline, and set to trigger only when both Corin and the father were
dead. James is the Legend's Progeny by blood; the title is literally *inherited*, not earned.
*Why:* it makes the whole cold open load-bearing (the box, the sword, the artifacts, the
"borrowed time" line, the falling-out, the "come here and open this" instruction all pay off),
and it gives James a personal mystery engine that runs parallel to the tower ladder.
*Override lever:* if the author prefers the grandfather to be **Earth-born** and to have
**stumbled onto** the portal ability, keep everything except swap "native legend" for
"the man who found the door" — the emotional beats survive; only Ch.9 lore changes.

**DECISION B — tower structure: one tower per book, or more?**
*Chosen:* **ONE tower per book.** Book 1 = the **Strength Tower** (first in the fixed order:
STR → VIT → AGI → WIS → INT → CHA; LUCK excluded), located two towns over from where James
lands. Book 1 spends ~65% of its length on *becoming someone who can attempt a tower at all*
(bond, class, town, guild, dojo, family history, first hunts, the road) and ~35% on the tower
itself. Each subsequent book escalates one tower / one stat / one home-truth about his family.
*Why:* 110k is not enough to rush a tower and still land the isekai grounding, the guild, the
dojo, the ally, and the grandfather mystery with any weight. One tower per book gives each
volume a clean spine, a distinct "stat flavor," and a natural cliff. It also protects the
NO-ROMANCE guardrail — the ally (Wren) gets a full book to become a real friend without the
plot needing to manufacture pairing pressure.
*Override lever:* if the author wants **two towers in Book 1** (STR + VIT), cut the family-
history and second-hunt breathing chapters (Ch.9, Ch.13) to summaries and compress the dojo
arc; the book gets faster and thinner. I recommend against it for Book 1.

## 0.5 MACRO-STRUCTURE (selected — full rationale lives in `outline.md`)

**Chosen structure: a five-movement PROGRESSION LADDER, not three acts.** LitRPG/progression
fantasy is natively a *rising-rung* form — the reader's pleasure is the staircase itself
(bond → class → level → title → guild → hunt → road → tower). Forcing that into three acts
with quarter-mark turns fights the genre and produces the machine-made shape. Instead the book
is built as **five uneven movements keyed to the world's own gates**, with turning points
deliberately jittered and chapter lengths varying ~1,800–6,800 words.

- **Movement I — The Card** (Earth / threshold), ~11%
- **Movement II — First Blood** (grounding in Seminova), ~14%
- **Movement III — Roots** (training, family history, first hunts), ~24%
- **Movement IV — The Road** (leaving for the tower, the ally), ~19%
- **Movement V — The Strength Tower** (the trial; chapters shorten as tension climbs), ~28%

Turning points (jittered, as % of book): **isekai threshold ~9%**, **enters guild society ~24%**,
**learns his blood is the plot ~48%**, **commits to the tower / meets Wren ~66%**, **tower
climax ~92%**. Sections are printed as **PART ONE … PART FIVE** (movement titles), not "ACT".
26 chapters, Σ ≈ **115,000 words** (budget check in `outline.md`).

## 1. PREMISE

**Structural premise:** *James Mason must climb seven towers to get home, but the ability that
could open the door home is the same forbidden inheritance that got his family hunted — and the
farther he climbs, the more he wants to stay.*

- **Irony engine:** the way out is the reason he was pulled in. His only route home (the portal
  ability) is locked behind exactly the trials, guilds, and enemies that make Seminova worth not
  leaving. The tool of escape is the hook that keeps him.
- **Escalation ladder (Book 1):** he wants to go home → to go home he must climb (and to climb he
  must become someone Seminova claims) → the more he climbs, the more he learns home was never
  safe and the grandfather he trusted engineered all of this.
- **Central question (Book 1 spine):** *Is James climbing to get back to a life he lost — or to
  become the person his grandfather always meant him to be?* (The seven-book question: *would he
  choose Seminova over Earth if the door finally opened?* — not answered here.)

**Concept Pitch Test:** "A guy inherits a locked box from his estranged grandfather, opens it at
the funeral, and gets dropped into a game-world where a talking wolf cub tells him he's the heir
of a legend everyone was hunting." Passes — instantly retellable, the box + the funeral + the
talking wolf are all shareable. **Risk flag:** the *middle* (dojo/training) is where progression
fantasy loses readers; the outline front-loads a hook per chapter and keeps the grandfather
mystery burning under the training so no chapter is "just leveling."

**Engagement type (from STATE, confirmed):**
```
engagement_type:
  primary:   self_insertion (55%)   # reader IS James — competent-but-ordinary man, wish-fulfillment climb
  secondary: fascination (30%)      # the system's rules, the grandfather mystery, the ancient class
  tertiary:  empathy (15%)          # fresh grief, the Dapple bond, real friendship with no romance
```
**Tension to manage:** self-insertion wants James blank and lucky; empathy wants him specific and
grieving. Resolution — keep his *competence* aspirational (reader lives the climb) but keep his
*grief and his flaws* specific (reader feels the man). The wish-fulfillment is the world; the
humanity is the wound. Never let the power fantasy erase the fact that he buried his parents on
page one.

## 2. THEME AS QUESTION

**Primary:** *When the life you lost was quietly a lie, is starting over an escape or a betrayal?*
James's Earth life (parents, the greyhound trips, the "normal") turns out to have been a
deliberately constructed hiding place. Every level he gains is also a step away from grieving
the people who built that hiding place to protect him.

**Secondary (genre-native):** *Does strength you were handed (an ancient class, an inherited
title, a bond you didn't earn) count as strength at all?* James starts overpowered by lineage
and luck; the book keeps asking whether he can back up what he was given.

**Theme presence rule:** present in ~80% of chapters; **RECEDES** deliberately in Ch.5 (pure
world-wonder), Ch.11 (raw grief, no thesis), and Ch.19 (clean tower-mechanics fun). Saturation
would make the leveling feel like a sermon.

## 3. CHARACTERS

> Genre profile: **Commercial / progression fiction** → lighter chaos sufficient (2–3 markers per
> major character). James and Wren get fuller profiles because they carry the empathy load;
> Dapple gets full chaos because a talking bond-animal is the book's charm engine and must never
> flatten into an exposition dispenser.

### James Mason — protagonist

**Surface:** Late-20s American, some kind of steady trades/logistics job, only child, competent
and self-reliant, the guy who "has no one to lean on but himself" and is quietly proud of it.
**Wound:** Raised by parents who *vanished for a week at a time on whims* and a grandfather full
of locked rooms and unanswered questions — James grew up loved but never *told* things. He learned
that the people he trusts keep secrets, and that asking gets you "you'll understand one day."
**Lie:** *If I stay self-sufficient enough, I won't be blindsided again.* (So he under-asks, under-
leans, and treats needing people as exposure.)
**Want (external):** Get home. Undo the isekai. Return to the one life he understood.
**Need (internal):** Let people in *before* he's forced to — accept a bond, a party, a found
family — and grieve the parents instead of solving them.
**Arc:** From "I do this alone and I'll figure the exit myself" → to letting Dapple, Wren, and a
guild actually *carry* part of him — and to admitting, at the tower's top, that part of him doesn't
want the exit. He does NOT fully resolve; Book 1 gets him to *let one person in* (Wren) and *stop
treating Dapple as a tool*.
**Voice markers:** dry, deadpan, deflects fear with a flat joke ("Uh, nice squirrel"); asks blunt
practical questions ("How exactly am I supposed to do that?"); swears when startled, not when
angry ("oh fuck this"); narrates his own competence sparingly, mostly notices what he *doesn't*
understand. Sentence length short-to-medium; rarely lyrical.
**Contradiction:** ferociously independent, yet the thing that undoes him every time is *someone
being kind to him without wanting anything back* — a stray gift wrecks him faster than a threat.

--- CHARACTER CHAOS (3 markers) ---
- **Irrelevant obsession:** James has strong, unshakeable opinions about **coffee** and the fact
  that Seminova has no coffee genuinely offends him. He rates every substitute against a remembered
  gas-station dark roast. Surfaces ~3x, never plot-relevant. (Re-read seed: the coffee was his
  dad's, not his — see Re-read Architecture.)
- **Cognitive distortion:** **sunk-cost + over-literalism** — he finishes things he's decided to
  do even when they've stopped making sense (he keeps the greyhound instead of driving; he'll
  clear a tower floor "properly" when retreat is smarter), and he takes system text *maddeningly
  literally*, arguing with menus.
- **Failed emotional management:** the grief ambushes him at **wrong, mundane moments** — he's fine
  at the funeral and then breaks down because a Seminovan market stall smells like his mother's
  kitchen (Ch.11). He can't cry on cue; it arrives sideways.

### CANON — James's class: **Beast Tamer (Ancient)** growth class (verbatim, do not alter)

> From drafted source pg.8. This exact name and stat block are canon — never genericize to
> "his ancient growth class."
> - **Class name:** **Beast Tamer (Ancient)** — a *growth class*.
> - **Stat growth:** **+2 INT, +1 STR, +1 CHA per level.**
> - **Ability:** bond any number of creatures up to your class rarity; may have only ONE creature of
>   a rarity ABOVE yours; the beast **may decline** the bond; the bond is two-way, so the **HIGHER**
>   of tamer/beast stat is **shared equally**, capped at a max of **five** beasts.
> - **Growth-class unlock:** must bond a **rare-or-higher** beast **before gaining your first level**;
>   the bonded beast's rarity **sets the class rarity**. (Dapple is Ancient → James's Beast Tamer came
>   back Ancient.)
> - Bonding grants innate beast-knowledge (hunting, society structure) and the **beta mark** (the
>   wolf's-head tattoo on his right hand) designating Dapple as beta over future beasts and a summons.

### Dapple — the bonded iron-hide wolf cub (Ancient), James's beta

> **CANON, do not alter:** iron-hide wolf cub, **ancient** rarity, **female**, speaks mind-to-mind
> in "a young excited female voice," rolls her eyes, "wolfy grin," calls James on his ignorance,
> gets the wolf's-head tattoo (his beta mark). She is the reason his class unlocked at Ancient.

**Surface:** a cub. Small, fluffy, sad-eyed when she wants something, obnoxiously delighted the
rest of the time. Reads as comic relief; is not.
**Wound / secret weight:** an *ancient*-rarity beast bonding a level-0 nobody is bizarre — she
chose James on instinct the instant he appeared, and she doesn't fully know why either. There's a
grief under her cheer she deflects harder than James deflects his.
**Want:** to grow, to be the strongest beta, to *not be treated like a pet* — and, quietly, to
never be left behind again.
**Voice markers:** breezy, slangy, uses "Duh" and "yep" and mock-incredulity; explains the world
like the reader is dim but affectionately; sudden gravity in one short sentence when it counts.
Never uses human idioms she couldn't know (she says "smells" where a person says "seems").
**Contradiction:** the most powerful being in the early book, packaged as the least serious.

--- CHARACTER CHAOS (full, 4 markers) ---
- **Irrelevant obsession:** **smell taxonomy.** She narrates the world by scent and ranks smells on
  a private hierarchy nobody asked about ("the guild smells like old fear and good soup"). Recurs.
- **Cognitive distortion:** **wolf-logic literalism** — applies pack/predator rules to human social
  situations and is baffled when they don't hold; assumes hierarchy where humans see friendship.
- **Unprompted memory:** flashes of *before James* — a den, other cubs, a warmth she won't name —
  surface at odd times (3x, mutating). Seeds why an ancient cub was alone in a field. (Series thread.)
- **Failed emotional management:** she overshoots into manic play or goes dead-silent; when scared
  she gets *louder and more insulting*, never quieter.

### Wren — the ally who parties up for the Strength Tower (NOT a love interest — hard guardrail)

> Author canon: "meets a girl who parties up with him for the first tower." STATE guardrail: **ally
> / party member, NOT romance.** Wren is written as a *friend and a foil* — the sibling-energy
> partner, the person who makes James better at being a person, full stop.

**Surface:** a wiry, fast-talking local scout/archer trainee a couple years younger than James,
already guild-registered, chronically broke, trying to assemble a party good enough to survive
Floor 1 because she *has* to clear the STR tower for reasons of her own.
**Wound:** she comes from a family that climbs — older sibling went into a tower and didn't come
out. She's climbing partly to finish what they started and partly to prove the tower can be beaten.
**Lie:** *If I'm useful enough / fast enough, no one I party with dies.* (Over-controls, over-plans,
takes point so no one else has to risk it.)
**Want:** clear Floor 1, get the reward, keep everyone alive.
**Need:** to let a teammate (James) carry weight — the exact mirror of James's arc, which is why
they click without romance: they're each other's *lesson*, not each other's *prize*.
**Arc:** learns to trust James's ancient-class oddness and stop solo-managing risk.
**Voice markers:** rapid, clipped, gambler's cadence; quotes guild regs and beast-stats from
memory; deflects sincerity with logistics ("we'll grieve later, we've got a floor").
**Contradiction:** meticulous planner who bets recklessly on people the moment she decides to trust.

--- CHARACTER CHAOS (3 markers) ---
- **Irrelevant obsession:** collects and re-sharpens a set of mismatched **knives** she names; has
  opinions about edges no one asked for.
- **Cognitive distortion:** **mind-reading** — assumes she knows what a teammate will do and plans
  around the imagined version, sometimes wrongly.
- **Failed emotional management:** laughs at the wrong moment — cracks jokes when someone nearly
  dies, can't stop, hates herself for it after.

### SECONDARY / MINI PROFILES

#### Master Orin — the dojo/training-center instructor in Hallowmere (MINI)
**Role:** the gruff ex-adventurer Dapple pushes James to train under; teaches James his own body.
**Their OWN problem:** his knees and reflexes are going; he's a former mid-tower climber quietly
terrified of the day he can't demonstrate a form. Runs the dojo because he can't climb anymore.
**One irrational behavior:** re-racks and re-oils weapons that are already clean, mid-conversation,
without noticing he's doing it.
**Voice marker:** speaks in imperatives and refuses to explain *why* until you've done it wrong
three times ("Again. No. Again.").

#### Guildmaster Thessaly — the Hallowmere adventurers' guild head (MINI)
**Role:** registers James, recognizes the **Legend's Progeny** title, and becomes the gatekeeper of
what James is allowed to know about his grandfather.
**Their OWN problem:** she knew Corin Vale — or knew *of* him — and carries a political debt/danger
about admitting a Threshold-Walker's blood walked into her guild. She's managing a risk James can't
see.
**One irrational behavior:** answers dangerous questions by pouring tea she never drinks.
**Voice marker:** formal, hedged, buries the real warning inside guild bureaucracy.

#### Corin Vale ("the Threshold-Walker") — the dead grandfather (present via memory / lore)
**Role:** the absent architect of the entire plot; James's every flashback of "grandpa richards"
is now doubled — the man who built secret rooms for a boy to find was also hiding a world.
**Their OWN problem (in memory):** guilt — he stole the inheritance choice from James's father and
then from James, telling himself it was protection.
**Voice marker (in remembered dialogue):** warm, riddling, "you'll understand then" — a man who
loved by withholding.

> - **The box that won't open for anyone but him** → pays off as *the door home that opens for no

> - **A gift given without a price** (the nova car, the beta mark, Wren splitting a reward) →
>   James's undoing, seeded early, detonated at the tower's top.
> - **Smell as memory** (Dapple's scent-world; his mother's kitchen in a market) → the sideways
>   grief mechanism.

## 4b. SHAREABLE MOMENTS (mapped ≥4)
1. **Ch.2–3:** the funeral, then the box opens *at* the funeral house and swallows him — "he opened
   his dead grandfather's box and it ate him." (hook)
2. **Ch.4/8:** "his starter class came back **Ancient** rarity and even his talking wolf had never
   seen that." (the wish-fulfillment brag that's still a mystery)
3. **Ch.9:** "turns out his estranged grandfather was a *legend* in the game-world, and James's
   quiet family was a witness-protection program." (the reframe)
4. **Ch.24:** the tower's reward is *a choice*, and the choice is the first crack of the door home —
   and James hesitates. (the irony engine made visible)

## 4c. EMOTIONAL RESIDUE (designed backward from Ch.26)
Ten minutes after finishing, the reader should be **restless with a divided loyalty they didn't
expect to feel** — rooting for James to get home *and* quietly hoping he won't, because Seminova
(Dapple, Wren, the climb, the grandfather he's finally getting to know in reverse) has become the
better life. The last chapter deposits this by giving James his first real *sight* of the way home
and letting him not reach for it yet.

## 4d. CULTURAL VOCABULARY
- **"Legend's Progeny"** (canon title) — designed to become the reader's shorthand for *inherited
  strength you haven't earned yet*. Introduced Ch.6 as flavor, weaponized Ch.9 (it's literal), owned
  by the reader by Ch.26.
- **"Threshold-Walker"** (grandfather's epithet, Decision A) — the book's phrase for someone who
  doesn't belong to either side of a door. Seeded Ch.9, becomes James's own likely epithet.

## 4e. IDENTITY EFFECT
Reader feels: *"I'm the kind of person who'd figure the system out and do it right"* — the
competence/self-insertion flatter — PLUS *"I'd want to earn it, not just be handed it,"* which is
exactly James's secondary theme. Reinforced every 3–4 chapters at each progression gate.

## 4f. RE-READ ARCHITECTURE (plant 4)
1. **The coffee obsession** (Ch.5) reads as a quirk; after Ch.9 it lands — the gas-station dark
   roast was his *father's* ritual on the greyhound trips, and the trips were his parents *checking
   the doors between worlds*. His grief has been hiding in a beverage.
2. **The black-bladed sword on the office wall** (Ch.2, drafted canon) is background dressing; after
   Ch.9 it's Corin's Threshold-Walker weapon and a Book-2 key — James walked past his inheritance.
3. **Dapple "saw you appear"** and bonded instantly (drafted canon, Ch.3) reads as luck; her
   unprompted den-memories (Ch.10/21) reframe it — she was *waiting* in that field. (Series thread.)
4. **Thessaly's untouched tea** (Ch.6) reads as manner; after her Ch.9 admission it's the tell that
   she was frightened of James from the moment he said his grandfather's name.

## 5. SYMBOL & MOTIF SYSTEM
> Keep 30–40% of description as pure TEXTURE. Only these four are load-bearing.

**The Box / the Door**
- First: Ch.2, the medium box with the gold inlaid symbol, opens for no one but James.
- Evolution: the card → the swirling vortex → the towers as "doors" → the portal ability as the
  ultimate locked door.
- Final (Ch.24/26): the tower reward cracks the first door home; the thing that only opens for him.
- Theme link: what opens only for you is both gift and trap.

**Scent / breath (Dapple's world)**
- First: Ch.3, "you smell different. Strong." Evolution: scent as truth-detector and as grief-
  trigger (mother's kitchen, Ch.11). Final: Ch.24 the guardian "smells like the box did" — the
  bloodline resonance. Theme link: the body remembers what the mind is protecting itself from.

**Locked / hidden rooms**
- First: Ch.2, grandpa's self-built house full of secret rooms he pushed James to find. Evolution:
  the towers are hidden rooms writ large; the guild withholds; the class withholds. Final: Ch.26 —
  James realizes his whole childhood was training to find hidden rooms. Theme link: love that hides.

**The unearned mark (the beta tattoo)**
- First: Ch.4, the wolf's-head on his hand — power he was *given*. Evolution: every handed gift
  (class, title, car). Final: the tower reward he *earns* stands beside the mark he didn't.

## 6. VOICE DEFINITION (global narrator — third-person limited on James, past tense; canon)

**Vocabulary level:** 3/10 — plain, American, contemporary; system text is clinical/terse by
contrast (the ONE register shift the book uses deliberately).
**Sentence rhythm:** Mixed, leaning short. Action clips; wonder breathes; system screens are
bulleted/bracketed and flat.
**Formality:** Casual-conversational. Contractions everywhere. Profanity sparse and reactive.
**Humor presence:** Frequent but dry — deadpan understatement, James-vs-menu bickering, Dapple's
sass. Never quippy Marvel banter; the jokes are deflections, not performances.
**Emotional register:** Restrained by default; the grief is *withheld* and leaks sideways. Wonder
is allowed to be sincere (the reader came for the wish-fulfillment — don't ironize the good parts).
**Distinctive features:** (1) the deadpan man / delighted wolf duet; (2) system text as a genuine
second voice — flat, literal, occasionally the source of the joke; (3) grief that never announces
itself. **Progression clarity is a feature, not a chore — numbers and mechanics are rendered
*clean and legible*, never buried in purple prose.**
**Anti-patterns (this voice NEVER does):** never explains a feeling James is having ("he felt a
wave of grief"); never lets James be lyrical about his own competence; never has Dapple use idioms
a wolf couldn't know; never turns a stat-screen into a metaphor; never front-loads lore the scene
didn't ask for.

--- VOICE UNDER PRESSURE (mandatory) ---
When James is overwhelmed (grief, terror, the vortex, a near-death floor): **the prose goes flat
and literal — it retreats INTO the mechanical.** He counts things. He reads the system text
instead of feeling. Sentences shorten to fragments; the humor cuts out entirely; he narrates
objects (the pen, the boarded window, the number on the screen) instead of the emotion. The tell
that James is falling apart is that he gets *more precise and less human* — until one small sensory
detail (a smell, a warmth) breaks through and the fragment finally lands. **Dapple under pressure
inverts:** she gets louder, faster, more insulting, more manic — never quieter.

## 7. STYLISTIC DEVICE — SYSTEM INTERFACE TEXT

**Device:** the Cygnis system's screens/notifications (status blocks, level-ups, class descriptions,
bracketed combat log lines — all present in the drafted canon).
**Function:** a second, non-human narrator that can say things James can't — flat truths, ironic
timing, escalation the prose doesn't editorialize.
**Frequency:** every chapter that has progression; roughly every chapter, but *sparingly* — a screen
is a punctuation mark, not a paragraph.
**Three levels:**
- ILLUSTRATE (≤7.0): the screen just restates what happened ("[You killed the squirrel]" after we
  watched him kill it). Decorative.
- COMMENT (7.5–8.0): the screen's timing/wording adds irony (a level-up notification landing on the
  exact beat James decides he wants to go home).
- REVEAL (≥8.5): the screen tells James (and the reader) something the narrative withheld — a
  condition, a title, a rarity, a "conditions not met" that reframes the scene.
**Target:** REVEAL or COMMENT. A screen that only ILLUSTRATES gets cut or merged.
**Formatting rule (canon-matched):** status blocks in fenced code; inline notifications in
[brackets]; keep them terse and legible.

## 8. OPENING STRATEGY (Ch.1)

**Chosen: "The ordinary made strange" + a controlled slow burn** — NOT in medias res, NOT a voice
bomb. The drafted opening is a man on a greyhound going to bury his parents; the wrongness is that
everything is *too* arranged (the funeral pre-planned, the car waiting, the grandfather's "come here
and open the box"). We play it as quiet realism with one degree of *off* — the reader should feel
the world is holding its breath before they know why. **Why:** the isekai hook is stronger if Earth
feels real and grief-heavy first; dropping the reader mid-vortex would spend the emotional capital
the whole book runs on. The strangeness is in the arrangement, not in fireworks. (Ch.3, the isekai,
then earns its wonder because Ch.1–2 were grounded.)

## WRITER GUIDANCE — GLOBAL WARNINGS

- **Do NOT let James narrate his own grief.** It leaks through objects and smells, never through
  "he felt." See Voice Under Pressure.
- **Do NOT flatten Dapple into an exposition wolf.** Every lore dump she delivers must cost her
  something or be interrupted by her own chaos (scent tangents, wolf-logic, a memory).
- **Do NOT romance Wren.** Hard guardrail. Their chemistry is *sibling / foil* — they are each
  other's lesson. If a scene starts to tilt romantic, redirect to the shared-arc mirror.
- **Do NOT bury the numbers.** Progression readers came for legible mechanics. Render stats/levels/
  class math cleanly; the artistry is in *pacing the reveals*, not obscuring them.
- **Do NOT let competence erase cost.** Ancient class + Legend's Progeny = handed power; the
  secondary theme demands every handed advantage be *questioned* on-page, especially by James.
- **Watch for AI defaults:** competence cascade (James solving everything smoothly), analytical
  simile ("like a X, as if Y"), metacognitive narration ("he realized he was realizing"), emotional
  temperature reports. The style gate enforces simile ≤4/1k, em-dash ≤~10/1k.
- **Canon lock:** never contradict the drafted prose (names, stat block, class mechanics, the beta
  mark, Dapple's voice/sex, the rarity ladder, Seminova/Cygnis, the seven-tower/one-per-stat rule).
