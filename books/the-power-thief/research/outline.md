## Macro-Structure

**Chosen structure:** **Four-movement escalation (kishōtenketsu-influenced) braided with a
recovered-memory thread.** Not three-act, not 20×5k.

**(a) Structure:** Four named MOVEMENTS, not acts. Present-timeline escape/return braided with
short recovered-memory INTERLUDES (Panama, the Vault, the emptied person). The "ten" (twist)
of kishōtenketsu lands as the amnesia reveal in Movement III — a reframe, not a conflict-climax
— then Movement IV is reckoning, not a bolted-on bigger fight.

**(b) Why THIS premise demands it:**
- The power system is the plot, and the reader must LEARN it as Kevin RE-learns it — a braided
  present/memory weave is the natural delivery vehicle and kills the seed's infodump problem.
- The amnesia mystery wants withholding and reframe, not a rising-stakes staircase; kishōtenketsu's
  twist-not-climax shape fits "the reveal recontextualizes everything" better than a 3-act climax.
- Escalation is by **power-tier and moral cost**, not just threat size — movements escalate what
  Kevin must *pay*, ending on a sacrifice choice, which a four-movement reckoning structure serves.

**(c) Turning-point map (jittered, off the quarters):**
- Movement I → II hinge at **~19%** (sky reunion; the world opens)
- Movement II → III hinge at **~44%** (decision to go back down / entry to Vault 7)
- The TWIST (amnesia truth: he chose it / whom he emptied) at **~72%**
- Movement III → IV hinge at **~78%** (the reckoning begins)

**(d) Section labels:** MOVEMENT I–IV (visible as Part headers). Memory interludes are titled
"INTERLUDE:" not numbered as acts.

**(e) Chapter-count logic:** 25 chapters, lengths content-driven, 1,600–6,800w (longest ≈ 4.25×
shortest). Includes short punch chapters (4, 17, 22) and long immersive ones (3, 16). Count falls
out of beats, not division.

**Budget check:** Σ chapter targets = **109,200w ≥ manuscript_target_words (90,000)** ✓ (also
clears the 85k hard floor with wide margin; drafts running ~10% short still pass).

Word targets by movement: I = 20,600 · II = 35,400 · III = 31,400 · IV = 21,800.

# MOVEMENT I — THE ASCENT (the escape) [~19%]

## Chapter 1: Ten-Count (in medias res) — 5,200w
**Premise:** Kevin wakes strapped in the Vault, amnesiac, and must survive an escape using a
power he doesn't know he has.
**Function:** Hook; teach the reader ABSORB-by-contact through USE, not exposition; establish
amnesia and the Vault.
**Emotional anchor:** The slow ten-count of skin contact as Kevin instinctively drains a guard
and doesn't understand how he knew to.
**Emotional surprise:** He laughs — wrong, helpless — over the technician Pym's body.
**Opening strategy:** In medias res + the ordinary made strange (his own competence unnerves him).
**Beats:** wakes bandaged / Jake kills Pym with the tray and frees him / first accidental theft by
touch / the corridor starts to *move*.
**Progressive:** opens the whole book; plants: what did they do to me?
**Theme presence:** present — theft's first cost, unnamed.
**Character chaos:** the helpless laugh (Kevin).
**Subtext:** Jake's chatter is surface-reassurance; real conversation is *do you even know me?*
**Reading speed:** fast; decelerate only on the ten-count.
**Beat subversion:** genre expects the amnesiac hero to have a cool signature power revealed with
a flourish; instead his power is a confusing, intimate *theft* he can't control and feels sick about.
**Writer warnings:** NO mythology. NO sky-lecture. Resist competence cascade — he's clumsy.

## Chapter 2: The Building That Fights Back — 3,200w
**Premise:** The shifting hallways (Wren's work) turn escape into a race against architecture.
**Function:** Introduce Manipulation as *experienced phenomenon* (Law effects visible, unexplained).
**Emotional anchor:** A door that wasn't there sealing behind them with a sound like a swallow.
**Emotional surprise:** Stillness — a corridor freezes mid-reform, and the quiet is worse.
**Opening strategy:** bridge — hard cut, motion continues.
**Beats:** maze reforms / guards appear in new halls / they glimpse Wren bleeding as she works
(Recoil, unexplained) / dead end.
**Progressive:** builds on ch1's motion; adds: the walls are *someone's body*.
**Theme:** RECEDES — pure survival/spectacle (one of the mandated breathing beats).
**Subtext:** —
**Beat subversion:** the "chase through collapsing base" usually rewards speed; here speed is
useless — the building is faster — so it rewards *outlasting* (Law: persistence decay), taught
by consequence.
**Writer warnings:** keep Wren a glimpse; don't explain the Law yet.

## Chapter 3: The Only Way Out Is Up — 6,000w (long immersive)
**Premise:** The yard; the only exit is flight — which Jake has and Kevin doesn't — forcing the
first RECALL.
**Function:** Deliver the seed's centerpiece AND secretly teach gift/recall by consequence.
**Emotional anchor:** Jake dropping out of the sky the instant Kevin recalls flight from him.
**Emotional surprise:** Kevin's first flight is not triumph but nausea and a crater of hurt guards.
**Opening strategy:** bridge — burst into open air.
**Beats:** sirens, riflemen flood the yard / Jake flies, tells Kevin to fly / Kevin "hops like an
idiot," cornered / mortal-pressure trigger fires RECALL / detonation, Jake falls, Kevin catches
him or Jake hits hard / Kevin wakes floating in the stratosphere.
**Progressive:** pays off ch1–2; opens Movement II.
**Theme:** present — the gift/recall as self-sacrifice-in-reverse; the first betrayal-shaped act.
**Character chaos:** Jake ranks "worst ways to die" mid-crisis.
**Shareable moment:** the man who can't fly recalls flight from his best friend, dropping him, to
save them both — power as theft even between allies.
**Reading speed:** accelerate to the detonation; hard decelerate into the silent stratosphere.
**Beat subversion:** the "hero unlocks his power in the nick of time" beat is inverted — unlocking
it *costs Jake*, so the triumph is contaminated.
**Writer warnings:** the recall must read as taking-from-Jake, not gaining. No exposition on why.

## Chapter 4: Cold at the Top of the World — 2,000w (short punch)
**Premise:** Two men floating in the stratosphere, alive, with nothing explained.
**Function:** Breath; first real Kevin/Jake intimacy; withhold mythology deliberately.
**Emotional anchor:** Earth curved beneath them; Jake checking the watch out of pure habit.
**Emotional surprise:** Jake won't answer the big question — deflects into a fishing memory.
**Opening strategy:** stillness after detonation.
**Beats:** they breathe (unexplained) / Kevin asks who he is / Jake starts, then can't.
**Progressive:** opens the mystery engine of Movement II.
**Theme:** present — what is a man with no memory owed?
**Character chaos:** Jake's fish-weight memory at a solemn beat.
**Re-read reward:** Jake checking the watch = rationing his own existence (invisible now).
**Beat subversion:** the post-escape "info download" (seed's sky-lecture) is REFUSED — Jake
deflects; the reader gets a person, not a lecture.

## Chapter 5: Down to the Surface — 4,200w
**Premise:** They descend to a safehouse; Kevin gets the first fragments and the first rules.
**Function:** Establish present-day stakes; introduce the decay economy through Jake's watch.
**Emotional anchor:** The watch ticking Jake's flight down toward zero on a kitchen table.
**Emotional surprise:** Kevin is relieved he can't remember — and hates that he's relieved.
**Opening strategy:** descent bridge.
**Beats:** safehouse / Jake explains only what's *useful* (his flight is Kevin's gift; it decays;
the watch counts it) / Meridian is hunting them / Kevin resolves, quietly, to go back down.
**Progressive:** closes Movement I; the goal (return to Vault 7) is set.
**Theme:** present — Kevin's sunk-cost self-accounting (§4 distortion) first voiced.
**Character chaos:** Kevin counts the safehouse stairs; there are an odd number; it bothers him.
**Subtext:** surface = logistics; real = Jake terrified of the day the watch hits zero.
**Beat subversion:** the mentor-figure "here are your powers, here's the plan" download is broken
into need-to-know scraps; the rules arrive as a *budget*, not a lecture.

# MOVEMENT II — THE SURFACE (relearning the rules) [~44%]

## Chapter 6: The Rules Have Teeth — 5,500w
**Premise:** A street confrontation forces Kevin to learn the multi-hold ceiling — and the Carry
— the hard way.
**Function:** Teach §1.6 ex.2 (the three-power ceiling) and §1.4 (the Carry) by consequence.
**Emotional anchor:** Kevin, holding a brawler's power, catching his own hands coming up wanting
the hit — an appetite that isn't his.
**Emotional surprise:** the borrowed instinct feels GOOD, and that's what scares him.
**Beats:** a powered mugging/ambush / Kevin takes a power and a stranger's reflexes ride in with

mean to / learns the ceiling / Jake covers.
**Progressive:** builds ch5's rules into lived cost; puts the Carry on-page.
**Theme:** present — what you take takes root in you.
**Character chaos:** Kevin's yellow-kitchen memory (his OWN, returning) intrudes mid-fight —
distinct from the borrowed passengers, first appearance.
**Reading speed:** fast fight; decelerate on the moment the appetite feels good.
**Beat subversion:** the "power-up montage" of progression fiction is inverted — every gain is a
subtraction; getting stronger makes him *less himself*, literally crowded by other selves.
**Writer warnings:** no stat-talk; the Carry is felt as impulse/appetite, never narrated as a meter.

## INTERLUDE: Panama, Six Years Ago — 3,000w (Ch.7)

## Chapter 8: What Meridian Wants — 6,500w

**Theme:** present — power as debt, embodied in Nadia.

## INTERLUDE: The Yellow Kitchen — 2,200w (Ch.9, short)

## Chapter 10: Placement — 4,800w
**Premise:** The Curator makes contact — not to fight, but to recruit — and shows Kevin his own
power worn without conscience.
**Function:** Introduce the MIRROR antagonist; externalize Kevin's "am I just a thief?" in someone
who does exactly what he does and calls it stewardship.
**Emotional anchor:** the Curator emptying a donor mid-conversation, gently, and thanking them —
the same ten-count Kevin uses, with a smile.
**Emotional surprise:** Kevin recognizes the technique in his own hands; the horror is kinship,
not difference.
**Beats:** the Curator arrives under flag of truce / demonstrates absorb-without-gift / her pitch:
"we're the same animal, I just don't lie about the cost" / she lets slip she *holds something of
his* (the loved one's power) / Kevin refuses, shaken.
**Progressive:** complicates the "Kevin = good guy" read; loads the personal stake (she has what
he took); escalates.
**Theme:** present — take vs give, made a person.
**Character chaos:** Jake mind-reads Kevin wrong and acts on it, worsening things.
**Subtext:** surface = a job offer; real = Kevin staring at what he could become.
**Shareable moment:** the villain isn't Kevin's opposite — she's Kevin without the part that hurts
to give, and she thinks that makes her honest.
**Beat subversion:** the "rival with the same power" beat usually means a duel; here she doesn't
fight — she *recruits*, and the threat is that her logic is coherent.

## Chapter 11: The Leash — 3,400w

## Chapter 12: The Door Down — 6,000w

**Emotional surprise:** the plan hinges on *keeping Nadia alive* — Kevin carrying her power and

**Beats:** intel on Vault 7 / choose the loadout (§1.6 ex.2) / Nadia's bargain / Jake's fear.

**Theme:** present.

**Beat subversion:** the "assemble the crew / gear up" montage is inverted into a series of

## Chapter 13: Threshold — 4,000w

## Chapter 16: Marrow — 6,800w (long immersive)
**Premise:** Kevin faces Marrow, who trades him the CONTENT of his past — what he did, and why he
was in the Vault — for cooperation.
**Function:** Antagonist centerpiece; the kishōtenketsu TWIST detonates here (~72%). The reveal is
about *what happened*, not the mechanism of forgetting (that was a blow to the head).
**Emotional anchor:** Marrow naming the woman in the yellow kitchen — and Kevin realizing he came
down into this place *on purpose*, for her.
**Emotional surprise:** the reveal reframes Kevin from victim to agent — he emptied someone he
loved (it broke her; the power passed to the Curator), and then he walked into Vault 7 himself,
hunting a way to take it back. Meridian caught him and beat the memory out of him; the amnesia is
just injury. He is not a blank innocent — he is the man who did the unforgivable thing and came to
undo it.
**Beats:** capture/parley / Marrow's mercy-framed account / the content reveal (the emptying; the
Curator holds the power; Kevin came here of his own will) / Kevin's world reframes.
**Progressive:** the twist — recontextualizes Movements I–II (his competence, his guilt, his laugh).
**Theme:** present — the book's question made unavoidable: is he the sum of what he took?
**Character chaos:** the helpless laugh returns, and now the reader understands it.
**Shareable moment:** the hero discovers the crime he's been running from is his own — he emptied
the person he loved, and walked into hell on purpose to try to take it back.
**Reading speed:** decelerate hard through the reveal.
**Subtext:** Marrow "offering answers"; real = Marrow recruiting him with guilt — *the emptying can
be made to mean something if you help us.*
**Beat subversion:** the "villain reveals hero's hidden past to break him" beat flips — the truth
doesn't break Kevin because part of him already knew he came here for a reason; the horror is that
Marrow can make the worst thing Kevin did sound like the down payment on a cure.
**Writer warnings:** deliver via artifacts/exchange, not a monologue; NO reference to memory-as-fuel.
