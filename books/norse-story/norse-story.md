The forest surrounding The Church of Edda, a community in the appalachian mountains that still worshipped the old gods, was full of activity on the day before the summer solstice. Johnathan Marks, the leader of this community, had been overseeing the preparations for the celebration that would be held at midnight since four that morning. Every five years all of the surrounding communities would gather together for one big celebration and it was their turn to host it. An outsider who had heard of their community had wanted to build a representation of yggdrasil using nets and webbing between two trees. Johnathan didn't know how he had heard of The Church of Edda, and the young man wouldn't say, but the photos of past projects he had completed impressed Johnathan. So, despite slight misgivings, he had agreed. Now that John was standing in front of the finished product, he couldn't help but be thankful he had agreed. The structure was eighteen feet tall and had silvered rope and gold webbing. It was awe inspiring how someone could create something like this. The only instruction that the young man had given him before getting into his car and driving off was to set it aflame at the stroke of midnight. John had looked in confusion at the man's car, noting the thor's hammer and the ancient symbol of the Valkyries on the cars back window, before shrugging and going back to his work. If he wanted John to burn it he would, but it was still a shame that such fine work would literally go up in flames. A bonfire was lit before the tree and groups of people were scattered around it talking. Not many people traveled between the communities, unless it was the six month trading trips that some went on, so most people were using this time to catch up with friends. The gathering would last for six more days, plenty of time to get all of the news. Jessica Smith, his wife of the past three years was chatting with maude clemmons from the Northguard Temple community, but seeing John standing alone she comes over to him. "Everything okay?" she asks wrapping a

arm around his waist. John puts an arm around her shoulders and gives her a light squeeze. "yeah everything's fine. You know how I get at these things." Jessica laughs and nods, "you think too much. It's always like you know too much but at the same time you never know enough." she then looks up at the tree, "reminds me of the all father. With all of his wisdom there is still things he could not do." John strokes Jessica's flaming red hair, "If only I knew that much," he said laughing as well, "Come on, let's get some food before the ceremony." So with three hours to go John and Jessica met with the four other communities leaders while eating from carved wooden bowls.
