# Voice DNA — Shadow God (Book 1)

> Executable, enforceable voice spec. Extracted from the author's Draft A/B prose

## What the author's own prose gives us (the benchmark seed)
From Draft A/B: **close third on Theran; plain, concrete, street-level diction; hunger as a lens ("his stomach rumbled and he was reminded that he had not eaten today"); short knife, purse-strings, alehouses — tactile poverty detail; a boy soothed by shadow ("almost as if they were a warm embrace"); functional, earnest, slightly run-on syntax.** The revision keeps the earnestness and concreteness, tightens the run-ons, and sharpens the interiority into *counting/measuring*. Preserve the plainness; do not upgrade Theran into a slick, ironic heist-narrator.

## 1. Global Narrative Voice

- **POV/distance:** Close third, single POV (Theran) throughout — including Corvane scenes, seen *from Theran* except where the outline marks intercut (Ch.8 parallel keeps Corvane external, behavior-only, never his interiority).
- **Vocabulary level:** 4/10. Plain Anglo-Saxon, street register. Reach for a richer word only inside the Shadow Domain (Ch.3, 14, 15) and even there, sparingly — weight/warmth/silence, not ornament.
- **Sentence rhythm:** Mixed, leaning short. Action clips into simple declaratives; interiority runs a little longer but never baroque. Fragments allowed under stress.
- **Formality:** Casual→conversational narration. Corvane's dialogue and the Assay's paperwork go Measured/Formal for contrast.
- **Humor:** Dry, subtle — Theran's deflection, Jeren's warmth, Bevin's pricing. NEVER quippy/Whedon/modern-banter.
- **Emotional register:** Restrained. Name the body, not the feeling. Open raw only at chosen anchors (Ch.9, 15, 18).
- **Distinctive features (write these IN):**
  - **Counting/measuring interiority** — Theran inventories steps, coins, exits, knots, distances; his mind quantifies as a coping reflex.
  - **Shadow by warmth/weight, not just darkness** — the dark is described as temperature and pressure ("the dark sat warm on his neck"), seeding the cost-economy.
  - **Hunger as lens** — food, warmth, and lack recur as the measure of everything (carried straight from the author's draft).
  - **Naming the exit** — Theran clocks the way out of every room; safety = reachable-elsewhere.
- **Anti-patterns (the voice NEVER does):** analytical/explanatory simile chains; metacognition ("he realized he was realizing"); emotional-temperature reports ("a wave of grief washed over him"); adverb-laden dialogue tags; modern quips; Weeks-coined caste vocabulary (no "wetboy," no guild-fist argot); lore-dump paragraphs.

### Voice under pressure (mandatory — samples required)
When Theran is overwhelmed: prose **goes flat and starts counting** — sentences shorten and numb, he inventories concrete things instead of feeling; **the wrong laugh leaks in**; **present tense occasionally cuts into the past-tense narration** for a beat, then recedes. He does NOT get more lyrical under stress — he gets more mechanical. Growth marker: by Ch.18 the wrong-laugh is *absent* where it once would have leaked.
Corvane under pressure inverts: **more courteous, more grammatical, quieter** — never louder.

## 2. Per-Character Voice Cards

#### Theran (POV)
- **Vocabulary band:** street orphan; plain, concrete, no schooling-words; magic described in physical terms he improvised himself.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** short declaratives; clips under stress; interiority quantifies (numbers, distances, counts).
- **Rhythm:** statements over questions; when he asks a question it's rhetorical and dry.
- **Verbal tics:** counts things; names exits; describes his own fear as a body-fact ("his hands wanted something to tie").
- **Never says:** his own feelings by name ("I'm scared/sad/angry"); anything sentimental aloud; thanks accepted gracefully.
- **Sample line:** "Three steps to the door, two to the window, and a drunk between him and both. He could work with a drunk."

#### Jeren
- **Vocabulary band:** street, warmer and louder; slang-rich; nicknames everything.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** run-on, comma-spliced, talks over silence; uses names constantly.
- **Rhythm:** questions and needling; fills every pause.
- **Verbal tics:** says "Theran" more than anyone; the invented baker-feud; never says "goodbye."
- **Never says:** anything that admits he's afraid of being left; the word "goodbye."
- **Sample line:** "Theran. Theran, look at me. No — that baker can rot, I'll get to him — Theran, are you *bleeding*?"

#### Wren
- **Vocabulary band:** market-mystic; half-scholarly (fading-line remnants) undercut by con-patter.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** half-finished proverbs she interrupts to mock; precise about the unseen, vague on purpose about herself.
- **Rhythm:** weary, ironic, trailing off.
- **Verbal tics:** starts a saying, undercuts it ("Blood remembers — or so the coin-takers say, gods rot them"); catalogues omens she calls nonsense.
- **Never says:** a straight expression of hope without immediately shaming it.
- **Sample line:** "The gods hear the faithful. That's the story. I was faithful. Look how well I hear."

#### Corvane (antagonist)
- **Vocabulary band:** educated bureaucrat; guild-Latinate; euphemism ("unregistered line," "reclamation," "regrettable").
- **Syntax fingerprint:** full, balanced, subordinated clauses; grammatically immaculate even mid-threat; apologizes while harming.
- **Rhythm:** unhurried; never raises volume; questions posed as courtesies.
- **Verbal tics:** corrects others' grammar/punctuation; speaks of children as records; "you understand the arithmetic."
- **Never says:** a raised-voice threat; a slur; anything cruel in tone (the cruelty is in the content).
- **Sample line:** "I'm sorry — 'quirk' is the street word. In the ledger he was a line. An unregistered line. We closed it. I did note his name."

#### Bevin
- **Vocabulary band:** fence's cant; everything has a price.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** short, transactional, gruff-affectionate.
- **Verbal tics:** prices feelings and events in coppers; won't discuss the color blue.
- **Never says:** an open endearment (he prices them instead).
- **Sample line:** "That guilt of yours is worth about two coppers. Put it down and eat."

#### Ferrick (High Lord, minor)
- **Vocabulary band:** noble, evasive.
- **Syntax fingerprint:** passive voice, agentless ("it was decided," "measures were taken").
- **Never says:** "I ordered."
- **Sample line:** "The curfew was thought necessary. By whom, I couldn't rightly say."

## 3. Voice Differentiation Matrix (cover-the-name test, pre-solved)

| Character | Sharpest distinguisher | Contrast vs nearest |
|---|---|---|
| Theran | quantifies everything; never names a feeling | vs Jeren: Jeren over-names *people*, Theran counts *things* |
| Jeren | compulsive name-saying, run-on, never "goodbye" | vs Bevin: Jeren spends words freely, Bevin prices them |
| Wren | self-undercutting proverbs | vs Corvane: Wren mocks her own certainty, Corvane is serenely certain |
| Corvane | grammatically immaculate euphemism, apologetic harm | vs Ferrick: Corvane owns his acts precisely; Ferrick disowns via passive voice |
| Bevin | prices feelings in coppers | vs Theran: both terse, but Bevin transacts, Theran inventories |
| Ferrick | agentless passive constructions | vs Corvane: opposite ends of "owning what you did" |

If any two ever converge on their sharpest marker in a scene, differentiate further before finishing the scene.

## 4. Anti-Pattern Budget (per 1,000 words — dark fantasy calibration)
- **Explanatory/analytical similes (Pattern #11, the #1 AI tell):** ≤ **4/1k** (matches style_check gate). Domain chapters trend LOWER.
- **Adverbs in dialogue tags:** near-zero (≤1 per chapter, and only if load-bearing).
- **"almost as if / as though" constructions:** ≤ **1 per chapter** — and the Ch.1 "almost as if they were a warm embrace" is the ALLOWLISTED motif; count it, don't multiply it.
- **Metacognitive narration ("he realized he was realizing"):** **0** ceiling.
- **Emotional-temperature reports ("a wave of X washed over"):** **0** ceiling — replace with body-fact.
- **Em-dashes:** ≤ ~10/1k (style_check gate).
- **Modern/quippy banter beats:** 0.
This is the ceiling the Writer aims UNDER and the Disruptor cuts down to. Add this book's deliberate motifs (warm-dark, knots, ledgers, seals) to `tools/style_check.py` ALLOWLIST so cross-chapter recurrence isn't flagged as a repeated-phrase tic.
