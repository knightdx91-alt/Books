## Chapter 1 (draft)

The darkness of the alleyway hid Azolth's small body as he hid in the shadows. He had been
waiting for an hour, hoping a mark would stroll by. After the beating he had received yesterday
when he had gotten caught cutting the purse of a high lord's servant, Azolth had decided to
start only doing his business at night. At ten years old Azolth was one of the younger street
urchins, even younger than his friend Jeren, but he was light on his feet. Azolth had always
felt more comfortable in the shadows. It was almost as if they were a warm embrace.

Azolth's stomach rumbled and he was reminded that he had not eaten today. He hoped that Jeren
had had more luck than Azolth was having. Azolth was about to give up and try another alley when
he heard the shuffling and stumbling sounds of a drunk man on his way home from the Alehouse.
Azolth peered cautiously from the alley to confirm what he heard. Sure enough a young man with
fine clothes was stumbling down the street with a dark bottle in his hand. Azolth looked both
ways to make sure that no one else was on the street. Seeing that they were alone, Azolth waited
patiently for him to pass, yet he couldn't help the flutter of excitement that blossomed in him.
With those fine clothes, unless he spent all his coin in the ale house, his purse should hold
enough to buy him and Jeren food for a few days.

The young man passed Azolth's hiding place and Azolth slipped quietly behind him, matching him
step for step so that they were in sync. Azolth palmed his short knife in his right hand and
glided forward until he was only a pace from the drunk's back. The sharp smell of spirits made
Azolth's nose burn as he got closer. Azolth could see a small bag hanging from the belt in front
of him, and he assumed it to be the money bag. "It had better be," he thought, "I can't see any
other." With a long practised ease he leans forward on a step and deftly cuts the purse strings,
catching it immediately so that it would make no noise. Azolth stood there, barely breathing, as
the drunk moved farther away. Once he was out of sight, Azolth let out a sigh of relief.

Azolth looked around once more to check he was alone, then took off running. In general, there
were not supposed to be anyone about after dark, as the local High Lord had imposed a curfew.
There were some burglaries reported and they had all happened at night. If you were caught you'd
go to jail, if you were lucky you wouldn't get beaten first. Street urchins were never lucky.
Azolth was one of the very few that dared to ply their trade after dark. Last week, Mouse had
gotten caught sneaking into a bakers window looking for stale bread. No one had seen him since.

## Draft B — alternate/second opening (name: THERAN). Scouting the Singing Siren.

> A different opening beat than Draft A, under the new name Theran. Establishes the artifact
> that likely triggers the plot (awakening his shadow bloodline). Author to decide which
> opening leads, or whether they combine.

Theran was watching as the shopkeeper closed and locked his door. The Singing Siren was the
most popular shop that sold enchanted items to the High lords and ladies. It wasn't just to
them but they were mainly the only ones with enough money to purchase those artifacts. Theran
had been doing his usual scouting, which mainly consisted of listening to street rumours, when
he heard something interesting. There had been news of an ancient artifact being found on the
Isle of Scera and it had just been brought to the Singing Siren today. The artifact, which the
finders had called a symbol of resonance, was supposed to be able to awaken a bloodline in you.
Most of the rumours said it was rubbish, as the artificers were the only known bloodline and
they keep a very tight rein on their birthing records. Yet even so, Theran knew he had to check
it out. Ever since he was little, Theran knew he was different. He had an ability to hide in
darkness where no one could find him, plus he could move almost completely silent in the dark
of night. It helped him evade the guards that patrolled the city at night. He hoped by stealing
this he would be able to do even more.

## What the opening establishes (for the architect)

- **Azolth** — POV. A 10-year-old street urchin/pickpocket. Younger than most urchins, light on
  his feet, skilled with a short knife and purse-cutting. **Feels more comfortable in shadows —
  "almost as if they were a warm embrace."** (The clear seed of his latent power → the title
  *Shadow God*.) Hungry, loyal (steals to feed himself AND Jeren).
- **Jeren** — Azolth's friend, a fellow (older) street urchin. Off-page in the opening.
- **Mouse** — another urchin; caught last week sneaking into a baker's window, vanished. Sets the
  stakes: getting caught means jail, a beating, or worse — disappearance.
- **Setting** — a low-fantasy medieval city ruled by a **High Lord** who has imposed a night
  curfew after a string of burglaries. Rigid class order ("high lords," their servants, vs. the
  urchin underclass). Alehouses, alleyways, bakers.
- **Tone** — gritty, grounded street-level fantasy; sympathetic child-thief; tension of a night
  score under curfew.

## Open questions the draft leaves (architect / author)
- **The shadow power** — Azolth's affinity for shadows is the engine. What is it, exactly? How
  does it awaken (this score gone wrong? Mouse's fate connected?)? "Shadow God" implies a huge
  arc — from urchin to something divine. What are the rules/costs?
- Who/what disappeared **Mouse** — mundane authority, or something supernatural tied to the shadows?
- The **High Lord** and the curfew — is the antagonist political, supernatural, or both?
- Genre lane/scope — grimdark? YA-ish progression fantasy? Series or standalone? Age of the
  narration (starts at 10 — does the book age him up)?
- What does Azolth WANT beyond survival, and what's the wound driving him?
