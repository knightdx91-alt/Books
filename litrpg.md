The silky hills outside of the greyhound bus window was lulling James to sleep. He had been riding the bus for eighteen hours, heading to his grandparents old house to attend the funeral of his parents. As their only child he had no one to lean on but himself. James had been at work a few days prior when he had gotten the call. The sheriff of marks county Virginia was a friend of the family, so him calling wasn't anything unusual but James could tell when he answered the phone that something was wrong. "Hello?" James said picking up his call and hitting the answer button. "Hey James," Alex mathers said hesitantly, "It's been awhile." The hesitance in Alex's voice put James on guard. "Yeah it has. Everything all right buddy?" Alex sighed, the sound coming through audibly, "James, I hate to have to be the one to tell you this. Your parents, they uh. They were in a car accident. It looks like they were forced off the road." James gripped the phone tighter, his knuckles turning white, "Are they okay? What's happened?" "I'm sorry man, they passed away at the scene." James sat down heavily in his chair not believing, or not wanting to, that they were gone. Hell, he had just seen them last weekend! Mind numb James listened to Alex explain that he didn't need to identify the bodies as everyone knew who they were, and his parents had already had funeral arrangements in writing with their lawyer, so all he needed to do was show up. James thanked him for calling and told him that he'd be there. Ever since his grandparents had passed a few years ago, he'd been uneasy. Almost as if he felt there was more coming. Now, that feeling was gone. The sight of the sign announcing their arrival into marks county pulled James from his thoughts. It wasn't long before they pulled up beside a small building. It looked run down and one of the windows was boarded up.

<!-- pg 2 -->
This small bus station outpost probably wasn't used much anymore by the looks of it, even some of the bricks looked to be crumbling. James stepped off the bus into the bright sunlight, squinting briefly before pulling his sunglasses off his shirt and putting them on. James looked around briefly until he saw his grandfathers chevy nova parked off to the side. Slinging his duffel bag over his shoulder, James made his way over to the car. James could easily have driven here, instead of taking the greyhound, but he decided to ride the greyhound as when he was younger he rode greyhounds often with his parents just going to random towns and exploring. They would just take off for a week, then come back and resume their regular lives. The nova was as clean as normal when James slipped behind the wheel. When James had told mark how he was going to make his way there, Mark had assured him he would have a car waiting for him. James had thought Mark would have gotten him a rental but mark knew how much this car meant to James, so it really shouldn't have been too big of a surprise. The house was dark when James pulled into the driveway but once the motion sensors picked him up, the lawn was bathed in light. James had kept the lights turned on and the water bill paid even though he had not been here in months. He had been surprised when grandpa richards had left him the house instead of leaving it to his dad, but in a way it had made sense. His dad and his grandpa had had a falling out over something when his dad was a teenager and they had never gotten over it. One of the last things his grandpa had told him was that if anything ever happened to his dad he should come here. When he asked why his grandpa had pulled a box from a hidden compartment built in his cherry wood desk. It was a medium size box with a gold inlaid symbol that James didn't recognize. His grandpa then

handed it to him and told him to open it. James had tried but the lid wouldn't budge. There was no lock and it looked like it should have opened with no problem, but no matter what he did he couldn't get it to open. His grandpa laughed at the look on his face, "It's okay, I knew you couldn't open it. I just wanted you to know that no one else can either. When the time comes that your father and I are no longer alive, come here and open this box. You'll understand then." Since that day, every time he talked to his grandpa, it seemed like his grandpa thought he was living on borrowed time. Now that both his grandfather and his father were gone, he knew he had to do what his grandfather had asked.

The inside of the house was a little dusty, as James never hired help to clean the place. Normally if he stayed here he spent a few days cleaning it from top to bottom. After the funeral that's probably the first thing he would tackle. James dropped his duffel bag by the door and headed into the office. The office hadn't changed much from when his grandfather was alive. The artifacts that James had tried to identify over the years still occupied the shelves along with the large sword that hung behind the desk. The sword had a dark black blade with a well worn handle, the leather stained with sweat. James had asked his grandfather about the sword before, only to be told he would learn about it one day. James had just shrugged, chalking it up as another mysterious thing about his family. James sat in the large leather wing backed chair and laid his hands on the top of the desk. There were so many memories not only in this room but the whole house. He had enjoyed exploring the large house, always on the hunt for hidden rooms or secrets. His grandfather had been the one that built the

house with his own hands and he freely admitted to putting secret rooms in the house, pushing james to find them. He had found a few over the years but he always felt there was always more to discover. Now sitting at this desk with the remnants of the past around him, he opened up the hidden compartment in the desk, withdrawing the box he had not seen in years. The box looked as if it had just been polished, the light shining brightly off of it's surface. James sat there for a moment staring at the box in his hands. Rubbing the sides lightly with his thumbs, he slowly lifts the lid. Nestled on top of soft purple satin lay a large card with symbols that James had never seen. Each symbol seemed to be painted on with gold, even the large symbol in the middle looked luminescent. As he stared at the large symbol, it began to move, transforming into a swirling mass. James tried to move, to drop the card, but it was like his muscles had locked up. He couldn't tear his eyes away from the swirling vortex that seemed to be drawing him in. Then suddenly he blacked out.

James woke to a pounding headache and something wet licking his face. He flailed about hitting something soft which made what he hit emit a yelp. James opened his eyes, seeing a clear blue sky above him. He was laying on soft green grass and sitting beside him was what looked like a grey wolf cub, who was currently looking at him with sad eyes. He was currently in the middle of a field, with a sparse forest off to his right and a town he could just barely make out in the distance in front of him. "What the hell happened?" James says aloud, "Where am I?" The wolf cub cocked it's head looking at him curiously. Suddenly, a screen appeared before his eyes. "Iron hide wolf cub (ancient) has requested a bond. Accept?" James looked at the cub in shock. "That's you?"

James asks. The wolf cub chuffs in response. James shakes his head "I accept." "Finally!" a young excited female voice says into his mind. "I didn't think you would ever wake up!" The wolf cub jumped around excitedly. James scooted back in shock, "you can talk?!" The cub rolls her eyes, "Duh, all enlightened animals can. Can wolves not talk where you are from?" she asks. James shakes his head, "No. You know I'm not from here?" The wolf nods, "yep, you smell different. Strong. plus, I saw you appear here." James looks around, "Where exactly is here?" The cub, who now introduced herself as dapple, laid down looking at him. "you're in the cygnis system, which is part of the collective worlds. Seeing that he was still confused dapple cocked her head, "you have heard of the system right?" James frowned, "The system?" Now it was dapples turn to look shocked, which was an odd look for a wolf, mouth open and tongue hanging out. "The system is everything," dapple says incredulous that his knowledge was lacking, "It's how we evolve and get stronger." James shrugs, "I've never heard of it. Maybe I don't have it." Dapple eyed him skeptically, "uh huh. say or think the word status." To humor the wolf, James says "status" fully expecting nothing to happen. Instead a new screen popped up.
